\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The H-LAYER consolidation reduced the remaining H-LAYER item to the completeness of
the admissible competitor class $\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$. v1.0 examined three exclusion
boundaries and concluded the sign-off reduces to ``crystalline order''. The operator
adversarial review found a LATTICE-but-OFF-SHELL escape that v1.0 did not handle. This
re-issue corrects the $\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$ definition, adds the missing boundary, and
records the universal fallback under which the analytic thresholds survive. Scope:
operating endpoint $I=2\times10^{-3}$.

\section*{2. The corrected $\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$ definition}

\[
\mathcal A_{\rm adm}=\left\{Q\subset\bigcup_{R\in\mathcal R}\!\bigl(\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}\bigr):
\begin{array}{l}
|Q|\le n_{\rm pack},\quad Q=-Q,\\
\angle(k_i,k_j)\ge\theta_{\min}(\hat r),\\
Q\text{ a registered crystallographic shell or shell-union competitor}
\end{array}\right\}.
\]
The pin $T'(Q)\le13$ is asserted ONLY on this registered shell / shell-union competitor
class. For arbitrary antipodal lattice subsets the fallback is only $T'(Q)\le N/2\le20$.

\section*{3. The four exclusion boundaries}

\textbf{Boundary 1 -- sub-$\theta_{\min}$ (DERIVED).} The coherence-indistinguishability
lemma bounds sub-resolution restructuring, $|F[P']-F[P]|\le c_{\rm ind}I^2=
\mathrm{MARGIN}/33$ at the endpoint ($c_{\rm ind}\approx30$--$32$, $\theta_{\min}=0.627$);
sub-$\theta_{\min}$ modes are absorbed into the Gaussian sea.

\textbf{Boundary 2 -- super-$n_{\rm pack}$ (DERIVED).} The spherical packing bound gives
$\le16/\theta_{\min}^2=n_{\rm pack}=40.7$ separated modes; $>n_{\rm pack}$ forces a pair
below $\theta_{\min}$ (pigeonhole), reducing to Boundary 1.

\textbf{Boundary 3 -- lattice but off-shell / arbitrary multi-radius subsets
(FALLBACK-CONTROLLED).} The direct adversarial construction shows the sharp pin
$T'(Q)\le13$ is NOT a theorem over arbitrary antipodal lattice subsets: there exist
antipodal lattice sets with
\[
N=40,\qquad T'(Q)=20>13.
\]
These are not non-lattice; they are lattice-supported but not registered single-shell or
shell-union crystallographic competitors. However, any real antipodal competitor obeys
the universal geometric fallback
\[
T'(Q)\le\frac{N}{2}
\]
(the sum-circles $C_t$ and $C_{-t}$ are disjoint for $t\ne0$, and antipodal symmetry maps
$Q\cap C_t$ onto $Q\cap C_{-t}$, so $2T'\le N$). With real antipodal symmetry and
$N\le n_{\rm pack}=40.7$, hence $N\le40$,
\[
T'(Q)\le20,\qquad 20<20.5<26.2<60.4 .
\]
Thus arbitrary antipodal escape competitors can FALSIFY the sharp $T'\le13$ pin, but they
do NOT falsify the H-diag (RES-1), RES-5, or SC-SCOPE thresholds, provided the packing
bound $N\le40$ and real antipodal symmetry are retained. The cost is thinness: at $T'=20$
the binding RES-1 ratio is $R_{\rm lead}(20)=0.974$ ($\times1.026$), still $<1$; the
SC-SCOPE joint is $\times1.082$, RES-5 floor $\times1.31$. \emph{Whether such off-shell
escape competitors are physically admissible in B1/B2 is the remaining operator question.}

\textbf{Boundary 4 -- non-lattice / incommensurate (MODELLING, with fallback).} A
non-crystalline condensate is a different ansatz: the crystalline-order restriction is the
genuine modelling input, softened by DR-2 decoupling $T'\ll_\varepsilon N^\varepsilon$
(R-022 T6-conditional / R-023 T4), off the critical path.

\section*{4. Verdict}

Of the FOUR boundaries: 1 and 2 are physically DERIVED; 3 is FALLBACK-CONTROLLED
($T'\le N/2\le20$, thresholds survive thinly); 4 is a MODELLING input. The operator
completeness sign-off therefore reduces to ``the ground state is crystalline AND
shell-supported'' (a registered shell / shell-union competitor), NOT merely crystalline.
No tier flip: B1/B2 T6 on $\{$H-LAYER$\}$, B5 T5.
