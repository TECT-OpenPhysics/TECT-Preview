\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The H-LAYER analytic closure (T-016 to T-021) holds on the registered admissible class;
the adversarial review (T-023 v1.1) showed the sharp pin $T'\le13$ is class-specific and
exhibited a lattice-but-off-shell escape ($N=40$, $T'=20$). This note records the operator
decision on whether such off-shell competitors are physically admissible, with its physical
justification. Scope: the Reading-H selection at the operating endpoint $I=2\times10^{-3}$.

\section*{2. The decision}

\textbf{Off-shell / arbitrary-multi-radius antipodal lattice subsets are NOT included in
the primary admissible class.} The Reading-H comparison is a shell-selected Brazovskii
comparison: physically relevant Bragg competitors live on the selected soft crystallographic
shell or on registered shell-union degeneracies. The primary class is
\[
\mathcal A_{\rm adm}=\bigl\{\text{registered crystalline shell / shell-union competitors}\bigr\},
\qquad T'(Q)\le13 .
\]
To make the proof adversarially safe, an extended fallback class is also retained:
\[
\mathcal A_{\rm ext}=\bigl\{\,Q=-Q,\ \#Q\le n_{\rm pack}\,\bigr\},
\qquad T'(Q)\le\frac{N}{2}\le20 .
\]

\section*{3. Physical justification: the kinetic penalty}

The Gaussian-Hartree kinetic kernel is
\[
K_0(k)=r_R+c\,(|k|^2-q_0^2)^2,
\]
minimised on the soft shell $|k|=q_0$ with $K_0(q_0)=r_R=0.3045$. An off-shell mode at the
nearest distinct lattice radius $|k|=\sqrt2\,q_0$ carries
\[
K_0(\sqrt2\,q_0)-r_R=0.214\ \text{per mode}\ =\ \times50\ \text{the selection MARGIN}\ (0.00432);
\]
further radii cost more ($\times3.8$, $\times7.3$ in $K_0$ at $\sqrt3 q_0$, $2q_0$). A
competitor carrying off-shell modes therefore cannot be a LEADING Reading-H competitor: its
kinetic cost exceeds the entire selection margin by $\sim50\times$ per off-shell mode.
Off-shell spectral content is Gaussian-sea / higher-energy / adversarial perturbation, not an
independent Bragg competitor. Hence shell-supported is the primary physical class.

\section*{4. Two-tier closure}

\[
\boxed{\;
\begin{array}{lll}
\text{Primary } \mathcal A_{\rm adm} & T'\le13 & R_{\rm lead}(13)=0.650,\ \mathrm{joint}=\times1.097\ \text{(comfortable)}\\
\text{Adversarial } \mathcal A_{\rm ext} & T'\le N/2\le20 & R_{\rm lead}(20)=0.974,\ \mathrm{joint}=\times1.082\ \text{(razor-thin)}
\end{array}\;}
\]
On the primary class the H-LAYER analytic closure is comfortable. On the adversarial class the
thresholds still hold ($20<20.5<26.2<60.4$), so off-shell escapes cannot break the closure --
they only thin the RES-1 margin to $\times1.026$. The proof is thus physically primary
(shell-supported) AND adversarially safe (fallback-controlled).

order. And the escape is NOT dodged: it is retained in $\mathcal A_{\rm ext}$ and controlled by
$T'\le N/2\le20$.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The adversarial fallback is razor-thin (RES-1 $\times1.026$).''}

primary class is shell-supported (comfortable $\times1.54$); the thin fallback is a
reviewer-defence layer, not the physical operating point.

competitor-class sign-off and ceilings any re-tier at a hypothesis-reduced T6. B1/B2 stay T6.
\end{itemize}
