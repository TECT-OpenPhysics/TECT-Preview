\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The H-LAYER analytic-closure consolidation (hlayer-analytic-closure-consolidation
v1.0) reduced the remaining H-LAYER item to the COMPLETENESS of the admissible
competitor class
\[
\mathcal A_{\rm adm}=\Bigl\{\,Q\subset\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}\ :\
|Q|\le n_{\rm pack},\ \angle(k_i,k_j)\ge\theta_{\min}\,\Bigr\},
\]
flagged as a modelling sign-off. Operator directive (2026-06-10): refine/strengthen
$\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$ before sign-off by examining each exclusion boundary. This
note grades the three boundaries; two are physically derived, leaving a single
modelling assumption. Scope: operating endpoint $I=2\times10^{-3}$, anchor
$\mu^2=5\times10^{-3}$.

\section*{2. Boundary 1 -- sub-$\theta_{\min}$ (DERIVED)}

The coherence resolution is $\theta_{\min}=\sqrt{\hat r}/(2q_0^2\sqrt C)=0.627$ rad;
spectral mass at finer angular scales is not resolved as distinct Bragg peaks. The
coherence-indistinguishability lemma (H-LAYER-AUX, AddC) bounds any sub-resolution
restructuring $P\to P'$:
\[
|F[P']-F[P]|\le c_{\rm ind}I^2,\qquad
c_{\rm ind}=1.5|U|+\frac{6\lambda'^2 J(0)}{4(1-a_0)}\approx30\text{--}32,\quad J(0)=0.290,
\]
so at the endpoint $c_{\rm ind}I^2\le\mathrm{MARGIN}/33$ (ratios $\times898/\times139/
\times33$ at $I=4\times10^{-4}/10^{-3}/2\times10^{-3}$). A sub-$\theta_{\min}$
``competitor'' is therefore not a distinct variational object -- it is absorbed into
the Gaussian-sea sector with a free-energy shift $33\times$ below the selection
margin. \textbf{Derived exclusion}, not an assumption.

\section*{3. Boundary 2 -- super-$n_{\rm pack}$ (DERIVED)}

By the spherical packing bound, at most $16/\theta_{\min}^2=n_{\rm pack}=40.7$ points
fit on the shell with pairwise separation $\ge\theta_{\min}$. Any configuration with
$|Q|>n_{\rm pack}$ MUST contain a pair separated by $<\theta_{\min}$ (pigeonhole),
which reduces to Boundary 1 (sub-resolution, absorbed into the sea). \textbf{Derived
exclusion} (geometric packing), not an assumption.

\section*{4. Boundary 3 -- non-lattice (MODELLING, with fallback)}

The exact pin $T'(Q)\le13$ uses the circle-divisor bound R-026 (T7), specific to
RATIONAL crystallographic shells $\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$. A non-lattice (quasiperiodic
/ incommensurate / arbitrary real-point) condensate is a different variational ansatz.
This is the genuine \textbf{modelling input}: that the selected ground state is
CRYSTALLINE. It is, however, softened -- arbitrary-$Q$ competitors are NOT
uncontrolled: DR-2 decoupling gives $T'(Q)\ll_\varepsilon N^\varepsilon$ via
Bourgain--Demeter (R-022, T6 conditional) and the affine-invariance / parabolic-
rescaling argument (R-023, T4), so even a non-lattice competitor carries a
subpolynomial additive-energy bound (hence $K_{\rm floor}$ control, just not the exact
$\le13$). The non-lattice case is off the critical path (DR-2 legacy fallback); the
crystalline-order restriction is the one remaining sign-off assumption.

\section*{5. Verdict}

Of the three $\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$ exclusion boundaries, TWO (sub-$\theta_{\min}$,
super-$n_{\rm pack}$) are physically DERIVED and ONE (non-lattice) is a modelling input
with a weaker-grade decoupling fallback. The operator completeness sign-off therefore

``is the selected ground state crystalline?'' -- with the reassurance that even the
non-crystalline case is additive-energy controlled (just at the conditional DR-2 grade).
No tier flip: B1/B2 T6 on $\{$H-LAYER$\}$, B5 T5.
