\section*{1. Purpose}

The H-LAYER Final Consolidation listed three blockers to an unconditional (T7)
Reading-H selection. Blocker A was the off-shell-admissibility MODELLING input: the
T-024 operator decision excluded off-shell / arbitrary-multi-radius competitors from
the primary class by a kinetic-penalty argument, but recorded it as a decision, not a
theorem. This note converts it into a theorem. Scope: lattice competitors with a
distinct-shell off-shell component, at the operating endpoint $I=2\times10^{-3}$,
across the ROBUSTNESS-MU2 band.

\section*{2. The theorem}

\textbf{Theorem (off-shell domination).} Let a competitor decompose as
$Q=Q_{\rm on}\cup Q_{\rm off}$, where $Q_{\rm off}$ carries amplitudes $A_{\rm off}$ on
a distinct lattice shell $|k|^2=q_0^2+\delta$, $\delta\ne0$ (separated from the soft
shell, $|\delta|\ge q_0^2$ for the nearest distinct radius $\sqrt2\,q_0$). Then
\[
\Delta F[Q]-\Delta F[Q_{\rm on}]\ \ge\ \eta_{\rm off}\,\|A_{\rm off}\|_2^2,\qquad
\eta_{\rm off}=c\,\delta_{\min}^2-C_{\rm int}(I)>0 .
\]

\textbf{Proof sketch.}
(i) \emph{Off-shell extra kinetic.} The dressed kinetic kernel is $K_0(k)=r_R+
c(|k|^2-q_0^2)^2$, the Hartree shift already absorbed into the gap $r_R$ (gap
equation). An off-shell mode therefore pays, OVER the on-shell gap, the extra
$c\,\delta^2\,|A_{\rm off}|^2$; at the nearest distinct shell $c\,q_0^4=0.214$. This is
$I$- AND $\mu^2$-INDEPENDENT.
(ii) \emph{Interaction gain is $I$-suppressed.} The only way $A_{\rm off}$ lowers $F$ is
the condensate interaction. Its leading channel is the off-diagonal bubble
$-\tfrac14 W(Q)^2 B(|Q|)$ with $W\sim 3u_{\rm eff}A_{\rm on}A_{\rm off}p_2$, so the gain
per $\|A_{\rm off}\|^2$ is $C_{\rm int}=O(A_{\rm on}^2)=O(I/N)$. A generous leading
bound ($p_2\le N$): $C_{\rm int}\le\tfrac94 u_{\rm eff}^2(I/N)B_{\max}N=7.1\times10^{-3}$.
(iii) \emph{Domination.} $\eta_{\rm off}=0.214-0.0071=0.207>0$ (margin $\times30$). The
$I$-independent kinetic penalty dominates the $I$-suppressed interaction gain. $\square$

\section*{3. Robustness and scope}

$\eta_{\rm off}>0$ throughout the ROBUSTNESS-MU2 band $[\times0.5,\times2]$ (worst
$0.2069$): $c\,q_0^4$ is $\mu^2$-independent and $C_{\rm int}$ grows only mildly with
$u_{\rm eff}$. The KINETIC DOMINATION is rigorous; the INTERACTION-GAIN bound is the
leading $O(I/N)$ channel, the higher channels (sextic, cross terms) being higher-order
in $I$ and absorbed by the $\times30$ margin. NEAR-shell modes ($\delta$ within the shell
width / the coherence resolution $\theta_{\min}$) are not off-shell competitors -- they
are sea-absorbed (Boundary 1, T-023). Thus the theorem covers exactly the
resolved-off-shell (distinct-shell) competitors that the T-024 decision excluded.

higher-$I$ channels. A fully rigorous $C_{\rm int}$ upper bound (all channels) is the

\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Near-shell modes evade the theorem.''} ADDRESSED: modes within
the shell width / $\theta_{\min}$ are sea-absorbed (Boundary 1); the theorem is for
resolved distinct-shell off-shell modes, $|\delta|\ge q_0^2$.

(one of three). Blockers B and C remain; B1/B2 stay T6 on $\{$H-LAYER$\}$.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): \emph{kinetic} $c\,q_0^4=0.214$ ($I,\mu^2$-indep);
\emph{gain} $C_{\rm int}\le7.1\times10^{-3}$ ($O(I/N)$); \emph{margin} $\times30$;
\emph{band} $\eta_{\rm off}\ge0.2069>0$ on $[\times0.5,\times2]$; \emph{sign} the gain
vanishes as $I\to0$ while the kinetic penalty does not.
