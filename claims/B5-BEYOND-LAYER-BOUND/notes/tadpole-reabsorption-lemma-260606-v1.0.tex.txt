\section*{2. The lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Tadpole reabsorption -- sketch]
In the matched bookkeeping, the third-cumulant correction of any
competitor pattern is
\[
\delta F_3[\mathcal P] \;=\; -\tfrac12\,\langle\, {:}V_3{:}^2 \,
\rangle_c \;=\; -\tfrac12 \sum_t |\hat g_3(t)|^2\; 6\,\widehat{G^3}(t),
\]
i.e.\ the SUNSET channel only: the tadpole contraction $9M^2\hat G(t)$
is absent identically.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(i) Matched bookkeeping evaluates each pattern at its self-consistent
Hartree optimum: the dressed propagator $G$ solves the per-pattern gap
equation, and the condensate amplitude solves the per-pattern
stationarity equation, which contains the Hartree-dressed quartic
source acting on the condensate --- the $3u_{\rm eff}MA$ line of the
production engine (Math437 \S1: ``identical to the $0.25\,UN_4 +
2.5\,VN_4M$ line''; the dressed coefficient $u_{\rm eff} = u + 10vM$
is Lemma 1's object).
(ii) Expanding the interaction about this optimum, every Gaussian
self-contraction of a vertex leg either renormalizes the propagator
(resummed into $G$ by the gap equation) or sources the condensate
(absorbed by the stationarity equation). Formally: the perturbation
must be written in terms of vertices NORMAL-ORDERED with respect to the
dressed Gaussian, $V_3 = \int g_3\,{:}\delta\varphi^3{:}$, because the
non-normal-ordered pieces are exactly what the self-consistent dressing
already counted.
(iii) Wick's theorem for normal-ordered vertices forbids
self-contractions: $\langle {:}\delta\varphi^3(x){:}\,{:}\delta
\varphi^3(y){:}\rangle = 3!\,G(x-y)^3 = 6G^3$. The $9M^2G$ term ---
which arises precisely from one self-contraction at each vertex ---
does not occur. Equivalently: the would-be tadpole linear source
$3Mg_3 = 3Mu_{\rm eff}F + O(F^3)$ coincides term-by-term with the
stationarity-equation source, and re-including it at third order
double-counts the resummation.
(iv) The $O(F^3)$ part of $g_3$ (weight $O(I^{3/2})$) generates a
correction to the stationarity point of relative size $O(I)$; its
effect on $\delta F_3$ is $O(I^4)$, two orders below the margin scale
($< 10^{-6}$ at all anchors by the U4 sextic-fiber arithmetic).
$\square$
\end{proof}

No new machine numbers. The lemma is structural; the single arithmetic
statement (the $O(F^3)$ remainder is $O(I^4) < 10^{-6}$) follows from
the U4 fiber arithmetic ($\langle F^6\rangle = O(I^3)$ scale,
$116.6\cdot2.5\,I^3\cdot$kernel $< 10^{-6}$ at $I \le 2\times10^{-3}$)
on certified constants.

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): consistency --- the struck
tadpole bound at the endpoint ($5.0\times10^{-3}$) was LARGER than the
sunset bound ($2.75\times10^{-3}$); had the cancellation gone the other
way (sunset struck, tadpole surviving) the lift would be dead at the
endpoint --- the direction matters and is fixed by the contraction
combinatorics ($6G^3$ = full cross-pairings, no self-contraction;
$9M^2G$ = the self-contraction pattern), not by magnitude tuning;
limit case --- for $\mathcal R_H$ ($F = 0$) both channels vanish and
the lemma is trivially consistent; sign-direction --- removing a
negative-definite channel can only RAISE competitor free energies,
i.e.\ the lemma moves the lift in the selection-favorable direction,
and it does so by construction (no double-resummation), not by
optimism.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Normal-ordering is a choice of bookkeeping;
choosing it BECAUSE it kills the dangerous channel is motivated
reasoning.''} DISMISSED: the choice was made by the chain long before
U4 --- matched bookkeeping with per-pattern self-consistent dressing IS
the executed production scheme (Math426--Math437); the lemma merely
states its third-order consequence. The double-counting argument is
independent of preference: including both the resummed Hartree source
AND the raw tadpole contraction counts the same Wick pairing twice,
which is an error in ANY scheme.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Competitor patterns in the class-wide
statement are quantified over, not individually re-optimized --- maybe
some are evaluated OFF their stationarity point, where the linear term
does not vanish.''} VALID-with-mitigation: the chain's comparison
functional evaluates each pattern at its own layer optimum (the races
optimize $t$ and $m$ per pattern; Prop A quantifies over ALL trials
$(t,m)$, dominating every off-optimum evaluation from below). For a
pattern evaluated off-optimum, the residual linear term generates a
SHIFT whose energy gain is bounded by the second-order expansion of the
layer functional along the shift direction --- an in-layer move that
the Prop-A quantification over $(t,m)$ trials already covers for
amplitude directions, and which for profile directions is an amended-
class move covered by the class quantification. The formal writeup
(R-U6-1) will carry this case explicitly.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The $O(F^3)$ remainder estimate assumes
generic fibers; resonant triples (sum landing back on a shell mode)
have $O(I^{3/2})$ amplitude interfering with the $O(I^{1/2})$ shell
source --- a cross term of order $O(I^2)$, not $O(I^4)$.''}
VALID-with-mitigation: the cross term shifts the stationarity SOURCE at
relative order $O(I)$ (the $F^3$-to-$F$ ratio), so the optimum moves by
$O(I)$ and the energy by $O(I^2)\times$second-derivative --- which is
the same order as the second-cumulant terms ALREADY counted in the
matched functional (the resonant-triple bookkeeping is exactly the
$N_4$ resonance accounting of the production engine, certified in the
Math437 gallery). The genuinely third-order remainder is $O(I^4)$ as
stated; the $O(I^2)$ resonant piece is not new. The R-U6-1 writeup will
make this two-line accounting explicit.
\end{itemize}
