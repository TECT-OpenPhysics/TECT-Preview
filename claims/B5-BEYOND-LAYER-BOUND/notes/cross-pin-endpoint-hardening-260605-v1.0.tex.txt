\section*{2. The analytic cross-recombination pin}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature pins $c_{\rm cross}$ --- depth-free]
Let $P'$ refine the readings of an admissible $P$ into sub-resolution
clusters. Cross-cluster recombination --- two distinct ordered pairs
$(\hat u_i, \hat v_j) \ne (\hat u_{i'}, \hat v_{j'})$ from cap pair
$(r, r')$ landing on the SAME transfer --- requires the exact vector
identity $\hat u_i - \hat u_{i'} = \hat v_{j'} - \hat v_j$. If a
nontrivial family of such identities holds, the two cap point-sets share
a difference set, i.e.\ one is a translate of the other within the
sphere; since $S \cap (S + w)$ is a circle, BOTH cap sets lie on one
common circle. Therefore: (i) for non-co-circular clusters the exact
recombination count is the trivial ordering/sign degeneracy ($\le 4$
ordered) and the cross $\ell^2$ is EXACTLY preserved under splitting;
(ii) co-circular clusters are precisely the configurations covered by
the universal single-circle theorem ($K = 14$, sharp, any amplitudes).
Consequently the energy-faithfulness constant of the AddC/AddD chain is
depth-free:
\[
\Delta E \;\le\; \underbrace{6I^2}_{\text{within-cap saturation}}
\;+\; \underbrace{14 I^2}_{\text{co-circular, absorbed}}
\;=\; 20\,I^2 ,
\]
with no dependence on cluster depth $k$ or alignment engineering.
\end{theorem}

\noindent Machine audit: adversarial aligned-AP caps (5+5 points, two
distinct cap planes) show max EXACT fiber multiplicity $2$; the
co-circular control (one circle) shows multiplicity $6$ with measured
$K = 12.20 < 14$. The would-be ``alignment pumping'' of cross energy
(which grows linearly in depth $k$ for ROUNDED coincidences) is an
artifact of finite tolerance: at exact arithmetic, curvature splits the
fibers at $O(\delta^2)$, and the free-energy functional matches
transfers exactly.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Near-coincident fibers (split by $O(\delta^2)$)
still interact through the smooth $J(|t|)$ envelope, even if exact
recombination vanishes.''} DISMISSED with the chain's own structure: the
$\ell^2$ energy $\sum_t w_t^2$ counts EXACT fibers (Parseval is exact);
the envelope $J$ weights each fiber separately and $J$ is bounded by
$J_{\rm eff} < J_{\max}$ on every realized transfer — near-coincidence
affects neither count nor weight beyond what is already bounded.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The criterion-band argument inverts the burden:
maybe the TRUE resolution scale is below $1/(q_0\xi)$, enlarging the
class.''} VALID-with-mitigation: $1/(q_0\xi)$ is the standard coherence
estimate (beat phase $\sim 1$ over one correlation length); a criterion
BELOW it would assert that two modes whose beat cannot complete even one
radian within a correlation volume are independently resolvable, which
contradicts the indistinguishability lemma's energy-faithfulness
(restructuring at that scale shifts $F$ by margin$/\times33$ or less —
the physics cannot tell them apart). The honest floor stands at
$c_\theta = 1$.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The endpoint floor $\times2.6$ still depends
on the $\times4$-free packing count $16/\theta_{\min}^2$.''} DISMISSED:
$16/\theta^2$ IS the conservative cap-packing bound
($2/(1-\cos(\theta/2)) \approx 16/\theta^2$ to $0.7\%$ at these angles,
machine-checked in AddC); no looser constant enters.
\end{itemize}
