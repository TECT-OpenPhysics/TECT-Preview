\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

AddA v1.2's amplitude-dyadic lift made H-KBAL structurally
non-load-bearing at the honest cost $64\sqrt7\,\sqrt n\,\log^2(2n)$,
with the sharp-constant optimization registered as follow-up. This
note delivers the first sharpening: half a logarithm, by a two-line
restoration of a constraint the original proof discarded. Scope: the
unconditional-amplitude theorem only; the official ledger (balanced
route) and the amended-class closure are untouched.

\section*{2. The sharpened theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Amplitude-dyadic lift, log-sharpened]
For arbitrary positive amplitudes (no balance hypothesis),
\[
\sum_{t \ne 0} w_t^2
\;\le\;
64\sqrt7\;\lambda'^2 I^2\, \sqrt{2n}\, \log^{3/2}(2n)
\;+\; O(\lambda'^2 I^2).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Identical to AddA v1.2 up to the class assembly: with $x_j :=
\sqrt{I_j\,N_j^{1/4}}$ the per-class-pair bound factorizes,
$\|w^{jk}\|_{\ell^2_t} \le \lambda'\,8\,7^{1/4}\,x_j x_k$, so by
Minkowski
\[
\|w\|_{\ell^2_t} \;\le\; \lambda'\,8\,7^{1/4}\Bigl(\sum_{j \le J} x_j
\Bigr)^{\!2},
\qquad
\|w\|_{\ell^2_t}^2 \;\le\; 64\sqrt7\,\lambda'^2 \Bigl(\sum_{j\le J}
x_j\Bigr)^{\!4} .
\]
The original proof bounded $\sum x_j$ by Cauchy--Schwarz against the
trivial $N_j \le N$, paying $J^2$ inside the fourth power. Instead,
keep BOTH constraints $\sum_j I_j = I$ and $\sum_j N_j = 2n$:
\[
\sum_{j \le J} x_j
\;=\; \sum_{j\le J} I_j^{1/2} N_j^{1/8}
\;\overset{\rm CS}{\le}\;
\Bigl(\sum_j I_j\Bigr)^{\!1/2}\Bigl(\sum_j N_j^{1/4}\Bigr)^{\!1/2}
\;\overset{\rm Jensen}{\le}\;
I^{1/2}\,\bigl[J^{3/4}(2n)^{1/4}\bigr]^{1/2}
\;=\; I^{1/2} J^{3/8} (2n)^{1/8} ,
\]
using the concavity of $x \mapsto x^{1/4}$ (power mean:
$\sum_{j=1}^J N_j^{1/4} \le J\,(\sum N_j / J)^{1/4} =
J^{3/4}(2n)^{1/4}$). Raising to the fourth power gives
$(\sum x_j)^4 \le I^2\,J^{3/2}\sqrt{2n}$ with $J = \log_2(2n) + O(1)$;
the tail classes are absorbed exactly as before. $\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent At the amended-class scale $n = 44$: $\log_2(88) = 6.46$,
$\log^2 = 41.7$ vs $\log^{3/2} = 16.4$ --- a $\times2.54$ improvement
of the unconditional constant; since the route's reach scales as the
inverse SQUARE of the prefactor in $K(n) \le 8 + c\sqrt n$, the
$n$-reach of the unconditional route extends by $\times6.5$.

\section*{3. The candidate second saving (flagged, not asserted)}

The per-class amplitude cap enters squared twice. With the dyadic
class $A \in (2^{-j-1}a_0,\, 2^{-j}a_0]$ and class point count $N_j$:
the LOWER end gives $\sum_{\rm class} A^2 \ge N_j\,(2^{-j-1}a_0)^2$,
i.e.\ $(2^{-j}a_0)^2 \le 4\,(\sum_{\rm class}A^2)/N_j$, so the cap
reads $\alpha_j^2 \le 4\,I_j^{\rm class}/N_j$ where $I_j^{\rm class} =
\sum_{\rm class}A^2$. The AddA proof uses $\alpha_j^2 \le 8I_j/N_j$;
whether the extra factor $2$ is a convention difference (class
intensity $2I_j$ vs $I_j$; signed-mode vs reading counting) or slack
is decidable only against the script's definitions. IF the $4$ is
available in the proof's convention, the prefactor improves
$64\sqrt7 \to 16\sqrt7$ (a further $\times4$). FLAGGED as a
definition-check item for the registered machine spot-check; NOT
asserted here.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``The Jensen step needs $J$ = the number of
OCCUPIED classes; the proof's $J = \log_2(2n) + O(1)$ counts
admissible indices. If occupied classes were fewer the original CS was
already tight there.''} DISMISSED in the favorable direction: Jensen
with the occupied count $J_{\rm occ} \le J$ only IMPROVES the bound
($J_{\rm occ}^{3/4} \le J^{3/4}$); quoting $J$ is the worst case. The
sharpening is uniform.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Minkowski-then-fourth-power may itself be the
lossy step; perhaps a direct $\ell^2$ assembly beats both versions and
the half-log fight is moot.''} VALID-with-mitigation: plausible --- a
direct assembly (orthogonality of transfer supports across well-
separated class pairs, or a $TT^*$ argument) is the natural next
optimization and is registered; it would supersede, not contradict,
this note. The half-log is what is PROVABLE today in two lines on the
existing architecture.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The section-3 candidate ($8 \to 4$) is the
kind of constant-fiddling that caused catch \#7 (the 28/13 slip);
flagging it at all risks premature use.''} DISMISSED by the handling:
the candidate is explicitly NOT asserted, carries its decision
criterion (the script's class-intensity convention), and is routed
through the registered machine check before any use --- exactly the
post-catch-\#7 discipline. The theorem of section 2 does not depend
on it.
\end{itemize}
