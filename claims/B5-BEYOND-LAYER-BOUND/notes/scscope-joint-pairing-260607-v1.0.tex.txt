\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The joint-assessment note (scscope-endpoint-joint-assessment v1.0) found the
additive endpoint joint at $\times0.757 < 1$ and registered the joint
incompatible-pairing argument as the route to recover the $\times1.32$ deficit.
This note carries out that route and reports the outcome: it does NOT close the
endpoint. Scope: the $I = 2\times10^{-3}$ endpoint; the pairing is evaluated in
its most favourable form, so the conclusion is robust.

\section*{2. The pairing argument and why it is the best case}

The two surviving third-order channels peak at different transfers: the sunset
per-transfer cost rides $J(t)$ (largest at small $t$), the quartic-difference
inflation $R(t) \propto \widehat{G^4}(t)/J(t)$ peaks at $t = 2q_0$
(scscope\_ghat4\_pertransfer). Modelling
\[
R_s(t) = R_{s,\max}\,\frac{J(t)}{J(0)},\qquad
R_q(t) = R_{q,\max}\,\frac{\Phi(t)}{\Phi_{\max}},
\qquad R_{s,\max}=1.42,\ R_{q,\max}=1.02,
\]
with maximally-separated peaks, the joint inflation is bounded by
$\max_t[R_s(t)+R_q(t)]$ rather than the additive $R_{s,\max}+R_{q,\max}$. Because
the peaks are maximally separated, this model gives the SMALLEST possible joint
max: any true per-transfer structure has peaks no further apart, so the true
joint inflation is $\ge$ this. Hence a non-closure here is unconditional.

\section*{3. Result: the endpoint still does not close}

Over the realized chords,
\[
\max_t[R_s(t)+R_q(t)] = 1.872 \;<\; R_{s,\max}+R_{q,\max} = 2.435
\]
(the pairing genuinely helps), but
\[
\boxed{\;\text{paired endpoint} = \frac{\rho}{1+\max_t[R_s+R_q]}
= \frac{2.6}{2.872} = \times0.905 \;<\; 1.\;}
\]
The maximum sits at the smallest transfer $t = t_{\min}$, where the sunset is
near its peak. The root cause is explicit: the sunset ALONE gives
$\rho/(1+R_{s,\max}) = \times1.076$ -- the third-cumulant sunset already nearly
saturates the $\times2.6$ second-order floor at the thinnest endpoint, so adding
even the small-$t$ tail of the quartic-difference ($R_q(t_{\min})=0.63$) pushes
the joint below unity. This is a structural largeness of the third cumulant at
$I = 2\times10^{-3}$, not a loose-pairing artefact.

\section*{4. Consequence: per-transfer refinements are exhausted}

The per-transfer programme that resolved M-ENDPOINT (sunset coupling) and
GHAT4-PERTRANSFER (quartic form factor) and now the joint pairing has been
carried to completion; it improves the endpoint from the sup-grade $\times1.0$
inventory to $\times0.905$ but does not reach unity. The SC-SCOPE all-orders
ENDPOINT therefore does not close by per-transfer/pairing means. The remaining
routes are genuinely different:
\begin{itemize}
\item a LARGER second-order STEP-5B endpoint floor (currently $\rho = 2.6$ at
$I = 2\times10^{-3}$): if the de-thinned floor were $\gtrsim \times3.9$ the
paired joint would close ($3.9/2.872 = \times1.36$); this is a B5 STEP-5B
sharpening, not a third-order task;
\item or accepting that the all-orders lift holds for $I \le 10^{-3}$ (where the
floor is $\times8.8$, paired joint $\times3.1$) and the selection at the
$I = 2\times10^{-3}$ endpoint is at second-cumulant scope.
\end{itemize}
