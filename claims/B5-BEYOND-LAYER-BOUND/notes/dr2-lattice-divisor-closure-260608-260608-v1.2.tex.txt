\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The configurations DR-2 must handle in TECT are BCC/FCC reciprocal-lattice
momentum shells -- lattice points on a sphere -- not adversarial sets. v1.0
reduced DR-2 for this class to the named classical hypothesis [DIV-CIRC]
(lattice points on a circle number $O_\epsilon(R^\epsilon)$) and graded it T6
CONDITIONAL. This v1.1 PROVES [DIV-CIRC] in full via a shift-removing
substitution, upgrading the result to T7 UNCONDITIONAL (modulo two textbook
number-theory facts). The route is self-contained: no Bourgain--Demeter
machinery, no R1/R2 residuals, no conjecture.

\section*{2. The lattice reduction and the proof of [DIV-CIRC]}

Let $\Lambda$ be a fixed crystallographic (rational) lattice ($\Lambda=\mathbb Z^3$
for concreteness; the argument extends to any crystallographic lattice after
clearing denominators) and
$Q=\Lambda\cap\{x:|x|^2=R\}$, $N=|Q|$. By Lemma A (R-025),
$E_+(Q)\le(1+T'(Q))N^2$ with $T'(Q)=\max_{m\neq0,\,r(m)>0}\#(Q\cap C_m)$ and
$C_m=\{x:|x|^2=R,\ x\cdot m=\tfrac12|m|^2\}$. We bound $\#(\mathbb Z^3\cap C_m)$.

\textbf{Lemma [DIV-CIRC].} For $m\in\mathbb Z^3$, $0<|m|^2\le4R$,
\[
\#(\mathbb Z^3\cap C_m)\ \le\ 6\,d\big(4R-|m|^2\big)\ \ll_\epsilon R^\epsilon,
\]
where $d(\cdot)$ is the divisor function, uniformly in $m$.

\textbf{Proof. (i) Shift removal.} The circle $C_m$ has centre $m/2$ (the
midpoint of every pair $a+b=m$). For $x\in\mathbb Z^3\cap C_m$ set $y:=2x-m$.
Then $y\in\mathbb Z^3$ and
\[
y\cdot m=2x\cdot m-|m|^2=2\cdot\tfrac12|m|^2-|m|^2=0,
\]
so $y\in\Lambda_m:=\mathbb Z^3\cap m^\perp$, a rank-$2$ sublattice. Moreover
\[
|y|^2=4|x|^2-4x\cdot m+|m|^2=4R-2|m|^2+|m|^2=4R-|m|^2=:R'.
\]
The map $x\mapsto y$ is injective ($x=(y+m)/2$). Hence
\[
\#(\mathbb Z^3\cap C_m)\ \le\ \#\{y\in\Lambda_m:|y|^2=R'\}\ =:\ r_Q(R'),
\]
where $Q(y)=|y|^2$ is the positive-definite INTEGRAL binary quadratic form
induced on $\Lambda_m$ (integer Gram matrix, $\Lambda_m\subset\mathbb Z^3$). This
is a HOMOGENEOUS (origin-centred) representation count -- the centre $m/2$ has
been removed exactly.

\textbf{(ii) Class-number bound.} For a primitive positive-definite integral
binary quadratic form of discriminant $\Delta$, Dirichlet's class-number formula
gives
\[
\sum_{[f]\ \mathrm{disc}\ \Delta} r_f(n)\;=\;w(\Delta)\sum_{d\mid n}\chi_\Delta(d)
\;\le\;6\,d(n),
\]
where $w(\Delta)\le6$ is the number of units and $\chi_\Delta\le1$. A single
class's count is at most the sum over all classes, so $r_Q(R')\le6\,d(R')$ (if
$Q=g\,Q'$ is imprimitive of content $g$, $r_Q(R')=r_{Q'}(R'/g)\le6\,d(R'/g)\le
6\,d(R')$). This bound is UNIFORM in $m$ (independent of $\Delta$, hence of the
plane).

\textbf{(iii) Divisor bound.} $d(R')\ll_\epsilon R'^\epsilon\le(4R)^\epsilon
\ll_\epsilon R^\epsilon$. $\square$

Both inputs -- the class-number formula and $d(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$ -- are
textbook; [DIV-CIRC] is therefore a theorem, not a hypothesis.

\section*{3. Theorem (lattice DR-2, unconditional)}

\textbf{Theorem.} For $Q=\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$ (any fixed crystallographic lattice $\Lambda$, after clearing denominators),
\[
\boxed{\,E_+(Q)\;\le\;\Big(1+6\max_{m\neq0}d\big(4R-|m|^2\big)\Big)N^2
\;\le\;\big(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon\big)N^2\,}.
\]
For Gauss-typical shells $R\asymp N^{2+o(1)}$, $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon
N^{2+\epsilon}$ -- DR-2 holds for the lattice class, UNCONDITIONALLY and with NO
decoupling.

\textbf{Proof.} Lemma A gives $E_+(Q)\le(1+T'(Q))N^2$, and [DIV-CIRC] gives
$T'(Q)=\max_{m\neq0}\#(\mathbb Z^3\cap C_m)\le6\max_{m\neq0}d(4R-|m|^2)\ll_\epsilon
R^\epsilon$. For $R\asymp N^2$, $R^\epsilon\asymp N^{2\epsilon}$. $\square$

The two statements are kept separate: the boxed bound holds for ALL lattice
shells; the $N^{2+\epsilon}$ form requires the absorption $R\le N^{O(1)}$, which
holds for Gauss-typical (dominant) shells but not for very sparse shells (where
the absolute energy is small in any case).

\section*{4. Numerical verification (exact arithmetic)}

\textbf{Proof chain} (dr2\_divcirc\_proof.py, 4/4, exact integers). On the
richest PROPER sum-circle of each shell $R=101,314,561,749$: the substitution
$y=2x-m$ lands in $\Lambda_m$ with $|y|^2=4R-|m|^2$ EXACTLY and is injective;

$r_Q(R')\le6\,d(R')$ with margin (max $T'/6d=0.25$). The degenerate $m=0$

signature); $E_+/N^2\le5.3$; $\mathbb Z^3=$FCC at $R=1826$ (lattice-independent).

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3, code-discipline 6)}

Four concrete objections, found before submission:

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Is the class-number bound really uniform over the
varying planes?''} YES, and this is the crux of why v1.1 is T7. The bound
$r_Q(R')\le6\,d(R')$ comes from ``single class $\le$ sum over all classes of the
same discriminant $=w\sum\chi(d)\le6\,d(R')$''; the right-hand side has NO
discriminant dependence, so it is uniform in $m$. Verified: $T'/6d\le0.25$ on
four shells.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The substitution might not be injective or might leave
$\Lambda_m$.''} Checked exactly: $y\cdot m=0$ (so $y\in\Lambda_m$) and
$|y|^2=4R-|m|^2$ hold for every $x$ on the richest circle, and $x=(y+m)/2$
recovers $x$ (injective). The parity coset $y\equiv m\ (2)$ is dropped (an upper
bound).
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Does this close DR2-SHARE / discharge H-ADM-COH?''} NO,
and this is the honest scope limit. The note closes the ADDITIVE-ENERGY DR-2 for
the lattice class. The STEP-5B carrier richness $\chi(P)$ and $T'$ are both
rich-circle quantities, closely related, but their exact equivalence is NOT
established here; the discharge of H-ADM-COH is a separate OPERATOR integration
decision. NO gate/hypothesis flip is made.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``Arbitrary-$Q$ DR-2?''} STILL OPEN. This route uses the
arithmetic of $\Lambda$ essentially (the rich-latitude example with $T'=N$ shows
non-lattice $Q$ is not reached). The decoupling route (T4+) remains the
general-case path.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Quantitative sanity check (mandatory).} Exact integer arithmetic.
Substitution: $|y|^2=4R-|m|^2$ verified per point. Class-number: $r_Q(R')\le6
d(R')$, $T'/6d\le0.25$. Magnitude: $E_+/N^2\le5.3$ ($\ll N$). Growth: $T'/N\to0$,
log--log slope $0.177<1$. Limit: $m=0$ excluded (split off as $r(0)^2\le N^2$);
$\mathbb Z^3=$FCC at $R=1826$. Direction: upper bound, all terms positive.
