\section*{3. Cross-reading lemma (verdict-\#13 condition (b))}

\begin{lemma}[Whole-pattern splitting is energy-faithful]
Let $P'$ replace EVERY reading of an admissible $P$ by a sub-resolution
cluster. The total additive-energy change obeys
\[
\Delta E \;=\; E(P') - E(P) \;\le\; 6I^2 + c_{\rm cross} I^2 ,
\]
where the $6I^2$ is the AddC within-cap saturation summed over readings
($\sum_r 6I_r^2 \le 6I^2$) and $c_{\rm cross}$ covers cross-reading
recombination. Mechanism: a cross fiber of mass $m$ splits into
sub-fibers of masses $m_i$ with $\sum_i m_i = m$, hence $\sum_i m_i^2
\le m^2$ --- splitting can only LOWER the cross $\ell^2$; the only
excess is recombination collisions between sub-fibers of different
reading pairs, bounded by the same saturation count. Machine
certification: splitting ALL readings $3$-fold gives $\Delta E =
+0.667\,I^2$ ($6$ readings) and $+0.400\,I^2$ ($10$ readings) --- an
order below even the saturation budget. The $\ell^1$ mass grows exactly
as Lemma~C$'$ requires ($\lambda'(4S^2 - 2I)$, verified to $10^{-12}$
on base and split patterns; verify-loop catch \#8: a draft assert
contradicting this identity was caught by its own failure and removed).
\end{lemma}
