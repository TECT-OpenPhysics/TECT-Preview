\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This note chronicles the DR-2 programme end-to-end. DR-2 is the unrestricted
sphere additive-energy bound $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$, the route
to removing the angular-separation hypothesis H-ADM-COH from the B1 selection
proof. The chronicle records what was tried, what failed, what closed, and at
what tier, so the programme's logic is auditable from one place. It introduces
no new result; every claim is cited to its result-ledger entry.

\section*{2. The obstruction: DR2-SHARE}

The elementary (extraction) route (dr2-extraction-lemmas v1.0) reduces the
additive energy of a competitor pattern to a sum over ``carrier'' circles: the
homogeneous part is capped per circle (universal $K=14$), and the dichotomy
adjoins rich circles. The sticking point, DR2-SHARE, is that adjoined circles
SHARE points, so $\sum_s I_{C_s}^2\le I^2$ fails and the cap degrades with the
sharing multiplicity $\chi(P)=\max_x\#\{$rich carriers through $x\}$. The
elementary route closes iff $\chi=O(\mathrm{polylog})$ class-wide -- an
incidence statement adjacent to a hard open conjecture.

\section*{3. Three routes}

\textbf{(I) Decoupling.} Bourgain--Demeter $\ell^2$-decoupling at $p=4$, $d=3$
gives $E_+\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$ for $\delta$-separated $Q$ (R-022,
separated case T6 conditional). The non-separated case needs an
induction-on-scales: the affine-invariance of additive energy (R-023, T7) plus
a decoupling-iteration inequality (R-024). Two corrections were forced by audit
(the false torus identity $\|f_\theta\|_4^4=E_+(Q_\theta)$, repaired via the
Besicovitch mean $M(|f_Q|^4)=E_+(Q)$; the ``exact paraboloid'' overclaim,
softened to $C^2$-perturbation stability). The honest residual is two cited
ingredients (local-to-global averaging; multi-scale $\epsilon$-bookkeeping --
the hard core of decoupling), so the arbitrary-$Q$ decoupling route rests at
T4+ STRONG EVIDENCE.

\textbf{(II) Elementary / PSM.} The external research reduces DR-2 to the
Pencil Rigidity / Pair-Sum Surface Multiplicity conjecture (T2), via the
cross-energy lemma (R-021, T7 -- which also corrected the external constant
$2|A||B|$ to $2\min(\cdot)$). This is a sound conditional reduction, not a
closure.

\textbf{(III) Lattice (Route A) -- the one that closed.} The competitor
patterns in TECT are not adversarial: they are BCC/FCC reciprocal-lattice
momentum-shell points. The sum-level-circle lemma $E_+(Q)\le(1+T'(Q))N^2$
(R-025, T7 unconditional) reduces DR-2 to the sum-circle richness $T'$. For
lattice shells, the substitution $y=2x-t$ (the sum-circle centre is $t/2$)
sends $\mathbb Z^3\cap C_t$ injectively into
$\{y\in\Lambda_t:|y|^2=4R-|t|^2\}$, a homogeneous rank-2 representation count
$\le6\,d(4R-|t|^2)\ll_\epsilon R^\epsilon$ by the Dirichlet class-number
formula (uniform). Hence $E_+(Q)\le(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon)N^2$ for any
crystallographic-shell subset (R-026, T7 UNCONDITIONAL modulo textbook number
theory; decoupling-free).

\section*{5. The resolution and the discharge}

The weighted bridge (R-027, T7): for any complex amplitudes $c$,
$\sum_t|w_t|^2\le(1+T'(Q))I^2$, $I=\sum_a|c_a|^2$. Identifying the STEP-5B
corner G1$'''$-AE with this weighted additive energy, R-026 gives, for the
lattice class, $\sum_t|w_t|^2\le(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon)I^2$ -- the G1$'''$-AE
bound with NO angular separation. The acceptance question ``is a subpolynomial
$K\sim R^\epsilon$ tolerable?'' was settled (R-028) as the finite inequality
$K_{\rm adm}=1+T'(Q)\le K_{\rm allowed}(n)=8+4\sqrt{14}\sqrt n$, verified with
margin $10.6\times$--$15.8\times$ (growing in $n$). Since H-ADM-COH only secured
G1$'''$-AE by angular separation, the operator (2026-06-08) DISCHARGED it for the
crystallographic lattice class, reducing the B1 active hypothesis set
$\{H\text{-LAYER},H\text{-ADM-COH},SC\text{-SCOPE}\}\to\{H\text{-LAYER},SC\text{-SCOPE}\}$
with B1 tier UNCHANGED T6.

\section*{6. What remains open}

The arbitrary real-point DR-2 ($Q\subset S^2$ not a lattice subset) is NOT
closed; it rests at T4+ via the decoupling route (residuals R1/R2) and is
retained only as a legacy fallback. It is not needed for the TECT mainline,
which uses only the crystallographic lattice class. The PSM conjecture (T2)
remains an alternative open route.
