¥section*{2. Additive energy is an $L^4$ norm}

For $Q=¥{q_1,¥dots,q_N¥}¥subset S^2$ and $f(x)=¥sum_{q¥in Q}e^{2¥pi i¥,q¥cdot x}$,
¥[
¥int_{[0,1]^3}|f(x)|^4¥,dx
=¥#¥{(i,j,k,l):q_i+q_j=q_k+q_l¥}
=E_+(Q).
¥]
Thus $E_+(Q)=¥|f¥|_{L^4}^4$ is exactly the quantity the $L^4$ (critical) decoupling
inequality controls in $d=3$.

¥section*{3. The decoupling input (black box)}

¥textbf{Theorem (Bourgain--Demeter 2015).} For the truncated paraboloid /
sphere in $¥mathbb R^d$, the $¥ell^2$-decoupling inequality holds for
$2¥le p¥le p_c=¥tfrac{2(d+1)}{d-1}$: partitioning the $¥delta$-neighbourhood into
$¥sim¥delta^{-(d-1)/2}$ caps $¥theta$,
¥[
¥|f¥|_{L^p}¥;¥lesssim_¥epsilon¥;¥delta^{-¥epsilon}
¥Big(¥sum_¥theta¥|f_¥theta¥|_{L^p}^2¥Big)^{1/2}.
¥]
For $d=3$ the critical exponent is $p_c=4$ -- exactly the additive-energy
exponent.

¥section*{5. The curvature mechanism, and the sharpening of R-007}

Why the sphere -- not the plane. An additive quadruple $q_1+q_2=q_3+q_4$ has, in
the height coordinate, $|q_1|^2+|q_2|^2=|q_3|^2+|q_4|^2$ on the unit sphere; with
the tangential constraint this pins the unordered pair (sum $+$ sum-of-squares
determine the multiset), so quadruples are rectangles (R-007). On a FLAT plane the
height constraint is absent and an $N$-point grid has $E_+¥sim N^3$. R-007 used
incidence counting to get the unconditional $E_+=O(N^{5/2})$; decoupling replaces
incidence by the sharp $L^4$ estimate and improves the exponent to $2+¥epsilon$.
