\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

DR-2 is the unrestricted-class bound $E_+(Q)\le C\,N^{2+\epsilon}$ for EVERY finite
$Q\subset S^2$. This note records the $\ell^2$-decoupling route to it, corrected
after an operator audit. It is NOT a closure: the separated case is rigorous
conditional on decoupling; the unrestricted case is a proof sketch. No tier or
gate flip is made.

\section*{2. Additive energy and a localised $L^4$ majorant (corrected)}

Let $f_Q(x)=\sum_{q\in Q}e^{2\pi i\,q\cdot x}$. The naive torus identity
$\int_{[0,1]^3}|f_Q|^4=E_+(Q)$ is FALSE: it needs $q\in\mathbb Z^3$, but
$q\in S^2$ is generally non-integer, so the exponential integral is a
$\mathrm{sinc}$ product, not a Kronecker $\delta$. The correct statements:
\[
E_+(Q)=\lim_{R\to\infty}\frac{1}{|B_R|}\int_{B_R}|f_Q(x)|^4\,dx
\qquad\text{(Besicovitch mean)},
\]
and, for the upper bound that decoupling controls, take a nonnegative Schwartz
$\eta$ with $\widehat\eta\ge0$ everywhere and $\widehat\eta\ge1$ on the ball of
radius equal to the minimal nonzero gap of the sumset $Q+Q$; with
$\eta_R(x)=R^{3}\eta(Rx)$,
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;C\int_{\mathbb R^3}|f_Q(x)|^4\,\eta_R(x)\,dx ,
\]
because every diagonal quadruple contributes $\widehat\eta\ge1$ and every
off-diagonal quadruple contributes $\widehat\eta\ge0$. This localised $L^4$
integral -- not the torus integral -- is the object decoupling bounds.

\section*{3. Separated case (T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on decoupling)}

\textbf{Theorem (Bourgain--Demeter 2015).} For the sphere/paraboloid in
$\mathbb R^d$, $\ell^2$-decoupling holds for $2\le p\le p_c=\tfrac{2(d+1)}{d-1}$;
for $d=3$, $p_c=4$.

\textbf{Corollary.} If $Q\subset S^2$ is $\delta$-separated with
$N\lesssim\delta^{-2}$, apply $\ell^2$-decoupling at $p=4$ to $f_Q$ (one frequency
per cap) and bound $\S2$'s majorant by the single-cap $L^4$ norms:
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;C\!\int|f_Q|^4\eta_R\;\lesssim_\epsilon\;N^{2+\epsilon}.
\]
This is rigorous modulo the cited theorem -- T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on decoupling.

\section*{4. Unrestricted case: multi-scale reduction (T3 PROOF SKETCH)}

For arbitrary finite $Q$ (the actual DR-2), decompose by dyadic separation scale
$\sigma=2^{-k}$. At scale $\sigma$, points are $\sigma$-separated within
$\sigma$-caps; rescale each cap by $\sigma^{-1}$ to a unit paraboloid patch (the
sphere is locally a paraboloid, height $=$ quadratic), where the separated
corollary of \S3 applies and gives the intra-scale contribution
$\lesssim_\epsilon N_\sigma^{2+\epsilon}$.

\textbf{OPEN GAP (the load-bearing step).} The cross-scale energy -- additive
quadruples whose four points come from different separation scales -- must also be
shown $\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$, and the $O(\log N)$ dyadic scales summed
into the $\epsilon$-loss. This is standard in the decoupling literature
(decoupling is intrinsically multi-scale) but is NOT written out here; until it
is, the UNRESTRICTED DR-2 is a PROOF SKETCH, not a theorem. The numerics (\S6)
test exactly this: a clustered cap-grid (the worst non-separated case) still has
exponent $\approx2$, not the planar $3$.

\section*{5. $N^{2+\epsilon}$ is not $N^2\log^B N$}

Decoupling yields $N^{2+\epsilon}$ for every fixed $\epsilon>0$. This is WEAKER
than $N^2\log^B N$ ($N^2\log^B N\Rightarrow N^{2+\epsilon}$, not conversely). For
the TECT application the admissible mode count $n_{\rm adm}$ is finite (R-009), so
$N^\epsilon$ is a bounded constant; but the unrestricted ASYMPTOTIC theorem must
state $N^{2+\epsilon}$, and the $N^2\log^B N$ form is a separate (stronger) target
not delivered here.

\section*{6. Curvature mechanism and numerics (supporting evidence)}

The reason the sphere obeys $N^{2+\epsilon}$ while a flat plane allows $N^3$: an
additive quadruple has $|q_1|^2+|q_2|^2=|q_3|^2+|q_4|^2$ on the unit sphere, which
with the tangential constraint pins the unordered pair (rectangle rigidity,
R-007). R-007 gave the unconditional $N^{5/2}$; decoupling would sharpen it to
$N^{2+\epsilon}$. Numerically (dr2\_decoupling\_exponent.py, 4/4), the empirical
growth exponent over $N=16$--$144$ is $2.03/1.95/2.01$ for
great-circle / sphere-cap-grid / random, versus $2.98$ for the SAME grid kept
FLAT -- a direct demonstration that curvature pins the energy from $N^3$ to
$N^2$. This is strong supporting evidence, not a proof.
