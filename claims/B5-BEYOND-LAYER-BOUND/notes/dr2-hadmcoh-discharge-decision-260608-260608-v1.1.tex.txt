\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

STEP-5B is CLOSED-CONDITIONAL on H-ADM-COH, whose sole role is to secure the
G1'''-AE corner (the weighted sphere additive-energy bound) by restricting to
angularly SEPARATED competitor patterns. R-026 and R-027 establish that bound
for the physical lattice class WITHOUT separation. This note assembles the
discharge decision: it fixes the competitor class, restates the two theorems,
and -- the decisive step -- turns the residual subpolynomial-$K$ acceptance into
a finite numerical inequality against the STEP-5B tolerance. It enacts no flip;
it is the decision record for the operator.

\section*{2. The physical competitor class}

We fix the B1 competitor class to crystallographic momentum-shell patterns
\[
Q\subseteq\Lambda^\ast\cap\{k:|k|^2=R\},
\]
where $\Lambda^\ast$ is the reciprocal BCC/FCC lattice. This is the physical
competitor class of the TECT shell calculation; no claim is made for arbitrary
real point sets $Q\subset S^2$ (arbitrary-$Q$ DR-2 stays OPEN, and is not needed
for the mainline). This is residual (b), an operator modeling identification,
here made explicit.

\section*{3. The two established theorems}

\textbf{Unweighted (R-026, T7).} For $Q\subseteq\Lambda^\ast\cap\{|k|^2=R\}$,
$E_+(Q)\le(1+6\max_{t\neq0}d(4R-|t|^2))N^2\le(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon)N^2$, via
Lemma A and the proved circle-divisor bound [DIV-CIRC] (substitution
$y=2k-t$).

\textbf{Weighted (R-027 v1.1, T7).} For any amplitudes $c_a\in\mathbb C$,
$I=\sum_a|c_a|^2$, $w_t=\sum_{a+b=t}c_ac_b$,
\[
\sum_t|w_t|^2\le(1+T'(Q))\,I^2 ,
\]
so the G1'''-AE quantity is controlled by the same sum-circle richness $T'$ for
arbitrary (complex) amplitudes.

\section*{4. The finite margin (residual (a), settled numerically)}

The STEP-5B closure tolerates an additive-energy constant growing as the
kappa-balanced bound $K_{\rm allowed}(n)=8+c_R\sqrt n$ with $c_R=4\sqrt{14}$
(theorem-grade, beyond-layer v2.0), against the layer margins
$\times55.6/\times8.8/\times2.1$. The effective constant supplied by \S3 is
$K_{\rm adm}=1+T'(Q)$. We verify (dr2\_hadmcoh\_margin.py, 3/3):
\[
\boxed{\ K_{\rm adm}=1+T'(Q)\ \le\ K_{\rm allowed}(n)=8+4\sqrt{14}\,\sqrt n\ }
\]
for every crystallographic shell and sub-pattern, with margin $10.6\times$ to
$15.8\times$ that GROWS in $n$ (because $T'\sim R^\epsilon$ grows far slower than
$\sqrt n$):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{rrrrr}
$R$ & $N$ & $T'$ & $K_{\rm adm}=1{+}T'$ & $K_{\rm allowed}(N)$ \\ \hline
101 & 168 & 18 & 19 & 202 \\
909 & 504 & 24 & 25 & 344 \\
1826 & 672 & 24 & 25 & 396 \\
4994 & 1152 & 32 & 33 & 516 \\
9974 & 2040 & 48 & 49 & 684 \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

For sub-patterns $Q'\subseteq$ shell the worst observed ratio is
$(1+T'(Q'))/K_{\rm allowed}(|Q'|)=0.307$, since $T'(Q')\le\min(|Q'|,T'(\text{shell}))$
and $8+c_R\sqrt n$ dominates both branches. The former operator judgment ``is a
subpolynomial $K\sim R^\epsilon$ acceptable?'' is thus replaced by a verified
inequality with an order-of-magnitude, widening margin.

\section*{5. Corner coverage (residual (c))}

The G1'''-AE corner is the non-transversal multi-circle high-$n$ pattern class.
Every such pattern is a subset of the crystallographic shell, hence covered by
\S3--\S4 with no separate treatment. This closes residual (c) mathematically.
