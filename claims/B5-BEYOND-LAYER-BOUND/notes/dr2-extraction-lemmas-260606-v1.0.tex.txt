\section*{2. The lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[L1: diametral-disjointness promotion -- exact]
On a circle $D$, every point has exactly one antipode (within $D$), so
distinct antipodal (diametral) pairs of $D$ are DISJOINT two-point
sets. Hence if a carrier $D$ carries $p_D$ pattern antipodal pairs,
then $|D \cap P| = 2p_D$ exactly: a carrier rich in pairs IS a pattern
circle rich in points. In particular the pigeonhole seed ("energy
$KN^2$ forces a carrier with $\ge K$ pairs") promotes to "forces a
circle containing $\ge 2K$ pattern points", whose INTERNAL energy the
universal single-circle theorem caps at $14\lambda'^2 I_D^2$ (any
amplitudes).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Antipodality on $D$ is the involution $x \mapsto 2c_D - x$; an
involution without fixed points on $D \setminus \{\text{center
preimages}\}$ partitions points into disjoint 2-cycles; each pattern
pair carried by $D$ is one such 2-cycle of pattern points. Disjointness
and the exact count follow; the cap is the v1.8 universal theorem
verbatim. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[L2: extraction decomposition -- exact bookkeeping]
Let $\mathcal C = \{C_1, \ldots, C_m\}$ be any family of circles. Call
an ordered pair $(u, v)$ HOMO if some $C_s \in \mathcal C$ contains
both $u$ and $v$ with carrier$(u,v) = C_s$, else HETERO. Then, by the
antipodal-carrier partition (every pair has exactly one carrier),
\[
\sum_{t\ne0} w_t^2 \;=\; E^{\rm homo}(\mathcal C) + E^{\rm het}
(\mathcal C),
\qquad
E^{\rm homo}(\mathcal C) \;\le\; 14\,\lambda'^2 \sum_{s=1}^m
I_{C_s}^2 ,
\]
where $I_{C_s}$ is the intensity of the pattern points on $C_s$, and
$E^{\rm het}$ is the carrier-partition energy of the hetero pairs ---
the SAME object one level down, restricted to pairs not internal to
any family member.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The partition is the v1.9 theorem (each pair has exactly one carrier);
the homo cap is the universal single-circle theorem applied per family
member to its internal pairs (a pair internal to $C_s$ has $C_s$ as
its carrier --- both points on $C_s$ and antipodal on it). The split
is exhaustive and disjoint by definition. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[L3: dichotomy step -- conditional]
Fix a threshold $K$. Either every hetero-pair carrier has
$p_D^{\rm het} < K$, in which case
\[
E^{\rm het} \;\le\; K \cdot \max_D w\text{-mass per pair} \cdot
(\text{hetero pair }\ell^1) \;\le\; K\,\lambda'^2\,c_{\ell^1}\,I\,S^2
\text{-form}
\]
($\ell^1$-controlled by Lemma C$'$ of the main chain), or some carrier
$D^*$ has $p_{D^*}^{\rm het} \ge K$, and by L1 $D^*$ contains $\ge 2K$
pattern points: adjoin $D^*$ to $\mathcal C$, strictly converting its
internal hetero pairs to homo.
\end{lemma}

\section*{3. The named obstruction (honest)}

\textbf{DR2-SHARE}: the iteration of L3 grows the family, but family
members may SHARE pattern points (a point can lie on many circles).
Then $\sum_s I_{C_s}^2$ is NOT bounded by $I^2$ (subadditivity fails
under sharing: a point contributing to $k$ circles is counted $k$
times), so the L2 homo cap degrades with the sharing multiplicity.
This is the exact sticking point: bounding the maximum point-circle
sharing within the adjoined rich-circle family is an incidence
statement of precisely the class assessed research-grade (adjacent to
the open circle-incidence conjecture --- the verdict-\#14 record's
assessment, here pinned to a single named quantity:
$\chi(P) := \max_x \#\{s : x \in C_s,\ |C_s \cap P| \ge 2K\}$).
The elementary route closes iff $\chi(P) = O({\rm polylog})$
class-wide; the chain's measured worst families (exponents 2.04--2.08)
are consistent with bounded $\chi$, which is the pre-registered
sharp-$O(N^2)$ conjecture seen from the sharing side.

\section*{5. Numerical verification}

No new machine numbers; L1/L2 are exact structural statements over the
certified carrier-partition machinery (v1.9: $\ell^1/\ell^2$
reconstructions exact to $10^{-12}/10^{-15}$). A registered
spot-check: measure $\chi(P)$ on the chain's worst families (rand,
ring unions, AP stacks) --- expected $O(1)$, consistent with the
measured exponents.

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): consistency --- L1 applied to
the single-circle case ($m = 1$, all pairs homo) reproduces the
universal theorem with zero hetero remainder, and applied to the
two-ring family reproduces the exact $c_{\rm ring}(n)$ accounting
(the five-orbit pairs are all homo on the two member circles plus the
axial transfers, which the closed form counts); limit case --- at
$K > n/2$ no carrier can be rich (L1 would demand $> n$ points) and
L3's first branch is automatic, recovering the trivial $E \le
K$-linear bound; sign-direction --- adjoining circles only moves
energy het $\to$ homo (never creates energy), so the iteration is
monotone in the bookkeeping, and the obstruction concerns only the
CAP's growth, stated honestly.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``L3's first branch quotes an l1-controlled
hetero bound without a sharp constant; as stated it is not yet a
usable inequality.''} VALID-with-mitigation: correct --- L3 is
labelled CONDITIONAL and its first branch's constant is deliberately
schematic (the Lemma-C$'$ $\ell^1$ cap gives A form, but the sharp
pairing with the per-pair $w$-mass needs the weighted version); the
note's claim is the ARCHITECTURE plus the exact L1/L2, with L3's
constant chase as the registered next step IF the operator ever puts
DR-2 on path.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The obstruction framing could be wrong: maybe
the iteration can avoid sharing by choosing disjoint sub-families
(greedy disjointification).''} VALID-with-mitigation: greedy
disjointification (keep only point-disjoint rich circles) preserves
the homo cap but WEAKENS the conversion (pairs internal to a discarded
rich circle stay het), so the dichotomy no longer terminates --- this
trade is exactly why the obstruction is genuine and is recorded as
such; a cleverer selection rule is the open content.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Pinning DR-2's difficulty to chi(P) may be
an over-reduction: the open incidence conjecture has more facets than
point-circle sharing.''} VALID-with-mitigation: the reduction claim is
scoped --- "the ELEMENTARY (extraction) route closes iff chi is
polylog-bounded" --- not that the conjecture equals the chi bound;
other routes (polynomial method, etc.) are untouched. The scoping is
in the text.
\end{itemize}
