\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

R-026 closes the (unweighted) additive-energy DR-2 for the crystallographic
lattice class at T7. STEP-5B is CLOSED-CONDITIONAL on H-ADM-COH, whose role is
to make the G1'''-AE corner -- the weighted sphere additive-energy bound -- hold
by restricting to angularly SEPARATED competitor patterns. This note maps the
lattice closure onto G1'''-AE through an amplitude bridge and analyses what
remains for an H-ADM-COH discharge. It introduces no new physics; it is a
reduction/integration note.

\section*{2. Three identifications}

\textbf{(i) G1'''-AE is a weighted additive energy.} The STEP-5B corner requires
$\sum_{t}w_t^2\le K(\lambda'I)^2$ with $w_t=\sum_{a+b=t}c_ac_b$ the
amplitude-weighted representation function of the pattern points $a,b$ on the
shell and $I=\|c\|_2^2=\sum_a|c_a|^2$. This is the weighted additive energy of the pattern.

\textbf{(ii) The ``carrier'' is the sum-level circle.} A pair $(a,b)$ with
$a+b=t$ is diametral on its carrier circle of centre $t/2$; that carrier is
exactly the sum-level circle $C_t=\{x:|x|^2=R,\ x\cdot t=\tfrac12|t|^2\}$ of
R-025. The carrier occupancy is $\#(Q\cap C_t)$, whose maximum is $T'(Q)$, and
the extraction-route carrier-richness $\chi(P)$ is the dual incidence quantity
(rich carriers through a point).

\textbf{(iii) H-ADM-COH is angular separation.} H-ADM-COH admits competitor
patterns whose pairwise angular separations are $\ge\theta_{\min}(\hat r)$;
finer-scale mass is assigned to the Gaussian sea. This is the SEPARATED-pattern
restriction -- the regime in which the additive-energy bound is classically
easy. Removing H-ADM-COH is the unrestricted (non-separated) problem.

\section*{3. The amplitude bridge (WEIGHTED Lemma A)}

\textbf{Lemma (weighted).} For finite $Q\subset S^2$ and any amplitudes
$c=(c_a)_{a\in Q}$,
\[
\boxed{\ \sum_t |w_t|^2\ \le\ \big(1+T'(Q)\big)\,I^2\ },\qquad
w_t=\sum_{a+b=t}c_ac_b,\quad I=\sum_a|c_a|^2 .
\]

\textbf{Proof.} Split $\sum_t |w_t|^2=|w_0|^2+\sum_{t\neq0}|w_t|^2$. For
$t=0$, $w_0=\sum_{a:-a\in Q}c_ac_{-a}$, so by Cauchy--Schwarz
$|w_0|\le\sum_a|c_a||c_{-a}|\le\big(\sum_a|c_a|^2\big)^{1/2}\big(\sum_a|c_{-a}|^2\big)^{1/2}=I$,
i.e.\ $|w_0|^2\le I^2$. For $t\neq0$, Cauchy--Schwarz over the $r(t)$ summands
gives $|w_t|^2\le r(t)\sum_{a+b=t}|c_a|^2|c_b|^2$, and
$r(t)\le\#(Q\cap C_t)\le T'(Q)$, so
\[
\sum_{t\neq0}|w_t|^2\le T'(Q)\sum_{t\neq0}\sum_{a+b=t}|c_a|^2|c_b|^2
\le T'(Q)\sum_{a,b}|c_a|^2|c_b|^2=T'(Q)\,I^2 .
\]
Adding the two parts gives the bound. $\square$

This is the amplitude-weighted analogue of Lemma A (R-025): the SAME sum-circle
richness $T'$ controls the weighted energy, for arbitrary amplitudes -- so the
amplitude degree of freedom of G1'''-AE does not require new geometry.

\section*{4. The integration bound}

\textbf{Theorem.} Let $Q\subseteq\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$ be any subset of a
crystallographic momentum shell, and $c$ any amplitudes. Then
\[
\sum_t w_t^2\ \le\ \big(1+6\max_{t\neq0}d(4R-|t|^2)\big)\|c\|_2^4
\ \le\ \big(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon\big)\|c\|_2^4 .
\]
Equivalently, $\sum_t |w_t|^2\le K\,I^2$ with $K=1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon$ --
the G1'''-AE bound with a SUBPOLYNOMIAL constant, holding for ALL subsets of the
shell (no angular separation).

\textbf{Proof.} WEIGHTED Lemma A bounds $\sum_t |w_t|^2$ by $(1+T'(Q))I^2$;
R-026 ([DIV-CIRC]) bounds $T'(Q)\le6\max_{t\neq0}d(4R-|t|^2)\ll_\epsilon
R^\epsilon$. $\square$

\section*{5. What this means for H-ADM-COH (operator decision)}

The bound of \S4 is exactly the G1'''-AE requirement, established for the
UNRESTRICTED (non-separated) lattice class. Since H-ADM-COH was the
angular-separation restriction that secured G1'''-AE, the lattice arithmetic
REPLACES separation: for the physical (crystallographic-shell) competitor class,
the weighted additive-energy bound holds without H-ADM-COH. The discharge is
nonetheless NOT enacted here; three residuals, all operator-level, remain:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] \emph{Subpolynomial vs constant $K$.} \S4 gives $K=1+C_\epsilon
R^\epsilon$, not a fixed constant. With $R\asymp N^2$, $K\sim N^{2\epsilon}$
(measured $K=1+T'$ grows like $R^{0.126}$). This matches the pre-registered
sharp-$O(N^2)$ conjecture (exponents 2.04--2.08) and is far inside the
$\times55.6/\times8.8/\times2.1$ margins for the operating $n$, but whether the
gate accepts a subpolynomial $K$ in place of the constant $K=14$ form is the
gate-keeper's call.
\item[(b)] \emph{Modeling identification.} The discharge requires that admissible
competitor patterns are exactly subsets of the crystallographic momentum shell
(physical for a lattice field theory); this is an operator modeling
confirmation, not a mathematical step.
\item[(c)] \emph{Corner coverage.} The G1'''-AE corner (non-transversal
multi-circle high-$n$) is a lattice-shell subset, hence covered by \S4; the
operator confirms no corner escapes the lattice identification.
\end{itemize}

If (a)--(c) are accepted, H-ADM-COH may be discharged for the lattice class and
B1's hypothesis set reduced accordingly. This note makes NO such flip:
DR2-SHARE stays OPEN, B5 T5, B1 T6, H-ADM-COH retained.

\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``This silently flips H-ADM-COH.''} It does NOT. \S5 lists

note's theorems (WEIGHTED Lemma A, the \S4 bound) are T7; the discharge is
deferred.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``$K$ is subpolynomial, so this is weaker than the
constant-$K$ G1'''-AE.''} VALID and recorded as residual (a). A subpolynomial
$K$ is consistent with the sharp-$O(N^2)$ conjecture and the measured exponents;
whether it suffices for the margin closure is the operator's call, not a
mathematical defect of the bound.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``The $\chi(P)$ route is bypassed -- is that legitimate?''}
YES: the extraction route's $\chi(P)$ obstruction (DR2-SHARE) was internal to one
proof strategy. The lattice bound proves the additive energy DIRECTLY (Lemma A +
divisor bound), so the weighted energy is controlled without ever iterating the
extraction; $\chi(P)$ is not needed. DR2-SHARE (the extraction obstruction) is
thereby rendered moot FOR THE LATTICE CLASS, though it stays formally OPEN.
\end{itemize}
