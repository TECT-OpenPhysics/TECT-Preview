\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

R-038 (dr2-t030-fewcircles) proved $E_+(Q)\le 3L^2N^2$ for $Q$ covered by $L$
circles, by Cauchy--Schwarz over the $L^2$ index pairs plus the R-021 per-pair
bound. The $L^2$ factor is the obstruction to a sharper open-core statement.
This note removes it: on a sphere the bound is \emph{linear} in $L$. The
operator direction was to sharpen the $L$-dependence so as to sharpen the
cover-number lower bound on a hypothetical $E_+\ge N^{2+\delta}$ extremizer.
Scope: arbitrary finite $Q\subset S^2$; the H-NONLATTICE-REMAINDER frontier;
NOT load-bearing for the published $C_{\rm full}$ head.

\section*{2. Theorem: the linear few-circles bound}

\begin{theorem}[linear few-circles bound, sphere]
Let $Q$ lie on an origin-centred sphere $S^2$, $N=|Q|$, and be covered by $L$
distinct circles. Then
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;(2+2L)N^2+4LN+4L^3\;\le\;6(L+1)N^2\quad(L\le N).
\]
\end{theorem}
\emph{Proof.} Write $E_+=r(0)^2+\sum_{m\ne0}r(m)^2$, $r(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=m\}$.
For $m\ne0$ and a pair $a+b=m$, compute $a\cdot m=a\cdot(a+b)=|a|^2+a\cdot b$ and
$|m|^2/2=\tfrac12(|a|^2+|b|^2)+a\cdot b$; hence $a\cdot m=|m|^2/2\iff|a|=|b|$,
which holds because all points of $Q$ are at the same radius (origin-centred
sphere). So both summands lie on the sum-level circle
$C_m=\{x\in S^2:x\cdot m=|m|^2/2\}$ and $r(m)\le t_m:=|Q\cap C_m|$. Each cover
circle $\Gamma_i$ is a sphere section, hence $\Gamma_i=C_{m_i}$ for a unique
$m_i\ne0$ (a great circle is $C_m$ for no $m\ne0$; its antipodal pairs feed only
the $m=0$ term). Two distinct circles meet in $\le2$ points, so for $m\notin\{m_i\}$,
$t_m=\sum_i|Q_i\cap C_m|\le 2L$; and $t_{m_i}\le\nu_i+2L$ where $\nu_i=|Q_i|$.
Therefore
\[
\sum_{m\ne0}r(m)^2\le 2L\!\!\sum_{m\ne0}\!\!r(m)+\sum_i(\nu_i+2L)^2
\le 2L\,N^2+\Big(N^2+4LN+4L^3\Big),
\]
using $\sum_{m\ne0}r(m)\le N^2$, $\sum_i\nu_i^2\le(\sum_i\nu_i)^2=N^2$,
$\sum_i\nu_i=N$, and $|\{i\}|=L$. Adding $r(0)^2\le N^2$ gives the claim;
$4LN\le4N^2$ and $4L^3\le4LN^2$ for $L\le N$ yield $6(L+1)N^2$. $\square$

The exact bound improves R-038 for every $L\ge2$ (any $N\ge4$); the simplified
$6(L+1)N^2$ form improves $3L^2N^2$ for $L\ge3$ (e.g.\ $L=8$: $54N^2$ vs $192N^2$;
at $L=2$ via the exact $6N^2+8N+32<12N^2$). The per-pair R-021 input is replaced by the sharper
sum-circle occupancy $t_m\le2L$, which is the multi-circle generalisation of
Lemma~A (R-025: $E_+\le(1+T')N^2$, the single-richest-sum-circle case).

\section*{3. Sphere-essentiality (a recorded guard)}

The identity $a\cdot m=|m|^2/2\iff|a|=|b|$ is the load-bearing step, and it
requires all points equidistant from the centre. \textbf{Off the sphere the
linear bound is not claimed (the proof mechanism $r(m)\le t_m$ fails)}: a same-radius latitude \emph{stack} (circles of equal
radius at distinct heights -- a cylinder, $|a|\ne|b|$) has product/AP structure
with $r(m)>t_m$ for vertical $m$, and indeed $E_+\sim 2LN^2$ there is consistent
with linearity but the \emph{proof} does not apply. The repository's correct
general-circle bound off the sphere is R-038's quadratic $3L^2N^2$ (whose R-021
input needs no equidistance). The proof-mechanism guard
($\#\{m\ne0:r(m)>2L\}\le L$, assert A6) FAILED on the first (cylinder) build and
PASSES on the rebuilt genuine integer sphere $x^2+y^2+z^2=1105^2$; the guard is
retained as the falsifier of any off-sphere misuse.

\section*{4. Consequence: a sharper cover-number lower bound and open core}

\begin{corollary}
If $E_+(Q)\ge N^{2+\delta}$ for $Q\subset S^2$, then the circle-cover number of
$Q$ satisfies $L\ge N^{\delta}/12$.
\end{corollary}
\emph{Proof.} $N^{2+\delta}\le E_+\le 6(L+1)N^2\le 12LN^2$ ($L\ge1$). $\square$

This doubles the exponent of R-038's bound ($L\ge N^{\delta/2}/\sqrt3$). Combined
with R-033 Cor.~1.2 (an $E_+\ge N^{2+\delta}$ family carries
$\ge N^{2+\delta}/(8k^2\log N)$ simultaneously $k$-rich \emph{sum}-circles), the
open core of T-030 is now pinned from two sides: a hypothetical extremizer must
have BOTH a super-polynomial point-cover number ($\ge N^{\delta}$ distinct
cover circles) AND super-polynomially many rich sum-circles. The lacunary /
few-circle families (R-038, technique-only witness) are excluded; the residual
is squarely the super-polynomially-many-circles regime, an open circle-incidence
problem.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3, code-discipline 6)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Just a multi-circle restatement of Lemma~A.''} VALID:
the occupancy bound $t_m\le2L$ off $\le L$ sum-circles is exactly the
multi-circle generalisation of Lemma~A's single-$T'$ bound. The new content is
the explicit $L$-linear closed form and the doubled cover-number lower bound
(Cor.), strictly improving R-038.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Is the linear $L$-factor tight on $S^2$?''} UPHELD as
open, and NOT claimed: on the genuine sphere the measured energy of rich-latitude
unions is $E_+/N^2\in\{2.97,2.59,2.42,2.32,2.27,2.22\}$ for $L=1\ldots8$ --
essentially $L$-INDEPENDENT ($\sim 3N^2$), far below $6(L+1)N^2$. The curvature
suppresses the energy (consistent with R-033's global $N^{9/4}$ ceiling); the
linear bound is correct but loose on these families, and its $L$-dependence may
not be saturable on $S^2$. No tightness is claimed.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Sphere-essential -- so the cylinder evidence is
spurious.''} VALID and documented (Sec.~3): the first test build was a cylinder,
the guard A6 caught the off-sphere $r(m)>t_m$, and the configs were rebuilt on a
genuine sphere where A6 passes. Off the sphere R-038's quadratic stands; the
linear bound is asserted only for $Q\subset S^2$, which is the T-030 setting.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``Great circles break the $m_i$ enumeration.''}
DISMISSED: a great circle is $C_m$ for no $m\ne0$, so it contributes $\le2$ to
every $t_m$ ($m\ne0$) and feeds only the split-off $r(0)^2\le N^2$ term; the
$\le L$ special sum-circles are exactly the non-great cover circles.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Quantitative sanity check (mandatory).} \emph{Mechanism.} On the genuine
sphere, $\#\{m\ne0:r(m)>2L\}\le L$ for $L=2,3,4,6,8$ (assert A6) -- the proof's
occupancy split holds. \emph{Magnitude.} $L=1$: $E_+/N^2=2.97$ (the rich
latitude, $N=108$, $E_+=34668$, matching R-033 F1) $\le 6\cdot2=12$. \emph{Limit /
improvement.} $B_{\rm lin}<3L^2N^2$ for all $L\ge2$ (assert A2). \emph{Estimator.}
vectorised $E_+=$ brute $O(N^4)$ ($396$, assert A8); all $L=1$ points verified on
$x^2+y^2+z^2=1105^2$. \emph{Reproducibility.} deterministic exact-integer; exits
$0$ iff all $6$ asserts pass.
