\section*{3. The N-counting is loss-free (power-sum lemma, PROVED)}

\begin{lemma}[Power-sum / superadditivity]
For $p\ge1$ and nonnegative integers $N_\theta$ with $\sum_\theta N_\theta=N$,
\[
\sum_\theta N_\theta^{\,p}\;\le\;\Bigl(\sum_\theta N_\theta\Bigr)^{p}=N^{p},
\]
with equality iff at most one $N_\theta$ is nonzero.
\end{lemma}
\emph{Proof.} $x\mapsto x^p$ is superadditive on $[0,\infty)$ for $p\ge1$:
$(a+b)^p\ge a^p+b^p$ (convexity + $0^p=0$); induct on the number of parts.
Strictness unless one term carries all the mass. $\square$

\textbf{Consequence.} Substituting the induction hypothesis
$E_+(Q_\theta)\le C\,N_\theta^{2+\varepsilon}$ into the one-step inequality,
\[
E_+(Q)\le D^2\Bigl(\sum_\theta (C N_\theta^{2+\varepsilon})^{1/2}\Bigr)^2
= D^2 C\Bigl(\sum_\theta N_\theta^{\,1+\varepsilon/2}\Bigr)^2
\le D^2 C\,\bigl(N^{1+\varepsilon/2}\bigr)^2 = D^2 C\,N^{2+\varepsilon},
\]
the last step by the Lemma with $p=1+\varepsilon/2\ge1$. The $N$-dependence is
therefore carried with NO loss at ANY split, balanced or not -- the
maximally-unbalanced partition $(N-1,1)$ obeys the Lemma exactly (machine: assert
1, $400$ random partitions plus $(N-1,1)$ for $N\le499$, worst $\mathrm{lhs}/
\mathrm{rhs}\le1$). This DISSOLVES the unbalanced-tree fear for the $N$-counting:
the only thing that can blow up is the $D$-factor.

\section*{4. The D-counting: naive tree fails; submultiplicativity repairs}

\textbf{The obstruction (naive fixed-scale tree).} Recursing the one-step
inequality at a FIXED scale $\delta_0$ multiplies a fixed $D_0$ per generation.
The maximally-unbalanced tree (peel one point per split) has depth $G=N-1$, so
the naive bound carries $D_0^{2G}=D_0^{2(N-1)}$ -- EXPONENTIAL in $N$, not
$N^{O(\varepsilon)}$ (machine: assert 3, $N=30$ gives $D_0^{2\cdot29}\approx
2.9\times10^{17}=e^{40.2}$). Any R2 proof that silently assumes balanced splits
or depth $O(\log N)$ is therefore WRONG; this is the documented failure mode.

\textbf{The repair (submultiplicativity / one-shot).} The $\ell^2$-decoupling
constant is submultiplicative across scales,
$D(\delta_1\delta_2)\le C_{\rm sub}\,D(\delta_1)D(\delta_2)$, and the
Bourgain--Demeter theorem packages the entire scale-$1$-to-$\delta$ passage as a
SINGLE application with a SINGLE loss $D(\delta)\le_\varepsilon\delta^{-\varepsilon}$
(the multi-scale induction is INTERNAL to its proof). Decoupling directly from
scale $1$ to the finest relevant scale $s$ (the min separation, where every cap
holds $\le1$ point = base case) gives
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le_\varepsilon\;s^{-2\varepsilon}\,N^2,
\]
ONE loss, independent of tree shape. For polynomially-separated $Q$
($s\ge N^{-C}$) this is $s^{-2\varepsilon}\le N^{2C\varepsilon}=N^{O(\varepsilon)}$
(machine: assert 4, $s=N^{-3}$ gives the fixed power $N^{6\varepsilon}$,
$\log$-dominating the exponential naive factor at $N=1000$: $4.1$ vs $1384.9$).
