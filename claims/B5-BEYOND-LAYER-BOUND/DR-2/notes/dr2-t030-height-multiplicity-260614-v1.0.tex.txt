\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

\textbf{Status.} The bound $E_+(Q)\le(2p_{\max}+1)N^2$ is PROVED for parallel circles with pairwise DISTINCT radii (T7 within that class). For genuine sphere latitudes it applies to subfamilies with $z_i^2$ pairwise distinct (on a sphere $z$ and $-z$ share a radius, so the full latitude union is not automatically distinct-radius). The broader sphere-latitude conjecture $E_+(Q)\lesssim N^2\log^{O(1)}N$ is STRONG EVIDENCE (T4), not a theorem; the test radii $5,10,\dots,5L$ are a distinct-radius test family, not an arithmetic sphere family ($\rho_i^2+z_i^2=R$). The remaining obstruction is the off-diagonal four-circle in-plane incidence energy, to be attacked in R-033.

R-039 proved $E_+\le 6(L+1)N^2$ (linear in the circle-cover number $L$) on a
sphere. The question (operator direction) is the \emph{true} $L$-dependence: is
$E_+=O(N^2\,\mathrm{polylog})$ for circle unions, or is the linear $L$ real? We
answer it: for distinct-radius parallel circles the linear $L$ is replaced by the
height additive multiplicity $p_{\max}$ (proved), and empirically the energy is
$O(N^2)$ \emph{regardless} of $p_{\max}$; the linear regime occurs only for
\emph{equal} radii (a cylinder, off the sphere). Scope: parallel circles (a common
axis; ``latitudes''); the H-NONLATTICE-REMAINDER frontier; not load-bearing for
the $C_{\rm full}$ head.

\section*{2. The refined bound (proved): $p_{\max}$ replaces $L$}

\begin{theorem}[height-multiplicity bound, distinct-radius parallel circles]
Let $Q$ be a union of $L$ circles in parallel planes (heights $z_1,\dots,z_L$) with
\emph{distinct} radii $\rho_i$, $N=|Q|$. Then
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;(2\,p_{\max}+1)\,N^2,\qquad
p_{\max}:=\max_H\#\{(i,j):z_i+z_j=H\}.
\]
\end{theorem}
\emph{Proof.} Write $m=(M_{xy},M_z)$, $a=(c_a,z_a)$, $b=(c_b,z_b)$. Then $a+b=m$
iff $z_a+z_b=M_z$ and $c_a+c_b=M_{xy}$. Grouping by the planes $(i,j)$ of $(a,b)$,
$r(m)=\sum_{(i,j):z_i+z_j=M_z} r^{ij}(M_{xy})$, $r^{ij}(M_{xy})=\#\{(c_a,c_b)\in
C_i\times C_j:c_a+c_b=M_{xy}\}$. For fixed $M_{xy}$, $c_a\in C_i\cap(M_{xy}-C_j)$:
two circles of radii $\rho_i,\rho_j$. For $i\ne j$ (distinct radii) they are
distinct, so $r^{ij}(M_{xy})\le2$ for \emph{all} $M_{xy}$, and $r^{ij}(0)=0$
(an antipode of a radius-$\rho_i$ point has radius $\rho_i\ne\rho_j$). For $i=j$:
$r^{ii}(M_{xy})\le2$ for $M_{xy}\ne0$ and $r^{ii}(0)=\nu_i$. Let
$p(M_z)=\#\{(i,j):z_i+z_j=M_z\}$. Then for $M_{xy}\ne0$, $r(m)\le2p(M_z)$, and
\[
\sum_{M_{xy}\ne0}\!\!r(m)^2\le 2p(M_z)\!\!\sum_{M_{xy}\ne0}\!\!r(m)
=2p(M_z)\!\!\sum_{(i,j)\in P(M_z)}\!\!\nu_i\nu_j,
\]
so summing over $M_z$ gives $\le 2p_{\max}\sum_{i,j}\nu_i\nu_j=2p_{\max}N^2$. The
$M_{xy}=0$ slice receives only the diagonal $i=j$ (distinct radii kill $i\ne j$):
$r((0,2z_i))=\nu_i$, so $\sum_{M_z}r((0,M_z))^2=\sum_i\nu_i^2\le N^2$. Adding,
$E_+\le(2p_{\max}+1)N^2$. $\square$

This is the multi-circle refinement of Lemma~A (R-025, the single-$T'$ case) and
strictly sharper than R-039's $6(L+1)N^2$ whenever $p_{\max}<3(L+1)/(2)-\tfrac12$,
in particular for sub-AP height structure (machine: assert A5, $p_{\max}\le2$ for
Sidon heights).

\section*{3. The empirical truth: distinct radii force $O(N^2)$}

The bound's $p_{\max}$ is itself loose. With distinct radii (the sphere case:
latitudes have radii $\sqrt{R-z_i^2}$ -- equal for $z_i$ and $-z_i$, hence distinct ONLY when the $z_i^2$ are pairwise distinct), the $p(M_z)$
colliding plane-pairs land on \emph{different} in-plane sums $M_{xy}$ (distinct
radii give distinct Minkowski annuli), so the energy never concentrates. Exact
integer experiments (distinct rich radii $5,10,\dots,5L$; assert A2, A3):
\[
\begin{array}{l|ccccccccc}
L & 2&3&4&5&6&7&8&10&12\\\hline
\text{Sidon heights: } p_{\max} & 2&2&2&2&2&2&2&2&2\\
\quad E_+/N^2 & 2.60&2.45&2.38&2.43&2.38&2.33&2.29&2.26&2.22\\
\text{AP heights: } p_{\max} & 2&3&4&5&6&7&8&10&12\\
\quad E_+/N^2 & 2.60&2.48&2.52&2.70&2.78&2.72&2.74&2.77&2.83
\end{array}
\]
Both stay $\sim3$, \emph{independent of $L$ and of $p_{\max}$}: distinct radii
suppress to $O(N^2)$ even at maximal height multiplicity. The contrast is decisive
(assert A4): \emph{equal}-radius circles (a cylinder) at AP heights give
$E_+/N^2=4.3,6.0,7.8,\dots,22.9$ for $L=2,\dots,12$ with $E_+/(LN^2)\approx1.9$
constant -- i.e.\ $E_+\sim2LN^2$, linear. The product structure $C\times\mathrm{AP}$
(equal radius makes every in-plane sum coincide) is what creates the linear $L$;
the full cylinder mechanism (all $L$ circles sharing ONE radius) cannot occur for genuine sphere latitudes, where at most two latitudes ($\pm z$) share a radius; the tested linear mechanism is thus absent for sphere latitude subfamilies with $z_i^2$ distinct.

\section*{4. Conjecture and the residual (an incidence problem $\to$ R-033)}

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Conjecture (sphere circle-union energy).} For
$Q\subset S^2$ a union of $L$ latitude circles, $E_+(Q)\lesssim N^2\log^{O(1)}N$,
$L$-independent.\medskip
The evidence is the data of Sec.~3 (STRONG EVIDENCE T4). The residual obstruction
to a proof is the \emph{off-diagonal $4$-circle in-plane energy}
$\#\{c_a+c_b=c_c+c_d:c_a\in C_i,c_b\in C_j,c_c\in C_k,c_d\in C_l\}$ for distinct-
radius circles: the diagonal $(i,j)=(k,l)$ already gives $O(N^2)$ (R-021 in-plane),
and bounding the genuinely-distinct $4$-tuple contribution by $O(N^2\,\mathrm{polylog})$
is a circle-incidence statement -- exactly the technology that R-033 (the $N^{9/4}$
incidence route) addresses. \textbf{Falsification gate (pre-registered):} a
distinct-radius parallel-circle family with $E_+/N^2\to\infty$ as $L\to\infty$ at
fixed per-circle richness would refute the conjecture; the scan to $L=12$ shows no
growth (worst $2.83$).
