\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

Operator target (R-033): bound the off-diagonal four-circle in-plane incidence
energy
\[
\sum_{\substack{z_i+z_j=z_k+z_l\\ \{i,j\}\ne\{k,l\}}}
\mathcal I_{ijkl},\qquad
\mathcal I_{ijkl}=\#\{c_a+c_b=c_c+c_d:c_a\in C_i,c_b\in C_j,c_c\in C_k,c_d\in C_l\},
\]
by $N^2\log^{O(1)}N$ or at least $o(N^{9/4})$ -- which lifts the R-040 sphere
$O(N^2)$ conjecture to a theorem. This note is step~2: two PROVED structural
results that localise the obstruction; it does NOT close the incidence bound.

\section*{2. The height-energy bound (proved)}

\begin{proposition}[height-energy bound]
For $L$ parallel circles with distinct radii at heights $z_i$,
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;N^2+2\sum_{k,l}\nu_k\nu_l\,p(z_k+z_l),\qquad
p(H)=\#\{(i,j):z_i+z_j=H\}.
\]
For uniform $\nu_i=N/L$ this is $E_+\le N^2+2(N^2/L^2)E_h$, with
$E_h=\sum_H p(H)^2$ the HEIGHT additive energy.
\end{proposition}
\emph{Proof.} As in R-040, for $M_{xy}\ne0$, $r(M_{xy},M_z)\le2p(M_z)$ and
$\sum_{M_{xy}\ne0}r(M_{xy},M_z)\le\sum_{(i,j)\in P(M_z)}\nu_i\nu_j$, so
$\sum_{M_{xy}\ne0}r^2\le2p(M_z)\sum_{(i,j)\in P(M_z)}\nu_i\nu_j$; the $M_{xy}=0$
slice is the diagonal $\le N^2$ (distinct radii). Summing over $M_z$ and using
$\sum_{M_z}p(M_z)\!\!\sum_{(i,j)\in P(M_z)}\!\!\nu_i\nu_j=\sum_{k,l}\nu_k\nu_l\,p(z_k+z_l)$
gives the claim. $\square$

This recovers both regimes (assert A2): Sidon heights have $E_h\le2L^2$ (so
$E_+=O(N^2)$, matching R-041), AP heights have $E_h\asymp L^3$ (so the bound is
$O(LN^2)$, matching R-039). The controlling parameter is the \emph{height} additive
energy $E_h$ -- a $1$-D additive-energy object, a genuine dimension reduction of
the latitude-union problem.

\section*{3. The sum-annulus mechanism}

\begin{proposition}[disjoint annuli kill the four-circle term]
$\mathcal I_{ijkl}=\sum_w r^{ij}(w)r^{kl}(w)$, and $r^{ij}(w)\ne0$ forces
$|w|\in[|\rho_i-\rho_j|,\rho_i+\rho_j]$. Hence if the two sum-annuli are disjoint,
$\mathcal I_{ijkl}=0$.
\end{proposition}
Annulus overlap is therefore NECESSARY for $\mathcal I_{ijkl}\ne0$ (it is NOT
sufficient: the discrete sum-sets must also coincide -- machine A3 exhibits
overlapping circles with $\mathcal I=0$). This is the geometric reason
\emph{spread} (distinct, well-separated) radii suppress the four-circle energy to
$O(N^2)$ (most cross-pair annuli are disjoint), while \emph{equal} radii (a
cylinder) put every annulus at $[0,2\rho]$ -- maximal overlap -- and produce the
linear $LN^2$ blow-up (R-040 contrast).

\section*{4. What stays open (the R-033 core)}

The height-energy bound is LOOSE exactly where $E_h$ is large: at AP $L=7$ the
measured $E_+=22988$ is $0.26\times$ the bound $89456$ (assert A4). The gap is the
in-plane incidence cancellation -- the annulus-overlap pruning of Sec.~3 plus the
discrete-coincidence sparsity -- which neither the Cauchy--Schwarz per-quadruple
bound $\mathcal I_{ijkl}\le3\sqrt{\nu_i\nu_j\nu_k\nu_l}$ nor the height-energy bound
captures. Closing it -- i.e.\ proving
$\sum_{z_i+z_j=z_k+z_l,\{i,j\}\ne\{k,l\}}\mathcal I_{ijkl}\lesssim N^2\log^{O(1)}N$
(or $o(N^{9/4})$) -- is the open circle-incidence problem and lifts the R-040
sphere conjecture to a theorem. \textbf{This is the precise remaining R-033 target.}
