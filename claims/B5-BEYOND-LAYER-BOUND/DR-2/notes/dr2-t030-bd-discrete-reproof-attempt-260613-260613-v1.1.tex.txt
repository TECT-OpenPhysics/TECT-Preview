\section*{3. The poly-separated subclass closes (in-bundle, modulo [BD-CONTINUOUS])}

\begin{proposition}[Poly-separated closure]
Let $Q\subset S^2$ (antipodal, $N$ points, $\mathrm{diam}\,Q\asymp1$ after the
affine normalization R-023) have minimal separation $s\ge N^{-C}$ for a fixed
$C$. Then $E_+(Q)\lesssim_{\varepsilon,C}N^{2+\varepsilon}$, modulo only the
continuous $\ell^2$-decoupling theorem.
\end{proposition}
\emph{Proof.} Apply $\ell^2$-decoupling at $p=4$ and scale $\delta=s$, so each
$\delta^{1/2}$-cap carries at most one point of $Q$; $f_Q=\sum_a e_a$ has one
frequency per cap. With the Besicovitch-mean bridge (R1 $=$ R-034,
$M(|f_Q|^4)=E_+(Q)$) and the single-frequency cap norms,
\[
E_+(Q)=M(|f_Q|^4)\;\lesssim_\varepsilon\;\delta^{-2\varepsilon}\,N^2
=s^{-2\varepsilon}N^2\;\le\;N^{2+2C\varepsilon},
\]
using $s\ge N^{-C}$. Re-labelling $\varepsilon$ gives $N^{2+\varepsilon}$. The
only non-elementary input is the continuous theorem; no discrete black box and
no separation-restriction beyond $s\ge N^{-C}$ enter. $\square$

This is a strict refinement: the bundled [BD-DISCRETE] is here replaced, for the
poly-separated subclass, by [BD-CONTINUOUS] alone (machine: assert 1, the
exponent $2+C\varepsilon'$ stays $2+O(\varepsilon)$ for every fixed $C$). The
crystallographic-shell case ($s\asymp N^{-1/2}$, poly-separated) is an
independent proven-case confirmation: $E_+/N^2\le5.27/4.93/3.93$ on
$R=101/909/4994$ (assert 4, R-026 data).

\section*{4. The clustered case reduces to [NEST-DEPTH] (the residual)}

For sub-polynomially-separated $Q$ ($s<N^{-C}$ for every $C$) the factor
$s^{-2\varepsilon}$ is not $N^{O(\varepsilon)}$ and Prop.~1 fails. The natural
move is recursion: a cluster in a ball of radius $\rho$ rescales (R-023,
loss-free) to a unit paraboloid patch, and one recurses on the rescaled
sub-configuration. The obstruction is the recursion DEPTH.

\begin{lemma}[Nesting-depth obstruction]
There exist $N$-point configurations whose nested-cluster recursion has depth
$\asymp\log(1/s)$, unbounded as $s\to0$ at fixed $N$.
\end{lemma}
\emph{Proof / witness.} The lacunary configuration with one-dimensional positions
$\{2^{-j}:j=0,\ldots,J\}$ (carried onto $S^2$) has $\min$-separation $s=2^{-J}$
and exactly $J=\log_2(1/s)$ dyadic scales at each of which a single point splits
off -- a genuine split per scale, depth $J$ (machine: assert 2, depth
$10/20/40$ at $J=10/20/40$). Iterating the one-step decoupling inequality at a
fixed scale across $J$ nested generations carries $D_0^{2J}$, exponential in the
depth (assert 3, $\exp(55.5)$ at $J=40$) -- the R-035 obstruction, now localized
to the nesting recursion. $\square$

\textbf{Consequence.} The in-bundle reproof closes the poly-separated subclass
but reduces the general case to controlling this depth. Define
\[
\text{[NEST-DEPTH]: a uniform bound on the nested-cluster recursion}
\]
\[
\text{depth (equivalently, } s\ge N^{-O(1)} \text{ suffices).}
\]
Then
\[
\text{[BD-DISCRETE]}\;=\;\text{[BD-CONTINUOUS]}\;+\;\text{[NEST-DEPTH]},
\]
and the in-bundle reproof discharges the first summand (textbook) but not the
second. [NEST-DEPTH] is the genuine open piece; it is the discrete
pigeonholing internal to Bourgain--Demeter's discrete corollary, which the power-sum
lemma (R-035) handles for the $N$-counting but which carries an irreducible
$D$-loss for sub-polynomial clustering.
