\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

dr2-decoupling-closure v1.1 proved DR-2 for $\delta$-separated $Q\subset S^2$
(T6 conditional on decoupling) and left the ARBITRARY-$Q$ (non-separated) case at
T4, with the cross-scale energy summation as the named residual. This note writes
that step: the decoupling-iteration inequality and the induction-on-scales. It is
conditional on the same decoupling black box; it does not reprove decoupling, and
it cites (does not reproduce) the tight multi-scale constant bookkeeping.

\section*{2. The decoupling-iteration inequality}

\textbf{Lemma (iteration).} Let $Q\subset S^2$ be finite, $\delta>0$, and
$\{\theta\}$ the partition of the sphere into $\delta^{1/2}$-caps; write
$Q_\theta=Q\cap\theta$. Then, conditional on $\ell^2$-decoupling at $p=4$,
\[
E_+(Q)\;\lesssim_\epsilon\;\delta^{-\epsilon}
\Big(\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)^{1/2}\Big)^2 .
\]
\textbf{Derivation.} By the majorant of v1.1 \S2.1, $E_+(Q)\lesssim\int|f_Q|^4
\eta_R$, $R\sim\delta^{-1}$, $f_Q=\sum_{q\in Q}e(q\cdot x)$, whose Fourier support
lies on $Q\subset S^2$. Decoupling at $p=4$ for the cap pieces $f_\theta=
\sum_{q\in Q_\theta}e(q\cdot x)$ gives $\|f_Q\|_{L^4}\lesssim_\epsilon
\delta^{-\epsilon}(\sum_\theta\|f_\theta\|_{L^4}^2)^{1/2}$, and
$\|f_\theta\|_{L^4}^4=E_+(Q_\theta)$. Squaring twice yields the inequality.
$\square$

\textbf{Numerical consistency} (dr2\_decoupling\_iteration.py, 3/3). The constant
$K=E_+(Q)/(\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)^{1/2})^2$ is $\le1.94$ on great-circle,
cap-grid and random sets at cap scales $n=2,4,8$, and STABLE across scales
(within $\times1.25$): the iteration holds with a small, non-blowing-up constant,
as the $\delta^{-\epsilon}$ factor predicts. The partition also breaks the
additive structure ($\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)\approx220$ vs $E_+(Q)\approx10^4$
at $n=8$): the cross-cap quadruples dominate, and the iteration converts them into
the controlled $(\sum\sqrt{E_\theta})^2$ form.

\section*{3. The induction-on-scales}

\textbf{Base case (separated).} If $Q$ is $\delta^{1/2}$-separated, each cap holds
$\le1$ point, $E_+(Q_\theta)\le1$, so $\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)^{1/2}\le N$ and the
Lemma gives $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon\delta^{-\epsilon}N^2$. With $\delta\sim$
(minimal separation)$^2\ge N^{-O(1)}$, $\delta^{-\epsilon}\le N^{O(\epsilon)}$, so
$E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$ -- this is v1.1 \S3.

\textbf{Recursion (clustered).} If some cap $\theta$ holds $m_\theta>1$ points,
$E_+(Q_\theta)$ is a SUB-problem on a $\delta^{1/2}$-cap. By the affine-invariance
rescaling (R-023), the parabolic map $T_\theta$ sends $\theta$ to a unit paraboloid
patch with $E_+(Q_\theta)=E_+(T_\theta Q_\theta)$ -- a FRESH instance of the same
problem at unit scale with $m_\theta<N$ points. Apply the Lemma to each, recursing.
The recursion terminates when every active cap is separated (depth $O(\log$
(dynamic range of separations)$)=O(\log N)$ for sets with $\ge N^{-O(1)}$
separations; deeper otherwise).

\textbf{The cited step.} Substituting the inductive bound $E_+(Q_\theta)
\lesssim_\epsilon m_\theta^{2+\epsilon}$ and summing, the naive accumulation gives
$\delta^{-\epsilon}$ PER LEVEL. The standard Bourgain--Demeter / Bourgain--Guth
multi-scale organisation (geometric choice of the per-level $\epsilon_k$, or the
fact that $\ell^2$-decoupling is ALREADY the multi-scale theorem and one
application at the critical scale carries a single $\delta^{-\epsilon}$) keeps the
TOTAL loss at $N^{O(\epsilon)}$, yielding $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$
for arbitrary finite $Q$. \emph{This bookkeeping is cited, not reproduced here.}

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3, code-discipline 6)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``The iteration inequality is conditional on decoupling.''}
DECLARED: yes; \S2 derives it FROM decoupling at $p=4$, it is not independent. The
note never claims to prove decoupling.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The per-level constant control is not written.''} UPHELD
and the reason the grade is T5, not T6: the geometric $\epsilon$-bookkeeping over
$O(\log N)$ levels is cited (Bourgain--Demeter), not reproduced. The iteration
constant is numerically bounded ($K\le1.94$, scale-stable), which is consistent
with but not a proof of the rigorous control.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Does very small minimal separation break it?''}
ADDRESSED: when points are closer than $N^{-O(1)}$ the single-scale bound is loose,
which is exactly why the recursion (R-023 rescaling) is needed; rescaling resets
the scale so the loss is per-level $O(1)$, not tied to the absolute minimal
separation. The numerics (K scale-stable) support this; the rigorous summation is
the cited step.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): magnitude -- $K\le1.94$ (small), and
$\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)\approx2\times10^2\ll E_+(Q)\approx10^4$ (the iteration
genuinely reduces); sign-direction -- the inequality is an upper bound, all terms
positive; limit-case -- separated $Q$ gives the v1.1 \S3 base case exactly;
consistency -- $K$ stable across cap scales ($\times1.25$) matches the
$\delta^{-\epsilon}$ prediction of a mild per-level constant.
