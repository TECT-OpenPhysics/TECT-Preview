\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

\section*{2. Theorem: Sidon heights give $E_+\le 6N^2$ (any radii)}

\begin{theorem}[Sidon-height bound]
Let $Q=\bigcup_{i=1}^L(C_i\times\{z_i\})$ be a union of circles in parallel planes
at heights $z_i$ (ANY radii $\rho_i$), $N=|Q|$, with the heights $\{z_i\}$ a SIDON
set (i.e.\ $z_a+z_b=z_c+z_d\Rightarrow\{z_a,z_b\}=\{z_c,z_d\}$). Then
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;6N^2.
\]
\end{theorem}
\emph{Proof.} In $E_+=\#\{(a,b,c,d):a+b=c+d\}$ the height coordinate gives
$z_a+z_b=z_c+z_d$, so by the Sidon property the height multiset matches,
$\{z_a,z_b\}=\{z_c,z_d\}$, hence the plane-index multiset $\{i_a,i_b\}=\{i_c,i_d\}$.
Indexing by the ordered pair $(i_a,i_b)$, the matched $(i_c,i_d)$ is one of
$(i_a,i_b),(i_b,i_a)$ -- at most two arrangements. The in-plane count of each is
either the rectangular energy $E_+(C_i,C_j)$ (arrangement $(i,j,i,j)$) or the
difference-correlation $\mathrm{corr}(C_i,C_j)=\sum_v d_{C_i}(v)d_{C_j}(v)$
(arrangement $(i,j,j,i)$); both are $\le3\nu_i\nu_j$ -- the first by R-021
($E_+$ of two circles), the second by Cauchy--Schwarz with $E_+(C)\le3\nu^2$.
Summing over ordered $(i,j)$,
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;\sum_{i,j}\big[E_+(C_i,C_j)+\mathrm{corr}(C_i,C_j)\big]
\;\le\;6\sum_{i,j}\nu_i\nu_j\;=\;6N^2. \qquad\square
\]
The bound uses the Sidon property of the HEIGHTS only; the radii are arbitrary
(machine: assert A7, equal radius $25$ + Sidon heights still gives $E_+\le6N^2$).
Measured $E_+/N^2\le2.6$ on the tested families (assert A2); the proved constant
$6$ allows for both arrangements. The Sidon-decoupling step is verified exactly:
zero quadruples with $a+b=c+d$ but mismatched height pair (assert A1).

\section*{3. The residual is the open four-circle incidence energy (R-033)}

For NON-Sidon heights (e.g.\ an arithmetic progression) the decoupling fails:
there are $a+b=c+d$ with $\{z_a,z_b\}\ne\{z_c,z_d\}$ (assert A4: $1248$ such
quadruples at $L=5$, AP heights). These mismatched-height quadruples are the
off-diagonal four-circle in-plane incidences
\[
\#\{c_a+c_b=c_c+c_d:\ c_a\in C_i,\,c_b\in C_j,\,c_c\in C_k,\,c_d\in C_l\},
\quad\{i,j\}\ne\{k,l\},
\]
and they do NOT reduce to a single bounded $2$-D concentric-circle energy: that
$2$-D energy GROWS with $L$ (assert A5: $E_+^{2\mathrm D}/N^2=3.2,3.9,5.4,6.0$ for
$L=2,4,6,8$), because the $3$-D heights suppress collisions that the $2$-D
projection overcounts. Hence the residual is genuinely height-aware: bounding it
by $O(N^2\log^{O(1)}N)$ (or below the current $N^{9/4}$, R-033) is exactly the
open circle-incidence problem, and closing it lifts the R-040 sphere conjecture to
a theorem. \textbf{This is the precise next target (R-033).}
