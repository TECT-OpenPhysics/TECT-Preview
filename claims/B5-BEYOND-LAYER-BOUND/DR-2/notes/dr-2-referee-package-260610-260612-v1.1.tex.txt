\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This referee document concerns DR-2 (the additive-energy / discrete-restriction bound) for the lattice competitor class. It is a harmonic-analysis / additive-combinatorics result, of interest independently of TECT. \textbf{Scope:} $Q=\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$ a fixed crystallographic lattice shell. \textbf{Not claimed:} the arbitrary (non-lattice) $Q$ case remains OPEN (the decoupling-based route is T4 conditional and not load-bearing); this package does not by itself close the full Reading-H $C_{\rm full}$ theorem. \textbf{Grade:} T7-candidate on the lattice class, modulo the explicit textbook Lemma NT (Sec.~3) and the 7-script clean-run; not a confirmed T7 publication until both are pinned.

\section*{2. Definitions and the result}

\paragraph{Definitions (patch v1.1-2).} Let $Q\subset\{|x|^2=R\}$ be finite with
$N=|Q|$. The additive energy and the sum-representation counts are
\[
E_+(Q)=\#\bigl\{(a,b,c,d)\in Q^4:\ a+b=c+d\bigr\},
\qquad
r_Q(m)=\#\bigl\{(a,b)\in Q^2:\ a+b=m\bigr\},
\]
and the maximal proper sum-circle occupancy is
$T'(Q)=\max_{m\ne0} r_Q(m)$. Lemma A (sum-circle richness) is the chain
\[
E_+(Q)=\sum_m r_Q(m)^2
\;\le\;
r_Q(0)^2+T'(Q)\sum_{m\ne0}r_Q(m)
\;\le\;
\bigl(1+T'(Q)\bigr)N^2,
\]
using $r_Q(0)\le N$ and $\sum_m r_Q(m)=N^2$.

\paragraph{The result.} For $Q=\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$ ($\Lambda$ a fixed
crystallographic lattice, rational after clearing denominators), the additive energy
obeys
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;\bigl(1+C_\Lambda \max_{m\ne0} d(4R-|m|^2)\bigr)N^2
\;\le\;\bigl(1+C_\Lambda C_\varepsilon R^\varepsilon\bigr)N^2,
\]
decoupling-free, where $d(\cdot)$ is the divisor function and $C_\Lambda$ is the
representation constant of Lemma NT below ($C_\Lambda\le6$ once the textbook lemma
is imported). For Gauss-typical shells $R\sim N^2$ this gives, after redefining
$\varepsilon$ ($R^\varepsilon\sim N^{2\varepsilon}$), $E_+\le_\varepsilon N^{2+\varepsilon}$.

\section*{3. Structure of the argument}

\paragraph{Circle-to-binary-form reduction (patch v1.1-3).} Fix $m\ne0$ and suppose
$a+b=m$ with $|a|^2=|b|^2=R$. From $b=m-a$,
$R=|b|^2=|m|^2-2a\cdot m+|a|^2$ forces $2a\cdot m=|m|^2$. Substituting $y=2a-m$,
\[
y\cdot m=2a\cdot m-|m|^2=0,
\qquad
|y|^2=4|a|^2-2\,(2a\cdot m)+|m|^2=4R-|m|^2,
\qquad
y\equiv -m \pmod 2,
\]
and $a\mapsto y$ is injective. Hence $r_Q(m)$ is bounded by the HOMOGENEOUS
representation count of $D=4R-|m|^2$ on the rank-two lattice section
$L_m=\{y\in\Lambda_{\rm int}:\ y\cdot m=0\}$ (machine check:
\texttt{dr2\_divcirc\_proof.py}, substitution exact, injectivity verified).

\paragraph{Lemma NT (textbook import; patch v1.1-4).} \emph{For every rank-two
lattice section $L_m$ as above, with induced positive-definite integral binary
quadratic form $q$ of content $g$ and primitive part $q_0$ of discriminant
$\Delta_0<0$,}
\[
r_{L_m}(D)=\#\{y\in L_m:\ |y|^2=D\}
\;\le\; C_{\rm NT}\,d(D),
\qquad
C_{\rm NT}=w(\Delta_0)\le 6,
\]
\emph{where $w(\Delta_0)$ is the number of automorphs ($6$ for $\Delta_0=-3$,
$4$ for $\Delta_0=-4$, $2$ otherwise).} Provenance: $r_{q}(D)=r_{q_0}(D/g)$
(zero unless $g\mid D$), and Dirichlet's representation formula gives the TOTAL
count over the full form class group of discriminant $\Delta_0$ as
$w(\Delta_0)\sum_{d\mid D'}\chi_{\Delta_0}(d)\le w(\Delta_0)\,d(D')\le
w(\Delta_0)\,d(D)$; a single class's count is at most the total. This is
textbook material (Dirichlet's class-number/representation formula; e.g.\
Landau, or Cox, \emph{Primes of the Form $x^2+ny^2$}), but the package treats it
as an IMPORT: the load-bearing chain is written with the constant $C_\Lambda$,
and $C_\Lambda\le6$ holds once Lemma NT is cited or proved in the bundle.
Machine evidence: $r\le 6\,d$ holds on every tested configuration with worst
ratio $T'/6d=0.25$ (\texttt{dr2\_divcirc\_proof.py} 4/4).

\paragraph{Assembly.} Lemma A + the reduction + Lemma NT give
$T'(Q)\le C_\Lambda d(4R-|m^*|^2)\ll_\varepsilon R^\varepsilon$ (divisor bound,
uniform in $m$), hence the stated result --- no $\ell^2$-decoupling is used. The
conditional decoupling route (\texttt{dr2\_decoupling\_iteration.py} /
\texttt{dr2\_decoupling\_exponent.py}) is retained as a CROSS-CHECK only and is
not load-bearing.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{Attack the scope.} The result is confined to the scope stated in
Sec.~1; a referee may push that boundary, which is fair --- the package makes no claim
outside it. In particular: lattice shells only; arbitrary-$Q$ OPEN; decoupling route
T4 conditional cross-check; no full $C_{\rm full}$ claim.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{Attack the tier / over-claim.} The sharpest objection is the
$6\,d(D)$ representation bound: ``Dirichlet class-number formula'' alone is not a
citation. ADDRESSED in v1.1 (operator-directed): Lemma NT is stated precisely with
its provenance, the load-bearing chain uses the relaxed constant $C_\Lambda$, and the
T7 label is restricted (footer): without the Lemma NT import the package is a T6/T7
draft, not a confirmed T7 publication. The claim-ledger tier is the arbiter.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{Attack reproduction / self-containment.} Each entry script is
self-contained (no runtime run-artefact reads --- audited after the SC-SCOPE
joint\_pairing lesson), carries self-test asserts, and ships in the bundle with its
transitive dependencies; a referee runs them and diffs against \texttt{expected/}.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): the Lemma-A chain is re-derivable from its
definitions ($\sum_m r_Q(m)=N^2$ exactly; $r_Q(0)\le N$); the substitution identity
$|2a-m|^2=4R-|m|^2$ is checked exactly on every tested configuration; the worst
machine ratio $T'/6d=0.25$ leaves a $\times4$ headroom below the Lemma NT ceiling;
and the falsifier (footer) is concrete and pre-registered (a lattice shell violating
the divisor bound, or a counterexample to the rank-2 representation count).
