\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The two general-position routes to DR-2 (decoupling, dr2-cross-scale-induction
v1.1; and the elementary circle-richness reduction, dr2-circle-richness-reduction
v1.0) leave the arbitrary-$Q$ problem OPEN: the former cites the hard core of
Bourgain--Demeter, and the latter reduces DR-2 to bounding the sum-circle
richness $T'(Q)$, which can be as large as $N$ for adversarial real configurations.
This note observes that the configurations DR-2 must handle IN TECT are not
adversarial: they are lattice points on a sphere (BCC/FCC reciprocal-lattice
momentum shells), and for these $T'(Q)$ is controlled UNCONDITIONALLY by a
classical divisor bound. The result is Route A, self-contained and
decoupling-free.

\section*{2. The lattice reduction}

Let $\Lambda\subset\mathbb R^3$ be a fixed lattice (take $\Lambda=\mathbb Z^3$;
the argument is lattice-independent) and
\[
Q\;=\;\Lambda\cap\{x:|x|^2=R\},\qquad N=|Q|.
\]
Every sum $m=a+b$ with $a,b\in Q$ lies in $\Lambda+\Lambda=\Lambda$ (an integer
vector), and the sum-level circle of Lemma A is
\[
C_m=\{x:|x|^2=R,\ x\cdot m=\tfrac12|m|^2\}.
\]
For $m$ with $r(m)>0$, $\tfrac12|m|^2=R+a\cdot b\in\mathbb Z$, so $C_m$ is the
intersection of the sphere with a RATIONAL plane: a circle whose $\Lambda$-points
form a coset of the rank-$2$ sublattice $\Lambda\cap m^\perp$. Writing $|x|^2$ in
a basis of that sublattice expresses $\#(Q\cap C_m)$ as the number of
representations of a fixed integer by a positive-definite BINARY QUADRATIC FORM
of discriminant $O(R)$.

\textbf{[DIV-CIRC] (classical).} The number of lattice points on a circle of
squared radius $\le R$ -- equivalently the number of representations of an
integer $n\le R$ by a positive-definite binary quadratic form of discriminant
$O(R)$ -- is $O_\epsilon(R^\epsilon)$, uniformly. (For $\Lambda=\mathbb Z^2$ this
is the divisor bound $r_2(n)\ll_\epsilon n^\epsilon$, provable from unique
factorisation in $\mathbb Z[i]$; the binary-form version follows from the
divisor bound for ideal norms in the associated order.)

\section*{3. Theorem (lattice DR-2, conditional on [DIV-CIRC])}

\textbf{Theorem.} For $Q=\Lambda\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$, conditional on [DIV-CIRC],
\[
\boxed{\,E_+(Q)\;\le\;\big(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon\big)N^2\,}.
\]
In particular, for Gauss-typical shells $R\asymp N^{2+o(1)}$ (the regime
$N\gg R^{\delta}$ holds), $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$ -- DR-2 holds
for the lattice class, with NO decoupling.

\textbf{Proof.} By Lemma A (R-025), $E_+(Q)\le(1+T'(Q))N^2$ with
$T'(Q)=\max_{m\neq0}\#(Q\cap C_m)$. Each $C_m$ is a circle on the sphere of
squared radius $R$, so by \S2 and [DIV-CIRC], $\#(Q\cap C_m)\le
\#(\Lambda\cap C_m)\le C_\epsilon R^\epsilon$ uniformly in $m$. Hence
$T'(Q)\le C_\epsilon R^\epsilon$ and the bound follows. For $R\asymp N^2$,
$R^\epsilon\asymp N^{2\epsilon}$, giving $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$.
$\square$

\section*{4. Numerical verification (exact arithmetic)}

dr2\_lattice\_divisor.py (5/5) computes $E_+$ and $T'$ EXACTLY (integers, no
tolerance) on $\mathbb Z^3$ shells $R=101,\dots,9974$ ($N=168,\dots,2040$):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{rrrrr}
$R$ & $N$ & $E_+/N^2$ & $T'$ & $T'/N$ \\ \hline
101 & 168 & 5.27 & 18 & 0.107 \\
909 & 504 & 4.93 & 24 & 0.048 \\
1826 & 672 & 4.10 & 24 & 0.036 \\
4994 & 1152 & 3.93 & 32 & 0.028 \\
9974 & 2040 & 3.92 & 48 & 0.024 \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The lemma assert $E_+\le(1+T')N^2$ holds on every shell (the proof check on real
arithmetic data). $T'$ grows sub-linearly: $T'/N$ falls monotonically
$0.107\to0.024$ and the log--log slope is $0.177$ with the per-point exponent
$\log T'/\log R$ falling $0.63\to0.42$ -- the signature of $R^{o(1)}$. $E_+/N^2$
stays $\le5.3$ (energy is $O(N^2)$, the $R^\epsilon$ ceiling is never approached).
$\mathbb Z^3$ and the FCC sublattice give identical $(N,T',E_+)$ at $R=1826$:
the mechanism is lattice-independent, so it applies to the BCC/FCC momentum
shells of TECT.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3, code-discipline 6)}

Four concrete objections, found before submission:

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Is [DIV-CIRC] really uniform over the varying rational
planes?''} RECORDED as the explicit named hypothesis. The $\mathbb Z^2$ case is
textbook; the binary-form version is uniform because the forms have discriminant
$O(R)$ and the representation count is $\ll_\epsilon(n\cdot\mathrm{disc})^\epsilon
\ll_\epsilon R^\epsilon$. The grade is T6 CONDITIONAL on [DIV-CIRC] precisely so
this classical input is named, not hidden; the operator may elevate to T7.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The bound is $R^\epsilon N^2$, which is $N^{2+\epsilon}$
only when $R\lesssim N^{O(1)}$.''} VALID and scoped: for very empty shells
($N\ll R^{1/2}$) the $R^\epsilon$ factor is not absorbed, but there the absolute
energy is small anyway; the DR-2-relevant (Gauss-typical, dominant) shells satisfy
$R\asymp N^{2+o(1)}$ and are covered. The theorem is stated as $E_+\le(1+C_\epsilon
R^\epsilon)N^2$ to avoid overclaiming the $N^{2+\epsilon}$ form universally.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Does this close DR2-SHARE / discharge H-ADM-COH?''} NOT
asserted. This closes the ADDITIVE-ENERGY form of DR-2 for the lattice class. The
STEP-5B carrier richness $\chi(P)$ and $T'$ are both rich-circle quantities and
are closely related, but their exact equivalence is not established here, so
whether the lattice closure discharges H-ADM-COH is an OPERATOR integration
decision. NO gate/tier/hypothesis flip is made.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``Arbitrary-$Q$ DR-2?''} STILL OPEN. This note covers the
lattice class only; the rich-latitude example (T'=N) shows general $Q$ is not
reached by this route. The decoupling route (T4+) remains the general-case path.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Quantitative sanity check (mandatory).} Exact integer arithmetic (no
tolerance): the lemma assert $E_+\le(1+T')N^2$ holds on all six shells.
Magnitude: $E_+/N^2\in[3.9,5.3]$ (bounded, $\ll N$). Growth: $T'/N\to0$
($0.107\to0.024$), log--log slope $0.177<1$ (sub-polynomial). Limit/consistency:
$\mathbb Z^3=$FCC at $R=1826$ ($N=672,T'=24$). Direction: $T'\ge1$, all terms
positive, upper bound.
