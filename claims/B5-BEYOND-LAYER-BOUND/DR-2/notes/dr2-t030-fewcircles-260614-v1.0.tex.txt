\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The in-bundle reproof attempt dr2-t030-bd-discrete-reproof v1.1 (R-036) split the
discrete decoupling residual as
\[
\text{[BD-DISCRETE]}=\text{[BD-CONTINUOUS]}+\text{[NEST-DEPTH]},
\]
closing the poly-separated subclass ($s\ge N^{-C}$) modulo the textbook continuous
theorem and naming \emph{[NEST-DEPTH]} -- control of the nested-cluster recursion
depth -- as ``the single genuine open piece''. Its witness is the lacunary
configuration $\{2^{-j}:j=0,\dots,J\}$ carried onto a great circle, with minimal
separation $s=2^{-J}$ and recursion depth $J=\log_2(1/s)$, unbounded at fixed $N$;
iterating the one-step decoupling inequality at a fixed scale across $J$ nested
generations costs $D_0^{2J}=\exp(\Theta(J))$.

This note asks the prior question the reproof note did not settle: \textbf{does the
witness witness hardness of the BOUND, or only of the TECHNIQUE?} We show the
latter, via an elementary covering-number bound, and use it to delineate the
genuine open core. \textbf{Not claimed:} any unconditional arbitrary-$Q$ closure;
any tier/gate flip. The track is the H-NONLATTICE-REMAINDER frontier and is
NOT load-bearing for the published $C_{\rm full}$ head (synthesis Sec.~4b).

\section*{2. The per-pair and single-circle bounds (R-021, T7 -- prior)}

We recall, with explicit attribution, the input on which everything below rests.

\begin{lemma}[R-021, sphere-circle cross-energy, T7]
For finite $A,B$ each supported on a circle of $S^2$ (planar section), the
rectangular additive energy satisfies $E_+(A,B):=\sum_m r_{A,B}(m)^2\le 3|A||B|$,
where $r_{A,B}(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in A\times B:a+b=m\}$. It holds for parallel and
non-parallel circles.
\end{lemma}
\emph{Mechanism (R-021).} For fixed $m$, $a+b=m$ with $a\in\Gamma_A$ forces
$a\in\Gamma_A\cap(m-\Gamma_B)$; the reflected-translated set $m-\Gamma_B$ is a
circle, and two \emph{distinct} circles in $\mathbb R^3$ meet in $\le2$ points, so
$r_{A,B}(m)\le2$ except for the at most one $m_0$ with $m-\Gamma_B=\Gamma_A$
(which forces equal radius and parallel planes, fixing $m_0$), where
$r_{A,B}(m_0)\le\min(|A|,|B|)$. Hence $E_+(A,B)\le\min(|A|,|B|)^2+2|A||B|\le3|A||B|$.

\begin{corollary}[single circle, T7 -- prior]
If $Q$ is supported on ONE circle then $E_+(Q)=E_+(Q,Q)\le 3N^2$.
\end{corollary}
This already disposes of the rich-latitude obstruction of R-025 (a latitude with
$T'=N$): one rich circle, however rich, has $E_+\le3N^2$. The aligned term is the
center-reflection sum $m_0=2c$ ($c=$ circle centre): for the great circle $c=0$,
$m_0=0$ and $r(0)=N$ are the antipodal pairs, giving $E_+\le(2N^2-N)+(N^2-N)<3N^2$.
(Machine: a rich latitude $x^2+y^2=1105^2$ at $z=47$, $N=108$, has $E_+=34668$,
$E_+/N^2=2.972$, exactly the published R-033 values F1/F5; assert A1.)

\section*{3. Theorem: the covering-number (few-circles) bound (new assembly)}

\begin{theorem}[few-circles additive-energy bound]
Let $Q$ be a finite set covered by $L$ circles, $Q\subset\bigcup_{i=1}^L\Gamma_i$.
Then
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;3\,L^2\,N^2,\qquad N=|Q|.
\]
\end{theorem}
\emph{Proof.} Fix a partition $Q=\bigsqcup_{i=1}^L Q_i$ with $Q_i\subset\Gamma_i$
(assign each point to one covering circle). For every $m$,
$r(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=m\}=\sum_{i,j=1}^L r_{ij}(m)$ with
$r_{ij}(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q_i\times Q_j:a+b=m\}$, the index pair being unique per
ordered pair $(a,b)$. Cauchy--Schwarz over the $L^2$ index pairs gives
$r(m)^2\le L^2\sum_{i,j}r_{ij}(m)^2$, and summing over $m$,
\[
\begin{aligned}
E_+(Q)=\sum_m r(m)^2
&\;\le\;L^2\sum_{i,j}\sum_m r_{ij}(m)^2
=L^2\sum_{i,j}E_+(Q_i,Q_j)\\
&\;\overset{\text{Lemma R-021}}{\le}\;3L^2\sum_{i,j}|Q_i||Q_j|
=3L^2\Big(\sum_i|Q_i|\Big)^2=3L^2N^2. \quad\square
\end{aligned}
\]

\begin{corollary}[a new unconditionally-closed class]
If $Q\subset S^2$ is coverable by $L\lesssim_\epsilon N^{\epsilon}$ circles then
$E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$ -- DR-2 holds UNCONDITIONALLY for $Q$,
with no decoupling theorem and no circle-incidence bound.
\end{corollary}

The bound is elementary (R-021 $+$ a Cauchy--Schwarz packaging) and is informative
only for $L=N^{o(1)}$: at $L\sim N^{1/2}$ it degrades to $3N^3$. Its role is to add
a covering-number axis to the existing sufficient conditions, complementary to the
richness axis of Lemma~A. Machine: the bound holds on every tested configuration
(asserts A2, A3, A8), including a maximally-aligned same-radius $L=4$ stack
($E_+=1{,}525{,}392=E_+(\text{circle})\cdot E_+(\text{AP}_4)=34668\cdot44$, ratio
$E_+/N^2=8.17\le 48=3\cdot4^2$) and an $L=3$ distinct-radius union (ratio $2.45\le27$).

\section*{4. The [NEST-DEPTH] witness is closed at $L=1$ (technique-only)}

\begin{proposition}[witness benignity]
The [NEST-DEPTH] lacunary witness $\{2^{-j}\}_{j=0}^{J}$ carried onto a great
circle is supported on ONE circle. By the Corollary of Sec.~2, $E_+\le 3N^2$ for
every $J$, \emph{independent} of the recursion depth $J=\log_2(1/s)$.
\end{proposition}

Consequently the $D_0^{2J}=\exp(\Theta(J))$ blow-up that the reproof note
exhibited is a property of the \emph{fixed-scale decoupling iteration}, not of the
additive energy: the witness that motivated [NEST-DEPTH] does not witness any
hardness of the target bound -- it has minimal ($\Theta(N^2)$) energy. Machine
(assert A6): with rational points $t_j=2^{-j}$ on $x^2+y^2=1$, exact integer
energy gives
\[
\begin{array}{lrrr}
J & N & \text{depth}=\log_2(1/s) & E_+/N^2\\\hline
4 & 5 & 3 & 1.800\\
5 & 6 & 4 & 1.833\\
6 & 7 & 5 & 1.857\\
7 & 8 & 6 & 1.875
\end{array}
\]
the depth grows while $E_+/N^2$ stays flat near $2$ ($<3$). \textbf{Reclassification.}
The genuine open content of [NEST-DEPTH] is NOT the lacunary / near-one-circle
family (benign by R-021) but the truly two-dimensional clustered family that
requires \emph{many} circles to cover -- exactly the regime Sec.~5 isolates.

\section*{5. Sharp delineation of the open core}

Collecting the unconditional sufficient conditions for arbitrary finite
$Q\subset S^2$:
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;\min\Big\{\,3L^2N^2\ \text{(this note)},\ (1+T')N^2\ \text{(R-025)},\
C\,N^{9/4}\ \text{(R-033, mod [CIRC-INC])}\,\Big\},
\]
with the lattice class (R-026, $T'\lesssim_\epsilon R^\epsilon$) and the
poly-separated class (R-037, modulo [BD-CONTINUOUS]) closed separately. A family
can therefore violate $E_+\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$ only if it is
SIMULTANEOUSLY (a) covered by no $N^{o(1)}$ circles ($L=N^{\Omega(1)}$),
(b) rich, $T'=N^{\Omega(1)}$, and (c) beyond the reach of the $N^{9/4}$ incidence
count. By R-033 Cor.~1.2 such a family must carry
$\ge N^{2+\delta}/(8k^2\log N)$ simultaneously $k$-rich sum-circles -- a
super-polynomial number of distinct rich circles. The few-circles bound shows
this multiplicity is \emph{necessary}: a bounded (or polynomial) circle cover is
incompatible with $E_+\ge N^{2+\delta}$. The open core of T-030 is thus precisely
the \textbf{super-polynomially-many-simultaneously-rich-circles regime}; the
lacunary/few-circle families that previously appeared as obstructions are not in
it.

delineation. No deep new analysis is claimed; the deliverable is a structural
sharpening plus one new closed sub-class.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The $L^2$ factor is lossy -- at $L\sim N^{1/2}$ the bound
is $3N^3$, vacuous.''} VALID and central to the honest scope: the theorem is
effective ONLY for $L=N^{o(1)}$ and explicitly does NOT touch the many-circle
core, which is named as the residual (Sec.~5). No closure of T-030 is claimed.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Every $Q$ is covered by some circles, so the hypothesis
loses no generality / the class is trivial.''} DISMISSED with scope: every $Q$ is
covered by $L\le N$ circles trivially, but the bound is non-vacuous only for

(latitude unions, bounded-degree-curve sets, the witness), and its complement --
the super-poly-circle regime -- is carried as the open core, not silently
included.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``The witness-benignity secretly uses the great circle's
$m=0$ antipodal structure.''} DISMISSED: $E_+(Q)\le3N^2$ for $Q$ on ANY single
circle is R-021's diagonal, valid for great AND non-great circles; the conclusion
needs only ``one circle''. Verified on a rich non-great latitude ($z=47$,
$E_+/N^2=2.972\le3$; assert A1) as well as the great circle ($z=0$).
\end{itemize}

\emph{Reproducibility.} All counts are exact integer; the driver is deterministic
and exits $0$ iff all $8$ asserts pass.
