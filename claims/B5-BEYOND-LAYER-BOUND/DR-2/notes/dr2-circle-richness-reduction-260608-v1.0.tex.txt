\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The decoupling route (dr2-cross-scale-induction v1.1) reduces unrestricted DR-2
to two cited ingredients of Bourgain--Demeter machinery, of which R2 (the
multi-scale constant control) is the hard core of decoupling itself and is not
reproducible here. This note steps off that route and proves, UNCONDITIONALLY
and elementarily, the part that IS provable: an exact reduction of the sphere
additive energy to a single geometric quantity, the occupancy of sum-level
circles. It yields elementary DR-2 for a natural class and pins the precise
obstruction for the rest.

\section*{2. The sum-level circle}

Let $Q\subset S^2$ be finite, $|Q|=N$, and for $m\in\mathbb R^3$ let
$r(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=m\}$, so that the additive energy is
$E_+(Q)=\sum_m r(m)^2$ and $\sum_m r(m)=N^2$.

\textbf{Geometric fact.} If $a,b\in S^2$ and $a+b=m$ then
\[
a\cdot m=a\cdot(a+b)=|a|^2+a\cdot b=1+a\cdot b,\qquad
|m|^2=|a|^2+2a\cdot b+|b|^2=2+2a\cdot b,
\]
so $a\cdot m=|m|^2/2$, and likewise $b\cdot m=|m|^2/2$. Hence both summands lie
on the \emph{sum-level circle}
\[
C_m:=S^2\cap\{x\in\mathbb R^3:\ x\cdot m=\tfrac12|m|^2\}.
\]
For $m\neq0$ this is a proper circle (a plane at distance $|m|/2>0$ from the
origin meeting $S^2$, nonempty iff $|m|\le2$); for $m=0$ the defining plane
degenerates to all of $\mathbb R^3$ and $C_0=S^2$.

\section*{3. Lemma A (unconditional)}

\textbf{Lemma A.} For every finite $Q\subset S^2$,
\[
\boxed{\,E_+(Q)\;\le\;\big(1+T'(Q)\big)\,N^2\,},\qquad
T'(Q):=\max_{\substack{m\neq0\\ r(m)>0}}\ \#\big(Q\cap C_m\big).
\]

\textbf{Proof.} Split $E_+(Q)=r(0)^2+\sum_{m\neq0}r(m)^2$.

\emph{(i) The $m=0$ term.} $r(0)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=0\}=\#\{a\in Q:-a\in Q\}
\le N$, so $r(0)^2\le N^2$.

\emph{(ii) The proper terms.} Fix $m\neq0$ with $r(m)>0$. Every pair $(a,b)$
with $a+b=m$ has $a\in Q\cap C_m$ and $b=m-a$ determined by $a$, so
$r(m)=\#\{a\in Q\cap C_m:\ m-a\in Q\}\le\#(Q\cap C_m)\le T'(Q)$. Therefore
\[
\sum_{m\neq0}r(m)^2\le T'(Q)\sum_{m\neq0}r(m)\le T'(Q)\,N^2.
\]
Adding (i) and (ii) gives $E_+(Q)\le N^2+T'(Q)N^2=(1+T'(Q))N^2$. $\square$

The split is sharp and necessary: for $N$ evenly spaced points on a great
circle the antipodal term contributes $r(0)^2=N^2$ and the proper circles have
occupancy $T'=2$, so the bound reads $(1+2)N^2=3N^2$ against the exact
$E_+=2.95\,N^2$ (dr2\_circle\_richness.py: $N^2$ antipodal $+\,2N^2$ proper).

\section*{4. Consequences}

\textbf{Corollary 1 (elementary unconditional DR-2 for bounded richness).} If
$T'(Q)=O(1)$ then $E_+(Q)=O(N^2)$ -- DR-2 holds with NO decoupling and NO
conjecture. The script confirms $T'=2$ for both random sphere sets
($E_+=1.98\,N^2$) and the great circle ($E_+=2.95\,N^2$); these natural classes
satisfy DR-2 by Lemma A alone.

\textbf{Corollary 2 (reduction of general DR-2).} For arbitrary finite $Q$,
\[
T'(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^\epsilon\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}\ \ (\text{DR-2}).
\]
Thus DR-2 follows from a bound on a single elementary quantity: the maximum
occupancy of a sum-level circle. This is a concrete realisation of the DR2-SHARE
``carrier richness'' $\chi(P)$ with an EXACT constant ($1+T'$), sharper than the
polylog framing.

\textbf{Scope honesty (the condition is sufficient, not tight).} Corollary 2 is
one-directional. The engineered latitude circle $z=z_0$ has a single rich
sum-circle ($m^\ast=(0,0,2z_0)$, all $N$ points on it, $r(m^\ast)=N$), so
$T'=N$, yet $E_+\approx3N^2$ still satisfies DR-2. Hence bounding $T'$ is
potentially STRONGER than DR-2, and Lemma A does not by itself make the general
problem easier; it isolates rich sum-circles as the only mechanism by which
$E_+$ can exceed $O(N^2)$ and supplies the unconditional bound for the rest.

Four concrete objections, found before submission:

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Does this close DR-2 or advance its tier?''} NO, and
the note must not be read that way. Lemma A and Corollary 1 are T7 UNCONDITIONAL
but cover only the bounded-richness class; the unrestricted DR-2 tier is
UNCHANGED (T4+ via the decoupling route). No gate/tier/hypothesis flip.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The $m=0$ circle is all of $S^2$, so $T'$ would be $N$
always, making the bound trivial $N^3$.''} DISMISSED: $m=0$ is excluded from the
$\max$ defining $T'$ and handled separately in step (i), where
$r(0)\le N$ gives the additive ``$+1$''. The great-circle check (tight at $3N^2$)
confirms the split is correct, not a loophole.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``$T'$ can be $N$ while $E_+$ is $O(N^2)$ (rich
latitude), so the reduction is vacuous.''} VALID and RECORDED as the scope-honesty
paragraph: Corollary 2 is sufficient, not tight. Its value is (a) the
unconditional bound for $T'=O(1)$ sets and (b) pinning rich sum-circles as the
sole obstruction -- not a claim that $T'\lesssim N^\epsilon$ is equivalent to
DR-2.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``Is this just the known carrier-richness reduction
re-labelled?''} PARTIALLY: it makes the carrier-richness concrete (the precise
quantity $T'$, exact constant $1+T'$, via the sum-circle/involution identity)
and proves the easy direction rigorously and unconditionally, whereas the prior
PSM reduction (dr2-pencil-rigidity-reduction v1.0) was a T3 sketch. Whether
$T'\lesssim N^\epsilon$ is equivalent to the PSM conjecture is NOT asserted here.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Quantitative sanity check (mandatory).} Estimator exactness: random
$E_+=2N^2-N$ verified for $N=16,32,64$. Lemma assert: $E_+\le(1+T')N^2$ holds
for all four configs (the proof check; a violation would refute Lemma A).
Limit/tightness: great circle saturates at $3N^2=(1+2)N^2$ (the $m=0$ split is
