\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

T-030 is the arbitrary-$Q$ (non-lattice) DR-2 problem: for every finite
antipodal $Q\subset S^2$ with $|Q|=N$, is the additive energy
\[
E_+(Q):=\#\{(a,b,c,d)\in Q^4:\ a+b=c+d\}
\]
bounded by $N^{2+\epsilon}$ for every $\epsilon>0$? This is the content of
B5's hypothesis H-NONLATTICE-REMAINDER-EXCLUDED -- the last remaining B5 T7
blocker after the 2026-06-12 operator verdicts (D1-A/D2-A/D3-A). Boundary
kept explicit throughout (main-line-synthesis v1.3 Sec.~4b): this track is
NOT load-bearing for the published $C_{\rm full}$ head theorem, whose Lemma~2
caps $T'\le\lfloor2\pi/\theta_{\min}\rfloor=10$ elementarily in-class;
T-030 is a frontier strengthening. This note consolidates the frontier
(Sec.~2), proves one new unconditional-modulo-classical intermediate exponent
(Sec.~3), discharges the R1 residual of the conditional chain by a written
proof (Sec.~4), and records the pre-registered machine experiments including
one honestly-fired gate (Sec.~5). No gate or tier action is taken.

\section*{2. Statement map of the frontier (consolidation)}

\subsection*{2.1 The exact open statement}

\textbf{Open (T-030).} For every finite antipodal $Q\subset S^2$, $|Q|=N$:
$E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{2+\epsilon}$. (Antipodality plays no role in any
partial result below; everything stated holds for arbitrary finite $Q$.)

\subsection*{2.2 What is proved, at what grade (pre-this-note)}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Lemma A (R-025), T7 unconditional}: $E_+(Q)\le(1+T'(Q))N^2$,
where $T'(Q)$ is the maximum occupancy of a proper sum-level circle
$C_m=S^2\cap\{x\cdot m=\tfrac12|m|^2\}$, $m\neq0$. Hence $T'=O(1)$ classes
(random, great circle) satisfy DR-2 elementarily.
\item \textbf{Lattice class (R-026 + Lemma NT), T7-NTstandard}: for
$Q\subset\mathbb Z^3\cap\{|x|^2=R\}$, $T'\lesssim_\epsilon R^\epsilon$
(divisor + Dirichlet class number), so $E_+\le(1+C_\epsilon R^\epsilon)N^2$.
SOLVED; this is what discharged H-ADM-COH for the lattice class.
\item \textbf{Separated $Q$, T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on Bourgain--Demeter}
($d=3$, $p=4$): via the Besicovitch mean $M(|f_Q|^4)=E_+(Q)$ and the
Schwartz-majorant bridge (dr2-decoupling-closure v1.1).
\item \textbf{Arbitrary $Q$, conditional chain, T4+/T5-candidate}: the
cross-scale induction (dr2-cross-scale-induction v1.1) reduces arbitrary
finite $Q$ to the separated base via affine rescaling + decoupling
stability, with TWO cited-not-reproduced residuals: R1 (local-to-global
translate-averaging) and R2 (multi-scale $\epsilon$-bookkeeping).
\item \textbf{PSM route}: Pencil Rigidity / Pair-Sum Surface Multiplicity
conjecture T2 (falsification gate $E_+(Q_N)\ge N^{2+\delta}$); reduction
PSM $\Rightarrow$ DR-2 at T3 with OPEN GAPS $\alpha$ (decomposition
existence) and $\beta$ (per-cluster uniformity). Cross-energy lemma R-021
(T7): $E_{\rm off}(A,B)\le2\min(|A|^2-|A|,|B|^2-|B|)$ for circle subsets.
\item \textbf{Affine invariance (R-023), T7}: $E_+$ is exactly
affine-invariant; cap clustering cannot beat $N^2$ by rescaling alone.
\item \textbf{Unconditional record for arbitrary $Q$ (pre-this-note)}: the
trivial/triple-count $N^{5/2}$ class. Lemma A gives only $(1+T')N^2\le N^3$
in general.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{2.3 Why the rich latitude blocks the naive $T'$ route}

The engineered latitude circle ($N$ points on $z=z_0$) has ONE maximally
rich sum-circle: $m^\ast=(0,0,2z_0)$ with $C_{m^\ast}$ equal to the latitude
itself, so $T'=N$ -- yet $E_+\approx3N^2$ (machine-exact: $2.972N^2$ at
$N=108$, Sec.~5). So $T'\lesssim_\epsilon N^\epsilon$ is SUFFICIENT for DR-2
(Cor.~2 of R-025) but NOT necessary, and no bound on $T'$ alone can close
T-030. The missing ingredient is visible in the witness: what matters is not
how rich the richest circle is, but HOW MANY simultaneously rich circles can
coexist. That is an incidence question, and Sec.~3 exploits exactly this.

\subsection*{2.4 What an unconditional proof must overcome}

By the structure corollary of Sec.~3 (Cor.~1.2), any family with
$E_+\ge N^{2+\delta}$ must contain $\gtrsim N^{2+\delta}/(k^2\log N)$
simultaneously $k$-rich sum-circles at some single dyadic richness
$k\in[c_1N^{\delta},\,c_2N^{(1-\delta)/3}]$. Classical incidence bounds cap
what this regime can do at $\delta=1/4$ (Pach--Sharir) or $2/9+\epsilon$
(Aronov--Sharir); pushing $\delta\to0$ unconditionally would need
near-optimal circle-incidence technology (the regime adjacent to the hard
unit-distance-type problems), while the conditional Bourgain--Demeter route
bypasses incidences entirely at the price of the R2 bookkeeping.

\section*{3. Theorem 1: the unconditional $N^{9/4}$ bound (new)}

\subsection*{3.1 The incidence input}

\textbf{[CIRC-INC] (classical; Clarkson--Edelsbrunner--Guibas--Sharir--Welzl
1990; Pach--Sharir curves with 3 degrees of freedom).} There is an absolute
constant $A$ such that for any $N$ points and any $n$ DISTINCT circles in the
plane, the number of point-circle incidences satisfies
\[
I\;\le\;A\big(N^{3/5}n^{4/5}+N+n\big).
\]
Transfer to $S^2$: stereographic projection from any $p\in S^2$ avoiding the
finitely many circles and points (such $p$ exists; finitely many circles have
measure zero) maps the configuration bijectively to points and distinct
circles in the plane, preserving incidences. So [CIRC-INC] holds verbatim for
circles on the sphere.

\subsection*{3.2 Setup and the dyadic decomposition}

As in R-025: $r(m)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=m\}$, $E_+(Q)=\sum_m r(m)^2$,
$\sum_m r(m)=N^2$. For $m\neq0$ both summands of $a+b=m$ lie on the
sum-level circle $C_m$, $r(m)\le t_m:=\#(Q\cap C_m)$, and the map
$m\mapsto C_m$ is INJECTIVE on $\{0<|m|\le2\}$: the plane
$x\cdot m=\tfrac12|m|^2$ has unit normal $m/|m|$ and distance $|m|/2>0$ from
the origin; a circle on $S^2$ determines its plane, and the pair (normal,
distance$\,>0$) determines $m=2d\,u$. Distinct $m$ therefore give distinct
circles, as [CIRC-INC] requires.

Let $n_k:=\#\{m\neq0:\ r(m)\ge1,\ t_m\ge k\}$ (a finite family of distinct
$k$-rich circles). Dyadic classes: $D_j=\{m\neq0:\ r(m)\ge1,\ 2^j\le
t_m<2^{j+1}\}$, $j\ge0$.

\textbf{Per-class bounds.} For $k=2^j$, $j\ge1$:
\[
\text{(i)}\ \sum_{m\in D_j}r(m)^2\le 2k\!\!\sum_{m\in D_j}\!\!r(m)\le 2kN^2,
\qquad
\text{(ii)}\ \sum_{m\in D_j}r(m)^2\le 4k^2|D_j|\le 4k^2\,n_k .
\]
The $j=0$ class has $r(m)=t_m=1$, contributing $\le N^2$; the $m=0$ term
contributes $r(0)^2\le N^2$.

\textbf{Richness count from [CIRC-INC].} Each of the $n_k$ circles carries
$\ge k$ incidences, so $k\,n_k\le I\le A(N^{3/5}n_k^{4/5}+N+n_k)$. For
$k\ge2A$ the $An_k$ term is absorbed ($An_k\le\tfrac k2 n_k$) and one of the
two remaining terms dominates, giving
\[
n_k\;\le\;(4A)^5\,\frac{N^3}{k^5}\;+\;4A\,\frac{N}{k}
\qquad(k\ge2A).
\]

\subsection*{3.3 Theorem 1 and proof}

\textbf{Theorem 1.} There is an absolute constant $C$ (explicit in the
[CIRC-INC] constant $A$) such that for EVERY finite $Q\subset S^2$,
\[
\boxed{\,E_+(Q)\;\le\;C\,N^{9/4}\,}.
\]

\textbf{Proof.} Split the dyadic sum at $k^\ast=N^{1/4}$:
\[
E_+(Q)\;\le\;2N^2+\sum_{1\le 2^j\le k^\ast}\ \sum_{m\in D_j}r(m)^2
\;+\;\sum_{2^j>k^\ast}\ \sum_{m\in D_j}r(m)^2 .
\]
Small classes by (i): $\sum_{2^j\le k^\ast}2\cdot2^jN^2\le4k^\ast N^2
=4N^{9/4}$. Large classes by (ii) and the richness count (the finitely many
dyadic $k$ with $k^\ast<k<2A$, possible only when $N^{1/4}<2A$, contribute
$\le2(2A)\cdot2AN^2=O_A(N^2)$ by (i)):
\[
\sum_{2^j>k^\ast}4k^2n_k
\;\le\;\sum_{2^j>k^\ast}\Big(4(4A)^5\frac{N^3}{k^3}+16A\,kN\Big)
\;\le\;\frac{32}{7}(4A)^5\,\frac{N^3}{(k^\ast)^3}+32A\,T'N
\;\le\;C'\,N^{9/4}+32A\,N^2,
\]
using $\sum_{k>k^\ast\,\rm dyadic}k^{-3}\le\tfrac87(k^\ast)^{-3}$, $T'\le N$,
and $N^3/(k^\ast)^3=N^{9/4}$. Summing, $E_+(Q)\le CN^{9/4}$. $\square$

\textbf{Remark 1.1 (Aronov--Sharir refinement).} Replacing [CIRC-INC] by the
Aronov--Sharir circle bound
$I\lesssim N^{2/3}n^{2/3}+N^{6/11}n^{9/11}\log^{2/11}(N^3/n)+N+n$ gives
$n_k\lesssim N^2/k^3+N^3k^{-11/2}\log^{O(1)}+N/k$ and, balancing at
$k^\ast=N^{2/9}$, the exponent $E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon N^{20/9+\epsilon}$.
The $9/4$ statement is kept as the headline because its input is the cleaner
classical bound; both are strictly below the prior $N^{5/2}$ record.

\textbf{Corollary 1.2 (structure of potential extremizers).} If
$E_+(Q)\ge N^{2+\delta}$ for some $\delta>0$ and $N$ large, then some single
dyadic class contributes $\ge N^{2+\delta}/(2\log_2N)$, and by (i), (ii) +
[CIRC-INC] its richness scale $k$ obeys
\[
c_1\,\frac{N^{\delta}}{\log N}\;\le\;k\;\le\;c_2\,N^{(1-\delta)/3}\log^{1/3}N,
\qquad
n_k\;\ge\;\frac{N^{2+\delta}}{8k^2\log_2 N},
\]
forcing $\delta<1/4$ (consistency of the window) and a SUPERLINEAR-in-$N$
number $n_k\ge N^{2+\delta}/(8k^2\log N)\ge N^{4/3+O(\delta)}$ of
simultaneously $k$-rich sum-circles. The rich latitude (ONE rich circle) and
all tested families are nowhere near this regime (Sec.~5).

\textbf{Honest grade.} Theorem 1 is PROVED MODULO [CIRC-INC]: every step
above is self-contained EXCEPT the incidence input, which is classical
published combinatorial geometry (CEGSW 1990 / Pach--Sharir), invoked the
way R-026 invokes textbook number theory (T7-NTstandard precedent). Whether
the repository registers this as the same modulo-classical grade is an
OPERATOR decision; no enactment here. Within the repo this improves the
unconditional arbitrary-$Q$ record from the $N^{5/2}$ class to $N^{9/4}$
($\delta=1/4$); the statement itself is assembled from standard tools and no
novelty beyond the repository record is claimed.

\section*{4. Lemma R1: the local-to-global bridge, now a written proof}

dr2-cross-scale-induction v1.1 rested on two CITED residuals; R1 was the
decoupling-compatible passage from the ball inequality to the
Besicovitch-mean energy inequality. R1 is in fact provable outright for
finite $Q$, because $|f_Q|^4$ is a trigonometric polynomial. We record the
proof.

\textbf{Lemma R1 (translate-averaged decoupling $\Rightarrow$ energy
inequality).} Let $Q\subset S^2$ be finite, $\{\theta\}$ a finite partition
of $S^2$ into caps, $Q_\theta=Q\cap\theta$, and
$f_P(x)=\sum_{q\in P}e(q\cdot x)$. Suppose the LOCAL decoupling inequality
holds: for some ball radius $R_0$, weight $0\le w\in\mathcal S(\mathbb R^3)$
with $\int w>0$ and $\widehat w$ absolutely summable over the (finite)
relevant frequency set, and constant $D$,
\[
\|f_Q\|_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}\;\le\;D\Big(\sum_\theta
\|f_{Q_\theta}\|_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}^2\Big)^{1/2}
\qquad\text{for every translate }y\in\mathbb R^3 .
\tag{4.1}
\]
Then
\[
\boxed{\,E_+(Q)\;\le\;D^4\Big(\sum_\theta E_+(Q_\theta)^{1/2}\Big)^{2}\,}.
\]

\textbf{Step 0 (one inequality suffices for all translates).} If (4.1) is
assumed only at $y=0$ (the usual statement on $B_{R_0}$), it holds for every
$y$ with the SAME $D$: substituting $g_P(x)=f_P(x+y)=\sum_q e(q\cdot y)
e(q\cdot x)$ preserves the frequency support and cap decomposition
(unimodular coefficients; decoupling constants are insensitive to them), and
$\|f_P\|_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}=\|g_P\|_{L^4(w)}$.

\textbf{Step 1 (exact translate-averaging identity).} $|f_Q(x)|^4=\sum_v
c_v\,e(v\cdot x)$ is a FINITE sum with $c_v=\#\{(a,b,c,d)\in Q^4:
a+b-c-d=v\}\ge0$ and $c_0=E_+(Q)$. For $S>0$, by Fubini (all sums finite),
\[
\mathrm{avg}_S:=\frac{1}{|B_S|}\int_{B_S}\|f_Q\|_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}^4\,dy
=\sum_v c_v\,\widehat w(v)\,\beta_S(v),
\qquad
\beta_S(v):=\frac{1}{|B_S|}\int_{B_S}e(v\cdot y)\,dy,
\]
with $\beta_S(0)=1$ and $|\beta_S(v)|\le3/(2\pi|v|S)^2\cdot(1+o(1))\to0$ for
$v\neq0$ (explicitly $\beta_S(v)=3(\sin u-u\cos u)/u^3$, $u=2\pi|v|S$).
Hence
\[
\lim_{S\to\infty}\mathrm{avg}_S=\widehat w(0)\,c_0=\Big(\int
w\Big)\,E_+(Q),
\]
and identically, for each cap,
$\lim_S\frac{1}{|B_S|}\int_{B_S}\|f_{Q_\theta}\|^4_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}dy
=(\int w)E_+(Q_\theta)$. (Machine check: dr2\_t030\_r1\_bridge.py verifies
$c_0=E_+$ as two independent exact integer counts and the convergence with
the predicted $S^{-2}$ envelope, final relative error $\le2.4\times10^{-5}$
at $S=80$ on both test configurations.)

\textbf{Step 2 (Minkowski).} Set
$A_\theta(y)=\|f_{Q_\theta}\|^2_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}$. Raising (4.1) to the
4th power and averaging over $y\in B_S$,
\[
\mathrm{avg}_S\;\le\;D^4\,\Big\|\sum_\theta A_\theta\Big\|_{L^2(\mu_S)}^2
\;\le\;D^4\Big(\sum_\theta\|A_\theta\|_{L^2(\mu_S)}\Big)^{2},
\qquad \mu_S=\tfrac{dy|_{B_S}}{|B_S|},
\]
by the triangle inequality in $L^2(\mu_S)$ applied to the FINITE sum over
caps. And $\|A_\theta\|_{L^2(\mu_S)}^2=\frac1{|B_S|}\int_{B_S}
\|f_{Q_\theta}\|^4_{L^4(w(\cdot-y))}dy$.

\textbf{Step 3 (limit).} Let $S\to\infty$ in Step 2: the left side tends to
$(\int w)E_+(Q)$ by Step 1; on the right, each of the finitely many cap terms
tends to $[(\int w)E_+(Q_\theta)]^{1/2}$. Dividing by $\int w>0$ gives the
boxed inequality. $\square$

\textbf{Consequence for the conditional chain.} In
dr2-cross-scale-induction v1.1, the iteration inequality
$E_+(Q)\lesssim_\epsilon\delta^{-\epsilon}(\sum_\theta
E_+(Q_\theta)^{1/2})^2$ now follows from the Bourgain--Demeter ball
inequality alone via Lemma R1 -- the local-to-global passage is NO LONGER a
cited residual. The chain for arbitrary $Q$ is now conditional on exactly:
(a) the Bourgain--Demeter ball decoupling black box (with its $C^2$-stability
for the rescaled caps, as already recorded), and (b) residual R2 (multi-scale
$\epsilon$-bookkeeping over $O(\log N)$ levels). Whether \{black box + R2\}
warrants moving the chain from T4+/T5-candidate is an OPERATOR decision; the
factual change here is the residual set shrinking $\{R1,R2\}\to\{R2\}$.

\textbf{PSM gaps (status note).} The PSM route's gaps $\alpha$
(decomposition existence) and $\beta$ (per-cluster uniformity) are untouched
by this note; the adversarial scan of Sec.~5 doubles as the PSM falsification
gate scan and found NO candidate family (all scaling exponents $\approx2$).
Theorem 1 supersedes the PSM route as the best unconditional statement: PSM
would give $N^2\log^BN$ but is T2, while $N^{9/4}$ is proved modulo
[CIRC-INC] only.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3,
code-discipline 6)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{Theorem 1's [CIRC-INC] is cited, not reproduced --
the same defect class as citing decoupling; why grade it differently?}
VALID with mitigation: it IS a citation, and the note says so in the grade
line itself (PROVED MODULO [CIRC-INC]). The mitigation is the precedent
class: CEGSW/Pach--Sharir is classical 1990s combinatorial geometry of the
same standing as the textbook number theory behind R-026's T7-NTstandard,
whereas Bourgain--Demeter is modern heavy machinery whose internal
bookkeeping (R2) is precisely what the conditional chain cannot reproduce.
The operator decides whether the repository accepts the analogy; nothing is
enacted here.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{The incidence bound needs DISTINCT circles; different

$|m|/2>0$; a circle determines its plane, and (normal, positive distance)
determines $m=2du$ -- the map $m\mapsto C_m$ is injective on $0<|m|\le2$
(Sec.~3.2). The only degenerate case $m=0$ is split off separately, exactly
as in Lemma A.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{Lemma R1 Step 0 assumes the decoupling constant is
uniform over translates.} DISMISSED: translation $x\mapsto x+y$ multiplies
each frequency atom by the unimodular constant $e(q\cdot y)$, preserving
frequency supports and all cap norms; (4.1) at $y=0$ therefore transfers to
every translate with the same $D$. No new hypothesis enters.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{Step 2/3 order of limits: Minkowski at finite $S$,
then $S\to\infty$ -- any illegal interchange?} DISMISSED: at each finite $S$
the inequality involves a FINITE sum over caps in $L^2$ of a probability
measure; the $S\to\infty$ limit is then taken of both sides, each cap term
converging by the Step-1 identity (finite Fourier sums; no dominated
convergence or infinite-sum interchange is invoked anywhere).
\item[($\epsilon$)] \emph{Gate G5 fired -- does the no-counterexample claim
survive?} VALID with mitigation, fully recorded in Sec.~5: the registered
proxy fired, the registered consequence (report + investigate) was executed,
and the scaling test (exponent $2.0057$) resolves it as a constant-factor
artifact. The claim made is exactly what the data supports: no tested family
shows super-quadratic GROWTH.
