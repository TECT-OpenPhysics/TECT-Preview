\section*{2. The convention is forced, not chosen}

Fix the Fourier convention
\[
\widehat f(k)=\int_{\mathbb R^3} e^{-ik\cdot x}f(x)\,dx,\qquad
f(x)=\int_{\mathbb R^3}\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\,e^{ik\cdot x}\widehat f(k).
\]
The quartic shell-integral is the 3D convolution of the bubble $J$ with itself,
\[
\widehat{G^4}(t)=(J*J)(t)=\int_{\mathbb R^3} J(s)\,J(t-s)\,\frac{d^3s}{(2\pi)^3}.
\]
In spherical coordinates $s=(q,\theta,\phi)$, $d^3s=q^2\,dq\,d\mu\,d\phi$ ($\mu=\cos\theta$),
and $J$ depends only on $|s|$, so the azimuthal integral contributes a factor $2\pi$:
\[
\boxed{\ \widehat{G^4}(t)=\frac{2\pi}{(2\pi)^3}\int_0^\infty\! q^2\,dq\!\int_{-1}^{1}\! d\mu\,
J(q)\,J(\mathrm{arg})=\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\int q^2 dq\int d\mu\,J(q)J(\mathrm{arg})\ }
\]
with $\mathrm{arg}=\sqrt{q^2+t^2-2qt\mu}$. The $1/(4\pi^2)$ prefactor is FORCED by the
$(2\pi)^3$ measure; it is not a free factor-$2$.

\textbf{Parseval (consequence).} At $t=0$, $\mathrm{arg}=q$ and $\int_{-1}^1 d\mu=2$, so
\[
\widehat{G^4}(0)=\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\cdot2\int q^2 J^2 dq=\frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int q^2 J^2 dq
=\int J^2\,\frac{d^3s}{(2\pi)^3},
\]
matching the standard radial Parseval -- ratio $1.0000$, a check of the derivation, not
its basis. A reimplementation with a $1/(2\pi^2)$ convolution prefactor omits the
$\phi$-normalisation, doubles $\widehat{G^4}$, and yields the spurious $R_{\max}=0.77>0.634$.
The factor-$2$ is exactly the omitted $\phi=2\pi$ over $(2\pi)^3$.

\section*{3. $R_{\max}<0.634$ (interval-enclosed)}

With the convention derived, $R(t)=12(5v/2)^2\lambda'^{-2}\widehat{G^4}(t)\,4(1-a_0)/J(t)$.
Over the physical transfer range $[t_{\min},2q_0]=[0.420,1.360]$, a $41$-node grid gives
$\max_j R(t_j)=0.3846$ (at $t=2q_0$) with maximum slope $L\le0.29$; the FULL-spacing
interval enclosure (conservative)
\[
R_{\max}\ \le\ \max_j R(t_j)+L\,\Delta t\ =\ 0.3846+0.0068\ =\ 0.391\ <\ 0.634
\]
(margin $\times1.62$). The full-spacing form bounds $R$ on every subinterval by the larger
endpoint plus the maximal variation; a tighter half-spacing or a CAS interval-arithmetic
enclosure would only improve the margin.

\section*{4. Anchoring by monotonicity}

$u_{\rm eff}(\mu^2)=u+10v\,M_R(\mu^2)$, $M_R(\mu^2)=\tfrac{1}{2\pi^2}\int q^2/(r_R(\mu^2)
+c(q^2-q_0^2)^2)\,dq$. Since $r_R(\mu^2)$ increases with $\mu^2$ (the gap map) and $M_R$ is
strictly decreasing in $r_R$, $M_R(\mu^2)$ is MONOTONE DECREASING ($dM_R/d\mu^2<0$, verified
$\approx-0.07$ across the band). Hence $u_{\rm eff}$ is monotone, and its interval minimum on
$[\times0.5,\times2]$ is at the largest $\mu^2$: $u_{\rm eff}(\times2)=2.674>0$. The
monotonicity certifies the entire band from the endpoints.
