\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This referee document concerns the SC-SCOPE programme: the second- and third-cumulant scope of the selection floor and its endpoint closure (sunset and quartic-difference channels). \textbf{Scope:} the all-orders endpoint at $I=2\times10^{-3}$, with sign-stability certified only on the explicit window $\mathcal W_{\rm SC}$ of Sec.~3. \textbf{Not claimed:} the closure is thin (sunset-limited, near-critical); it is a near-critical selection boundary, not a comfortable margin. This package does not by itself close full STEP-5B, does not close admissible-class exhaustiveness, and does not enact the full Reading-H $C_{\rm full}$ theorem.

\section*{2. The result}

The SC-SCOPE third-cumulant joint inequality is positive at the endpoint: each channel (sunset cap $\times1.13$, realized quartic $R_{\max}=0.385<0.634$ with the Parseval-pinned $1/(4\pi^2)$ convolution measure, ratio $1.0000$) and their joint with the second-order cost satisfy $\mathrm{joint}>1$ (conservative $\times1.040$, verified-floor $\times1.082$). The endpoint sits just below the structural sunset cap; the selection floor closes (thin).

\section*{3. Structure of the argument}

\paragraph{Joint assembly (patch v1.1-2).} Under the canonical CONSERVATIVE additive
bookkeeping (scscope-floor-sharpening v1.4 corrected form; NOT the retracted pairing
formula), with $\Delta F_{\rm margin} = \mathrm{MARGIN} = 4.32\times10^{-3}$ (single
upstream source \texttt{sectorb\_common.margin\_of}, $\mu^2 = 5\times10^{-3}$),
\[
\mathrm{joint}
=\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}}{C_2 + C_{\rm sunset} + C_{\rm quartic}},
\qquad
C_2=\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}}{\rho_{\rm lat}},
\]
\[
C_{\rm sunset}=\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}\,(1-1/\rho_{\rm lat})}{S},
\qquad
C_{\rm quartic}=R_{\max}\,C_2,
\]
equivalently $\mathrm{joint} = \bigl[(1+R_{\max})/\rho_{\rm lat} + (1-1/\rho_{\rm lat})/S\bigr]^{-1}$,
with $S = 1.13$ the rigorous sunset cap (M-ENDPOINT $M(0.33675)=0.10495$) and
$\rho_{\rm lat}$ the STEP-5B layer-floor ratio: conservative
$\rho_{\rm lat} = K_{\rm budget}/K_{\rm floor} = 266.7/40.7 = 6.55$
($K_{\rm floor}\le T'\le n_{\rm pack}$), verified $\rho_{\rm lat}=12.6$
($K_{\rm floor}\le 0.52\,T'$). Evaluating,
\[
\boxed{\ \mathrm{joint} = \times1.040\ (\rho_{\rm lat}=6.55)\ \dots\ \times1.082\ (\rho_{\rm lat}=12.6)\ >\ 1.\ }
\]

\paragraph{Provenance of the $0.634$ threshold (patch v1.1-3).} Setting
$\mathrm{joint}=1$ at the conservative floor gives the critical quartic ratio
\[
R_{\max}^{\rm crit}
=\rho_{\rm lat}\Bigl(1-\frac{1-1/\rho_{\rm lat}}{S}\Bigr)-1
=6.55\Bigl(1-\frac{1-1/6.55}{1.13}\Bigr)-1
= 0.639 .
\]
The registered closure gate $0.634$ (first registered in scscope-floor-sharpening
v1.4 \S5b, machine-checked by \texttt{scscope\_joint\_correction.py} 5/5) rounds this
critical value DOWN, i.e.\ the registered gate is strictly TIGHTER than the algebraic
critical value, so $R_{\max}<0.634$ implies closure with safety in the conservative
direction. The certified $R_{\max}=0.385$ clears the gate with $\times1.65$ headroom;
the closure survives a $+50\%$ normalisation slack ($0.577<0.634$) but not a full
factor $2$ ($0.769>0.634$) --- which is why the Parseval pin (ratio $1.0000$,
derived from the $(2\pi)^3$ convolution measure) is load-bearing and is itself
machine-certified.

\paragraph{Certified sign-stability window (patch v1.1-4).} The v1.0 phrase
``sign-stable across nearby $(I,\mu^2)$'' is replaced by the explicit certified window
\[
\mathcal W_{\rm SC}
=\Bigl\{(I,\mu^2):\
I\in[4\times10^{-4},\,2\times10^{-3}],\ \
\mu^2\in[0.5,\,2]\times\mu_0^2,\ \mu_0^2=5\times10^{-3}\Bigr\},
\]
on which $\mathrm{joint}>1$ holds throughout (evidence:
\texttt{scscope\_endpoint\_sweep.py} 4/4, run artefact
\texttt{runs/260609-scscope-endpoint-sweep/}): the $I$-sweep gives
$\times1.126\,(4\times10^{-4}) \to \times1.104\,(10^{-3}) \to \times1.040\,(2\times10^{-3})$
(the certified endpoint is the thinnest point of the band and is positively closed;
the critical boundary $\mathrm{joint}=1$ sits at $I\approx2.5\times10^{-3}$, OUTSIDE
$\mathcal W_{\rm SC}$); the $\mu^2$-band sweep at the endpoint stays
$\times1.034$--$\times1.051>1$, consistent with ROBUSTNESS-MU2. No sign-stability
claim is made outside $\mathcal W_{\rm SC}$. The thinness is structural:
$\mathrm{joint}(\rho)$ saturates at the sunset cap $\times1.13$ as
$\rho\to\infty$, so no floor sharpening can exceed it.
