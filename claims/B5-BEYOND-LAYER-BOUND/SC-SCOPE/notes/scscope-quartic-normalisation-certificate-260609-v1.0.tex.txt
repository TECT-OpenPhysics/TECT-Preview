\section*{2. The convention is pinned (Parseval)}

The quartic-difference contribution uses $\widehat{G^4}=G*G*G*G=(J*J)(t)$
(quartic-difference-channel v1.0, exact by associativity, $J=G*G$). The only
open question is the absolute normalisation of the convolution. By Parseval, with
the standard Fourier convention,
\[
\widehat{G^4}(0)=(J*J)(0)=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^3}\int J(q)^2\,d^3q
=\frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty q^2J(q)^2\,dq .
\]
Computing the left side by the convolution routine and the right side directly
(scscope\_quartic\_certificate.py),
\[
\boxed{\ \frac{(J*J)(0)_{\rm conv}}{(2\pi^2)^{-1}\!\int q^2J^2}=1.0000\ }
\]
to four digits. The convolution is therefore STANDARD-normalised; the
factor-$2/(2\pi)^3$ caveat is resolved. As a cross-check the Young ceiling holds,
$(J*J)(0)=1.27\times10^{-3}\le J(0)\|J\|_1=4.76\times10^{-3}$ (ratio $0.27$),
confirming no factor-2 over-count.
