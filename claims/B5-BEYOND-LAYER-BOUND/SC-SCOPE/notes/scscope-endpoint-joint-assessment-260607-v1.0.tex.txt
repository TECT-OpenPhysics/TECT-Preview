\section*{2. GHAT4-PERTRANSFER: the per-transfer quartic-difference form factor}

The quartic-difference channel inflates the certified second-order budget by
$R(t) = 12\,(5v/2)^2\lambda'^{-2}\,\widehat{G^4}(t)\,4(1-a_0)/J(t)$, with
$\widehat{G^4} = G\!*\!G\!*\!G\!*\!G = J\!*\!J$. The sup-kernel estimate paired
the Young ceiling $\widehat{G^4}\le J(0)M^2$ with the floor $J(t)\ge J(2q_0)$
SIMULTANEOUSLY, giving $R_{\rm sup}\approx1.59$ ($\Rightarrow$ inflation
$\times2.6$, endpoint $\times1.0$ marginal). That pairing is incompatible. The
convention-free shape factor
\[
\Phi(t) = \frac{\widehat{G^4}(t)}{\widehat{G^4}(0)}\cdot\frac{J(0)}{J(t)}
\]
(all measure prefactors and the $J$-normalisation factor-2 cancel) was evaluated
on the realized chords $t\in[t_{\min}, 2q_0]$ with $\widehat{G^4}(t)=(J\!*\!J)(t)$
computed by a converged convolution quadrature (grid-stable to $0.00\%$). The
maximum is at the antipodal transfer $t = 2q_0$:
\[
\max_t \Phi(t) = 1.77 \;\ll\; \Phi_{\rm sup} = J(0)/J(2q_0) = 2.77,
\qquad \text{reduction} = \frac{\max_t\Phi}{\Phi_{\rm sup}} = 0.64 .
\]
The incompatible pairing is confirmed (the realized ratio is well below the sup),
but the reduction is only $0.64$: $\widehat{G^4}$ is broad, falling only to
$0.64\,\widehat{G^4}(0)$ at $t=2q_0$. Hence the firmed per-transfer ratio is
\[
R_{\max} = R_{\rm sup}\cdot 0.64 \approx 1.02 ,
\]
which is $\ge 1$ -- precisely the condition the quartic-difference note
pre-registered as triggering the joint endpoint re-derivation. Taken ALONE the
quartic-difference endpoint is $\times2.6/(1+R_{\max}) = \times1.29 > 1$;
$R_{\rm sup}$ is the cited convention-bearing estimate (not recomputed here).

\section*{3. The joint endpoint inequality (the honest negative)}

Each third-order channel clears the endpoint margin INDIVIDUALLY -- second-order
$\times2.60$, sunset $\times1.13$ (dressed, M-ENDPOINT), quartic-difference
$\times1.29$ (per-transfer), tadpole $0$ (U6). But all costs draw on the SAME
layer margin. With $C_2 = \mathrm{MARGIN}/\rho$, composed $=\mathrm{MARGIN}(1-1/\rho)$,
$C_{\rm sunset} = \text{composed}/1.13$, $C_{\rm quartic} = R_{\max}C_2$,
$C_{\rm tadpole}=0$ ($\rho=2.6$, $\mathrm{MARGIN}=0.00432$):
\[
\boxed{\;\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}}{C_2 + C_{\rm sunset} + C_{\rm quartic}}
= \times0.757 \;<\; 1 .\;}
\]
The summed second+third-order cost exceeds the layer margin by $\times1.32$. The
SC-SCOPE all-orders endpoint therefore does NOT close under the current
(individually positive) bounds. This is an honest negative, registered in
negative-results.

\section*{4. What closure would require}

The individual-positivity / joint-failure gap ($\times1.32$) is the signature of
a bound that is still loose at the JOINT level: the sunset bound is largest at
SMALL transfers (it rides $J(0)M$), the quartic-difference is largest at LARGE
transfers ($\Phi$ peaks at $t=2q_0$), and the second-order is spread across the
chord set. A JOINT incompatible-pairing argument -- bounding
$\max_t[\,c_{\rm sunset}(t) + c_{\rm quartic}(t)\,]$ rather than
$\max_t c_{\rm sunset} + \max_t c_{\rm quartic}$ -- would recover up to the
$\times1.32$ deficit, exactly as the single-channel per-transfer argument
recovered the sup-grade crudeness one level down. Sharper per-transfer sunset and
quartic bounds (both currently inherit one sup-grade factor) are the alternative.
Either is a genuine new derivation, registered as the next SC-SCOPE step; neither
is performed here.
