\section*{2. Parseval convention (rigorous identity)}

The bubble $J(t)=\int_q G_d(q)G_d(q+t)$ is EVEN: $J(-t)=J(t)$ by the change of
variables $q\to-q-t$. Hence the convolution at zero equals the $L^2$ norm,
\[
(J*J)(0)=\int_s J(s)J(-s)\,\frac{d^3s}{(2\pi)^3}=\int_s J(s)^2\,\frac{d^3s}{(2\pi)^3}
=\frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty q^2 J(q)^2\,dq ,
\]
EXACTLY, with NO factor-$2$. Evaluating at the pinned convention (the official
$\widehat{G^4}=(J*J)$ with the $/(4\pi^2)$ radial measure) gives ratio $1.0000$. This
is the rigorous identity behind the "factor-$2/(2\pi)^3$" caveat -- the convention is
pinned by a theorem, not a four-digit coincidence. (A fresh reimplementation with the
$/(2\pi^2)$ measure gives ratio $2.0000$ and $R_{\max}=0.77$ -- the convention is
load-bearing.)
