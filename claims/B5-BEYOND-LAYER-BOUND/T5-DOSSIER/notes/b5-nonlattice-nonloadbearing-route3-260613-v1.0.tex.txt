\section*{2. B5's beyond-layer bound depends on the competitor only through (T', n)}

$Q=-Q$; packing-bounded $|Q|=n\le n_{\rm pack}=16/\theta_{\min}^2$; coherence-

\[

where $T'(Q)=\max_{t\ne0}\#(Q\cap C_t)$ is the maximal proper sum-circle
occupancy. No other competitor feature enters; in particular the lattice/non-
lattice distinction enters ONLY via $T'$.

\section*{3. Lemma 2 caps T' pattern-generically (lattice and non-lattice alike)}

\begin{lemma}[Coherence circle-packing, res5\_036; restated]
For an admissible $Q$ and any $t\ne0$, $\#(Q\cap C_t)\le\lfloor2\pi/\theta_{\min}\rfloor$.
\end{lemma}
\emph{Proof (pure geometry).} $C_t$ is a Euclidean circle of radius
$\rho=\sqrt{q_0^2-|t|^2/4}\le q_0$. Its $Q$-points are pairwise geodesically
$\ge\theta_{\min}$, hence pairwise Euclidean $\ge d=2q_0\sin(\theta_{\min}/2)$.
The $k$ cyclically-ordered points have a smallest adjacent gap
$\Delta_{\min}\le2\pi/k$; coherence forces $d\le2\rho\sin(\Delta_{\min}/2)\le
2\rho\sin(\pi/k)$, so with $\rho\le q_0$, $k\le\pi/\arcsin(\sin(\theta_{\min}/2))
=2\pi/\theta_{\min}$. $\square$

\textbf{Key point: the proof uses ONLY $(\theta_{\min}, q_0)$ -- no lattice,
arithmetic, divisor, or decoupling input.} Across $\mathcal R$,
$\theta_{\min}\ge0.596$, so the cap is $\le10$ (machine: assert 1, sharp value
$10$ on the largest circle $\rho=q_0$; assert 2, monotone in $\rho$ and never
above the cap; assert 3, the idealized equatorial packing meets the cap while the
realized full-sphere admissible max is $8<10$ per the head's adversarial search;
assert 4, random NON-LATTICE $\theta_{\min}$-separated antipodal configs obey the
cap). The bound is identical for lattice and non-lattice competitors.
