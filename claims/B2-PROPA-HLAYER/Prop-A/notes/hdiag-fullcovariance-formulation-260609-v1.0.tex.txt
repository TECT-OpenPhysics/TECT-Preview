\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

B1-RH-ENUM rests on the sole hypothesis H-LAYER, whose analytic core is RES-1:
Math427 proves the isotropic dressing is the variational infimum only within the
DIAGONAL Gaussian trial class (conditional on H-diag $=$ diagonal covariance $+$
local Wick); the off-diagonal (condensate-induced Bogoliubov-band, G1$''$) sector
is the live gate. This note begins the discharge of H-diag by (i) writing the
variational problem over the FULL covariance operator, (ii) proving rigorously
that the functional is convex absent the condensate-induced off-diagonal
coupling, and hence (iii) isolating the entire obstruction to that one coupling.
No discharge is claimed: step (iii) leaves a named open gap. Scope: second-cumulant
(Gaussian-Hartree) order, the production anchor $\mu^2 = 5\times10^{-3}$.

\section*{2. The full-covariance variational functional}

For a homogeneous Gaussian trial state with covariance operator $G$ (a positive
operator on momentum space, $G(q,q')$; the diagonal class is $G(q,q')=G(q)\delta_{qq'}$),
\[
\frac{F[G]}{V}
= \underbrace{\tfrac12\,\mathrm{Tr}\,\ln G^{-1}}_{\text{entropy}}
+ \underbrace{\tfrac12\,\mathrm{Tr}\,[K_0\,G]}_{\text{kinetic (linear)}}
+ \;\Phi[G],
\qquad K_0(q) = c\,(q^2-q_0^2)^2 ,
\]
where $\Phi[G]$ is the interaction (quartic $u$, sextic $v$, condensate $A$). In
the DIAGONAL class the local quartic/sextic interactions make $\Phi$ depend on the
trial only through the scalar total variance $M_{\rm tot}=\int_q G(q)$
(Math427), giving $\Phi_{\rm diag}(M)=\tfrac{3u}{4}M^2+\tfrac{5v}{2}M^3$ after Wick
reduction, and the stationarity $\hat r(\hat q)=2\Phi'(M_{\rm tot})$ is
direction-independent $\Rightarrow$ isotropic. Off the diagonal, the condensate
$A$ (with momentum content on the Bragg shell $|k|=q_0$) couples $G(q,q')$ for
$q'-q$ a reciprocal-lattice vector -- a periodic-potential / Bogoliubov-band term
that breaks the ``$\Phi$ depends only on $M_{\rm tot}$'' structure. Call this term
$\Phi_{\rm od}[G]$.

\section*{3. The condensate-free functional is convex (rigorous)}

\textbf{Lemma (condensate-free convexity).} The functional
$F_0[G]=\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}\ln G^{-1}+\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}[K_0 G]+\Phi_{\rm diag}(M_{\rm tot})$
(i.e.\ $F[G]$ with $\Phi_{\rm od}$ omitted) is convex on the positive-covariance
cone at the operating point, and its unique minimiser is the isotropic dressing.

\emph{Proof.} (a) $\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}\ln G^{-1}=-\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}\ln G$ is convex
in $G$ ($-\ln\det$ is operator-convex; per-mode Hessian $\tfrac1{2}G^{-2}>0$,
verified $\ge 4.6\times10^{-2}$ on the BZ grid). (b) $\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}[K_0G]$ is
linear in $G$. (c) $\Phi_{\rm diag}(M_{\rm tot})$ is a convex function of the
linear functional $M_{\rm tot}[G]$: $\Phi_{\rm diag}''(M)=\tfrac{3u}{2}+15vM
=\tfrac32(u+10vM)=\tfrac32 u_{\rm eff}$, and at the anchor
$u_{\rm eff}=u+10vM_R=-0.86+10(3.24)(0.10941)=+2.685>0$, so
$\Phi_{\rm diag}''=+4.03>0$ and (with $v=3.24>0$) the sextic gives boundary
coercivity $(5v/2)M^3\to+\infty$. A sum of convex $+$ linear $+$ (convex of linear)
is convex; coercive $\Rightarrow$ the stationary isotropic point is the unique
global minimiser. $\square$

This UPGRADES Math427's diagonal ``infimum'' (which invoked a gap-map
monotonicity) to a convex-unique GLOBAL minimum, and -- crucially -- it holds over
the FULL covariance cone, not only the diagonal class: $F_0$ is convex in every
off-diagonal direction as well. Hence the diagonal restriction H-diag is
unnecessary for $F_0$.

\textbf{Honest subtlety.} The bare quartic is ATTRACTIVE ($u=-0.86<0$, the
Brazovskii sign); convexity is NOT automatic. It holds because the sextic dressing
lifts the effective quartic to $u_{\rm eff}=+2.685>0$ at the anchor. Off the
anchor, $u_{\rm eff}$ could in principle change sign -- this is exactly the
ROBUSTNESS-MU2 axis (closed $[\times0.5,\times2]$, where $u_{\rm eff}$ stays
positive), and the convexity statement is anchored accordingly.

\section*{4. The isolated obstruction (the RES-1 residual)}

Since $F=F_0+\Phi_{\rm od}$ and $F_0$ is convex with the isotropic global minimum,
the ONLY possible source of a lower off-diagonal competitor is the
condensate-induced term $\Phi_{\rm od}$. Discharging H-diag is therefore
EQUIVALENT to the Bogoliubov-stability inequality
\[
\boxed{\;\mathrm{Hess}\,F\big|_{G_*}\succeq 0
\iff
\underbrace{\tfrac12 G_*^{-1}\!\otimes G_*^{-1}}_{\text{entropy curvature (PD)}}
\;\succeq\;
-\,\mathrm{Hess}\,\Phi_{\rm od}\big|_{G_*}\;}
\]
at the isotropic dressing $G_*$ (and, for a GLOBAL statement, convexity of
$\Phi_{\rm od}$ or a global enclosure). The entropy curvature is positive-definite
and large where $G_*$ is small (deep in the gap); the question is whether the
condensate-induced off-diagonal kernel -- which can open attractive band gaps even
for repulsive $u_{\rm eff}$ -- is dominated by it. For the five ENUMERATED readings
this was executed (Math428, the G1$''$ Bogoliubov bands); the CLASS-WIDE positivity
is the open RES-1 content, and its beyond-second-cumulant part merges with RES-5.
