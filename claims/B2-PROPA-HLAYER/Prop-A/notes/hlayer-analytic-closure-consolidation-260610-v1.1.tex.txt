\subsection*{5.1 Adversarial-class caveat and fallback bound}

The sharp pin $T'(Q)\le13$ is a registered pin over $\mathcal A_{\rm adm}$; it is NOT a
theorem over arbitrary antipodal lattice subsets. Indeed there exist antipodal lattice sets
with
\[
N=40,\qquad T'(Q)=20>13,
\]
which are lattice-supported but not registered single-shell / shell-union competitors. Any
real antipodal competitor obeys the universal geometric fallback
\[
T'(Q)\le\frac{N}{2}
\]
($C_t$ and $C_{-t}$ disjoint for $t\ne0$; antipodal symmetry maps one onto the other, so
$2T'\le N$). With $N\le n_{\rm pack}=40.7$, hence $N\le40$, $T'(Q)\le20$, and
\[
20<20.5\ (\text{RES-1})<26.2\ (\text{RES-5})<60.4\ (\text{SC-SCOPE}).
\]
Thus off-shell escape competitors falsify the SHARP $T'\le13$ pin but do NOT falsify the
H-diag, RES-5, or SC-SCOPE thresholds, provided the packing bound $N\le40$ and real antipodal
symmetry are retained; the cost is thinness (RES-1 $R_{\rm lead}(20)=0.974$, $\times1.026$;
SC-SCOPE joint $\times1.082$). Whether such escape competitors are physically admissible in
B1/B2 is the operator question. (Full grading: aadm-exclusion-boundaries v1.1, Boundary 3.)
