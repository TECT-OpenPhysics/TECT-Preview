\section*{2. The full off-diagonal Hessian}

$\mathrm{Hess}\,F[G_*] = E + B_{\rm od}$, with the entropy curvature
$E=\tfrac12 G_*^{-1}\!\otimes G_*^{-1}\succ0$ (positive-definite) and the
interaction Hessian $B_{\rm od}=\mathrm{Hess}\,\Phi|_{G_*}$. In the off-diagonal
(Bloch, reciprocal-transfer $Q$) sector $B_{\rm od}$ has three parts:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hartree} -- depends on $G$ only through $M_{\rm tot}=\mathrm{Tr}\,G$,
hence NO off-diagonal block (convex, $\Phi''_{\rm diag}=\tfrac32u_{\rm eff}>0$,
companion note);
\item \textbf{Exchange (Fock)} -- $\propto u_{\rm eff}$ times the off-diagonal
$\int\!\int G(x,y)^2$ kernel: this is the load-bearing off-diagonal block;
\item \textbf{Condensate} -- $\propto u_{\rm eff}A^2 p_2(Q)$ (the Math428 $W(Q)$
source), the additive-energy-weighted coupling.
\end{itemize}
The worst-direction certificate $\|E^{-1/2}B_{\rm od}E^{-1/2}\|<1$ is equivalent to
$E+B_{\rm od}\succeq0$ over ALL off-diagonal $\delta G$, i.e.\ the entropy curvature
dominating the (possibly negative) exchange block.

\section*{3. Why $u_{\rm eff}>0$ does not suffice (the obstruction)}

The Hartree convexity established that $F_0$ (condensate-free) is convex because
$\Phi''_{\rm diag}=\tfrac32u_{\rm eff}>0$. But the EXCHANGE block carries the
opposite sign of the direct term: for a repulsive interaction the Fock exchange is
ATTRACTIVE. Here the bare quartic is itself attractive ($u=-0.86<0$) and only the
sextic dressing lifts the EFFECTIVE direct coupling to $u_{\rm eff}=+2.685>0$; the
exchange block's sign is therefore NOT fixed by $u_{\rm eff}>0$. Consequently
$B_{\rm od}\succeq0$ is NOT guaranteed, and the second-order envelope $R_{\rm lead}$
(which uses the bare entropy propagator $E^{-1}$, i.e.\ assumes $E+B_{\rm od}\approx
E$) does NOT bound the full norm. The full norm requires the exchange-Hessian
eigenvalues.

channel overlaps are bounded by the pair multiplicities $p_2$, so
