\section*{2. The off-diagonal Hessian from the Bragg-shell coupling (Math428)}

For a condensate $\phi_A = A\sum_{j=1}^{N} e^{ik_j x}$ ($|k_j|=q_0$), the
Gibbs--Bogoliubov second-order off-diagonal free-energy share is (Math428 \S3)
\[
\Delta F_{\rm od}(A) = -\tfrac14\sum_{Q\neq0} W(Q)^2\, B(|Q|),
\qquad
B(|Q|) = \int\!\frac{d^3q}{(2\pi)^3}\,G_d(q)\,G_d(q{+}Q),
\]
\[
W(Q) = (3u + 30vM_R)\,A^2\,p_2(Q) + 5v\,A^4\,p_4(Q)
      = 3u_{\rm eff}\,A^2\,p_2(Q) + 5v\,A^4\,p_4(Q),
\]
where the leading coupling carries the dressed quartic $u_{\rm eff}=u+10vM_R$
(\S3 of the companion note: $u_{\rm eff}=+2.685>0$) and the PAIR-STRUCTURE FACTOR
\[
p_2(Q) = \#\{(i,j):\, k_i + k_j = Q\} .
\]
$B(|Q|)>0$ is the convergent bubble (entropy/log-det curvature; $B(0)=-M'(\hat r)$),
so $\mathrm{Hess}\,\Phi_{\rm od}$ is the negative-definite quadratic form
$-\tfrac14\sum_Q (\cdot)^2 B$ and the stability inequality is the ratio
\[
\boxed{\;R(A) = \frac{|\Delta F_{\rm od}(A)|}{\Delta F_{\rm diag}(A)}
= \big\|E^{-1/2}\,\mathrm{Hess}\,\Phi_{\rm od}\,E^{-1/2}\big\| < 1.\;}
\]

\section*{3. The off-diagonal weight IS the additive energy (exact)}

The leading off-diagonal weight is $\propto p_2(Q)$, and its $\ell^2$ mass over
transfers is, identically,
\[
\sum_{Q} p_2(Q)^2
= \#\{(i,j,k,l):\, k_i + k_j = k_k + k_l\}
= E_+(\{k_j\}),
\]
the ADDITIVE ENERGY of the Bragg set. (Verified for the BCC first shell:
$N=12$ $\{110\}$-type vectors, $\sum_Q p_2(Q)^2 = 540 = E_+$ by direct quadruple
count; $p_2(0)=N=12$ reproduces Math428.) Hence
\[
|\Delta F_{\rm od}(A)|
\le \tfrac14 (3u_{\rm eff}A^2)^2\, B_{\max}\sum_{Q\neq0} p_2(Q)^2
+ \text{(quartic)}
\le \tfrac94 u_{\rm eff}^2 A^4\, B_{\max}\,E_+ ,
\qquad B_{\max}=\max_{Q\neq0}B(|Q|).
\]
The off-diagonal Hessian mass is bounded by the additive energy of the condensate
momentum set -- the SAME object as the carrier-richness / additive energy of DR-2.

\section*{4. Reduction to the R-025/R-026 machinery (class-wide)}

R-025 (Lemma A) gives $E_+\le(1+T'(\{k_j\}))N^2$ with $T'$ the maximal sum-circle
occupancy; R-026 gives $T'\ll_\varepsilon R^\varepsilon$ for any crystallographic
(lattice) shell. (BCC check: $E_+=540\le(1+T')N^2=(1+4)\cdot144=720$, $T'=4$,
$E_+/N^2=3.75=O(1)$.) The diagonal positive part is $\Delta F_{\rm diag}\ge
c_2 A^2$ with $c_2 = n\,r_R = \tfrac{N}{2}r_R$ (Math428: $c_2=1.827$ for BCC).
Imposing the operating-intensity constraint $A^2 \sim I/N$ (fixed condensate
intensity $I$ spread over $N$ modes),
\[
R(A)\ \lesssim\
\frac{\tfrac94 u_{\rm eff}^2 A^4 B_{\max} E_+}{\tfrac{N}{2} r_R A^2}
\ \sim\
\frac{u_{\rm eff}^2\,B_{\max}}{r_R}\,\frac{A^2 E_+}{N}
\ \sim\
\frac{u_{\rm eff}^2\,B_{\max}}{r_R}\,\frac{I\,E_+}{N^2}
\ \lesssim\
\frac{u_{\rm eff}^2\,B_{\max}}{r_R}\,I\,(1+T') ,
\]
using $E_+/N^2\le 1+T'$. For the lattice class $T'\ll_\varepsilon R^\varepsilon$, so
$R$ is bounded by the operating intensity $I$ times a SUBPOLYNOMIAL-in-$N$ factor:
the off-diagonal stability is controlled CLASS-WIDE by the additive-energy bound,
not pattern-by-pattern. This is the same structural mechanism by which R-026
discharged H-ADM-COH.
