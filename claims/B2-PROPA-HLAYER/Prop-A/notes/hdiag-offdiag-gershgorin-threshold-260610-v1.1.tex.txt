\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The full-operator-norm formulation note constructed the off-diagonal Hessian
$\mathrm{Hess}\,F|_{G_*} = E + B_{\rm od}$ and isolated the obstruction to the
worst-direction certificate $\|E^{-1/2}B_{\rm od}E^{-1/2}\|<1$ as the sign of the
exchange (Fock) block, naming it a SPECTRAL residual (the exchange-Hessian
eigenvalues). This note sharpens the residual in three honest steps -- a
diagonality lemma removing the condensate-direction caveat, a triangle inequality
isolating the exchange block with an explicit budget, and a row-sum bound
replacing the eigenvalue spectrum by a single competitor-dependent scalar
threshold. None is a closure; the scalar that remains is RES-5. Scope: the
operating endpoint anchor $\mu^2=0.005$; the full admissible crystallographic
competitor class (BCC quoted as the anchor specialisation).

\section*{2. Block decomposition and the diagonality lemma}

With $E = \tfrac12 G_*^{-1}\!\otimes G_*^{-1}\succ0$ (positive-definite, diagonal
in $Q$) the interaction Hessian splits as $B_{\rm od} = B^{\rm cond} + B^{\rm exch}$,
the Hartree part carrying no off-diagonal block. From Math428 the condensate/bubble
off-diagonal free-energy share is
\[
\Delta F_{\rm od}(A) = -\tfrac14\sum_{Q\neq0} W(Q)^2\,B(|Q|),\qquad
W(Q) = 3u_{\rm eff}A^2 p_2(Q) + 5vA^4 p_4(Q),
\]
a sum of INDEPENDENT channel contributions.

\textbf{Diagonality lemma.} \emph{If a free-energy share is a sum of independent
per-channel terms $\sum_Q f_Q(G_{QQ})$, its second variation
$\delta^2 = \sum_Q f_Q''(G_{QQ})\,(\delta G_{QQ})^2$ has no $Q\neq Q'$ cross term;
hence the induced $\delta G$-Hessian $B^{\rm cond}$ is block-diagonal in the channel
index $Q$.} Consequently $\|E^{-1/2}B^{\rm cond}E^{-1/2}\|_{\rm op}=\max_Q$
(single-channel ratio), a worst-direction (all-directions) statement, NOT merely
the condensate-direction one. The exchange block $B^{\rm exch}$ is genuinely
off-diagonal; its sign is not fixed by $u_{\rm eff}>0$ (bare $u=-0.86<0$,
$u_{\rm eff}=+2.685>0$ via the sextic dressing).

\section*{3. The condensate block over the FULL admissible class}

The constant certificate bounds the single-channel ratio by
$R_{\rm lead}(Q)$, and the floor identity pins it to the additive-energy floor
$K_{\rm floor}=E_+/N^2-1$:
\[
R_{\rm lead}(Q) = \mathrm{const}\,(1+K_{\rm floor}(Q))\,I,\qquad
\mathrm{const}=\frac{9}{4}\frac{u_{\rm eff}^2 B_{\max}N}{c_{\rm diag}}=23.2,\quad
I=2\times10^{-3}.
\]
\textbf{Operator-review correction.} v1.0 quoted $R_{\rm lead}=0.174$, which is the
BCC ANCHOR value ($K_{\rm floor}=2.75$). Over the full admissible class the floor is
larger (uniform worst $K_{\rm floor}=12.13$, T-014; non-uniform $T'\le13$, T-015):
\[
\boxed{\;
R_{\rm lead}^{\rm BCC}=0.174\,(\times5.7),\quad
R_{\rm lead}^{\rm unif}=0.609\,(\times1.64),\quad
R_{\rm lead}^{\rm nonunif}\le0.650\,(\times1.54),\quad \text{all}<1\;}
\]
so the condensate block is worst-direction certified $<1$ over the WHOLE class, but
with margin $\times1.54$, not $\times5.7$.

\section*{4. Triangle inequality and the competitor-dependent threshold}

By the triangle inequality,
$\|E^{-1/2}B_{\rm od}E^{-1/2}\|_{\rm op}\le R_{\rm lead}(Q) + \rho_{\rm exch}$ with
$\rho_{\rm exch}=\|E^{-1/2}B^{\rm exch}E^{-1/2}\|_{\rm op}$, so the full norm is $<1$
provided $\rho_{\rm exch} < 1 - R_{\rm lead}(Q)$ (budget $0.350$ at the binding
non-uniform point, $0.826$ at the BCC anchor). For a symmetric operator
$\|M\|_{\rm op}\le\max_Q\sum_{Q'}|M_{QQ'}|$; factoring the exchange row sum as
$N_{\rm exch}(Q)\,b_{\rm exch}$ ($N_{\rm exch}=\max_Q\#\{Q':Q-Q'\in\{k_i-k_j\}\}$,
combinatorial; $N_{\rm exch}=17\le n_{\rm chans}=54$ for BCC) gives the
\textbf{competitor-dependent closure criterion}
\[
\boxed{\;b_{\rm exch}(Q)\ <\ b_\ast(Q):=\frac{1-R_{\rm lead}(Q)}{N_{\rm exch}(Q)}
\quad\forall\,Q\in\mathcal A_{\rm adm}\;}
\]
with the explicit values $b_\ast^{\rm BCC}=0.0486$, $b_\ast^{\rm unif}=0.0230$,
$b_\ast^{\rm nonunif}=0.0206$ (the binding full-class target). The v1.0 single number
$0.0486$ was the BCC anchor only; the class target is $\sim2.4\times$ tighter. This
replaces the exchange-Hessian eigenvalue spectrum by one scalar bound per competitor.

\section*{5. What this is / is NOT}

A REDUCTION (T3 formulation), not a closure: $b_{\rm exch}$ (the dressed Fock-bubble
magnitude/sign) is the RES-5 scalar and is NOT bounded here. The increment over the
formulation note is that (a) the condensate block is now worst-direction certified
over the full class (diagonality lemma), (b) the residual is the exchange block ALONE
with explicit competitor-dependent budget, (c) the eigenvalue spectrum is replaced by
a single scalar threshold per competitor. No tier flip: B1/B2 remain T6 on
$\{$H-LAYER$\}$.
