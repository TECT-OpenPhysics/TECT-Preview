\section*{2. The band-positivity criterion}

From Math428 the condensate off-diagonal free-energy share is
\[
\Delta F_{\rm est}(A)=\Delta F_{\rm diag}(A)+\rho\Big[-\tfrac14\sum_{Q\neq0}W(Q)^2 B(|Q|)\Big],
\qquad W(Q)\propto A^2,
\]
with $\rho$ the exact/second-order resummation ratio. The Bogoliubov band is positive
iff $\Delta F_{\rm est}>0$, i.e.\ $\Delta F_{\rm diag}>\rho\cdot\tfrac14\sum W^2 B$:
\[
\boxed{\ \Delta F_{\rm est}>0\iff \rho\,R_{\rm lead}<1,\qquad
R_{\rm lead}=\frac{\tfrac14\sum_{Q\neq0}W^2 B}{\Delta F_{\rm diag}}\ }
\]
the band sign is governed by the single product $\rho\,R_{\rm lead}$ (verified: the
band proxy $1-\rho R_{\rm lead}$ is $>0$ at product $0.5$, $<0$ at $1.5$, $=0$ at $1$).

\section*{3. At $\rho=1$: class-wide reduction to $R_{\rm lead}<1$}

The conservative no-resummation-credit value $\rho=1$ (explicitly reported by Math428)
gives the criterion $R_{\rm lead}<1$. T-018 pins $R_{\rm lead}$ over the admissible
class via the floor identity
\[
R_{\rm lead}(Q)=\mathrm{const}\,(1+K_{\rm floor}(Q))\,I,\quad
\mathrm{const}=\tfrac94\tfrac{u_{\rm eff}^2 B_{\max}N}{c_{\rm diag}}=23.2,\quad
K_{\rm floor}(Q)\le T'(Q)\le13,
\]
so for EVERY admissible competitor
\[
R_{\rm lead}(Q)\ \le\ 23.2\,(1+13)\,(2\times10^{-3})\ =\ 0.650\ <\ 1
\qquad(\text{margin }\times1.54).
\]
Therefore $\rho\,R_{\rm lead}\le1\cdot0.650<1$ at $\rho=1$: the second-cumulant
off-diagonal bands are POSITIVE class-wide. This extends Math428's five enumerated
readings (band min $+6.7\times10^{-4}>0$ at $\rho=1$) to all admissible patterns by
the same criterion.

\section*{4. The screening resummation is a credit ($\rho\le1$)}

The resummation ratio is $\rho=1/(1+g)$ with $g\ge0$ the screened-response coupling of
the stable disordered parent (screening, not anti-screening). Hence $\rho\le1$ and the
$\rho=1$ bound is conservative. With the Math428-consistent $g=1.03$,
$\rho=0.493$ and $\rho\,R_{\rm lead}\le0.32$ -- a further $\times3$ margin. The
class-wide stability does not rely on the resummation credit; $\rho=1$ already closes it.
