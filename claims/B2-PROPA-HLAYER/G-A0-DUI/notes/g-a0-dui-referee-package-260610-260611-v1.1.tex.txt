\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This referee document concerns G-A0-DUI: the differentiation-under-the-integral justification for the slaved-variance derivative, supporting the $A=0$ (no-condensate) uniqueness. \textbf{Scope:} the slaved-variance derivative $M'(m)$ on the window. \textbf{Not claimed:} only the DUI lemma and its consequence for $A=0$ uniqueness.

\section*{2. The result}

Lemma L1 ($M'(m)<0$) is proven by dominated convergence with the dominating function $k^2/[m_0+C(k^2-q_0^2)^2]^2$, which is integrable and pointwise-dominating; G-A0-DUI is CLOSED. With L2/L3 in closed form, $A=0$ is the unique stationary point.

\section*{3. Structure of the argument}

The variance integral $M(m)=\tfrac1{2\pi^2}\int k^2/D\,dk$ is differentiated under the integral sign; the derivative integrand is dominated by an integrable function independent of $m$ on the window, justifying the interchange (dominated convergence). Hence $M'(m)<0$ pointwise, and the free-energy derivative inherits the sign that forces $A=0$ uniqueness. The dominating lower bound $D(k,m)\ge m_0+C(k^2-q_0^2)^2$ holds uniformly for all $m$ in the certified window with $m_0>0$, which is exactly what makes the dominating function $m$-independent and integrable.

\section*{4. Numerical reproduction}

The sign-decomposition script reproduces $M'<0$ and the $A=0$ uniqueness over the window with self-test asserts; run it from the bundle. The expected output is $M'(m)<0$ on the window and no second $A\neq0$ stationary point.
