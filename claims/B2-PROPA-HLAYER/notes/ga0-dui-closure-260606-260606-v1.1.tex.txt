\section*{2. G-A0-DUI: the explicit argument}

With $D(k,m) = m + C(k^2 - q_0^2)^2$ and the radial reduction
$M(m) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty \frac{k^2}{D(k,m)}\,dk$:

\begin{lemma}[L1, now unconditional]
For every $m > 0$, $M$ is differentiable with
\[
M'(m) \;=\; -\frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty \frac{k^2}{D(k,m)^2}\,dk \;<\; 0 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix $m > 0$ and set $m_0 = m/2$; for $|h| < m/2$ every intermediate value
$\tilde m \in [m/2, 3m/2]$ satisfies $\tilde m \ge m_0$, so the SAME
dominator applies on BOTH sides ($h \gtrless 0$), giving two-sided
differentiability at $m$. For $\tilde m \ge m_0$, $\partial_{\tilde m} D^{-1}
= -D^{-2}$ satisfies the pointwise bound
\[
\Bigl|\partial_{\tilde m} \tfrac{1}{D(k,\tilde m)}\Bigr|
= \frac{1}{D(k,\tilde m)^2}
\;\le\; \frac{1}{D(k,m_0)^2}
\;=:\; \frac{1}{[m_0 + C(k^2-q_0^2)^2]^2} ,
\]
and the dominating function $k^2/D(k,m_0)^2$ is integrable on $(0,\infty)$
across all three regions: near $k=0$ the denominator is bounded below by a
positive constant so the integrand is $O(k^2)$; near the shell $k=q_0$ it is
bounded by $k^2/m_0^2$ (where $D \to m_0$); and as $k \to \infty$ it decays as
$k^2(Ck^4)^{-2} = C^{-2}k^{-6}$. By the Leibniz rule under dominated convergence,
differentiation and integration commute, giving the stated $M'$; the
integrand $k^2/D^2$ is strictly positive, so $M'(m) < 0$ strictly.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent Machine confirmation (independent radial quadrature):
$M' = -\frac{1}{2\pi^2}\int k^2/D^2$ matches the finite-difference $M'$
to $0.04$--$0.6\%$ at $m = 0.1, m^*, 1.0$; the dominating-function
integral is finite ($2.36$ at $m_0 = 0.05$); the pointwise domination
$D(m^*)^{-2} \le D(m_0)^{-2}$ holds with max ratio $1.000$. G-A0-DUI is
CLOSED.
