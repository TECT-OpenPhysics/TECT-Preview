\section*{4. The floor-$\kappa$ lever (route A) and its DR-2 character}

The lever tightening the joint from conservative ($\kappa=1$) toward verified is
$\kappa=K_{\rm floor}/T'$, $K_{\rm floor}=\sum_{t\ne0}w_t^2/I^2$,
$w_t=\sum_{u+v=t}A_uA_v$. Two rigorous results:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] \textbf{PROVED refinement.} By Cauchy-Schwarz $w_t^2\le r(t)S_t$,
$S_t=\sum_{u+v=t}A_u^2A_v^2$, with $\sum_{t\ne0}S_t=I^2-\|A\|_4^4$,
\[
\boxed{\ K_{\rm floor}\le T'\Bigl(1-\tfrac{\|A\|_4^4}{I^2}\Bigr)\le T'\bigl(1-\tfrac1n\bigr).\ }
\]
\item[(b)] \textbf{EXACT scan.} Over all crystallographic single shells ($n\le42$,
uniform amplitudes) the exact additive energy gives worst $\kappa=K_{\rm floor}/T'
=0.75$ (at $R=4$, $n=6$) -- correcting the prior incomplete-sample $0.52$ (which
omitted the small shells $R\le8$).
\end{itemize}
The refinement (a) is weak for dense patterns ($1-1/n\to1$); the tight worst-case
$\kappa<1$ over the DENSE admissible class is the additive-energy / circle-incidence
problem = the DR-2 frontier. So the floor lever is DR-2-adjacent.
