\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The RES-5 entry note established the common-mode control parameter $a_0$ and the
leading envelope. The operator directed the all-order bound (three goals: the
all-order split; the per-order $O(a_0)$ bound; the summed series with $C_{\rm cm}<10.4$).
This note executes the programme as a REDUCTION: it shows RES-5 closure is
equivalent to a geometric-domination condition on the condensate-perturbation
series, and that the base (measured) order sits a factor $\sim5$ inside the
closure region. It does NOT close RES-5 -- the all-order domination is the deep
residual. Scope: the structure and the closure arithmetic; anchor $\mu^2$,
operating endpoint.

\section*{2. The condensate-perturbation series (goal 1: the split)}

At fixed $I$ the dressing $\hat r(I)$ is pattern-independent, so the exact
selection difference is a perturbation series in the condensate
$\lambda'\mathcal P^2 = O(a_0)\,\hat r$ on the common sea $D_0(I)$:
\[
\Delta F = \sum_{k\ge2}\Delta F^{(k)},\qquad
F^{(n)} = \underbrace{F^{(n)}_{\rm cm}(D_0)}_{\text{cancels in }\Delta F^{(n)}}
+ F^{(n)}_{\rm pd}(D_0;\mathcal P^2),\qquad
\Delta F^{(k)} \sim a_0^{\,k-1}\,\chi^{(k)},
\]
where $\chi^{(k)}$ is the $k$-th connected density susceptibility of the common
sea (pattern-independent). The common-mode part $F^{(n)}_{\rm cm}$ -- which carries
the STRONG Brazovskii fluctuations -- cancels exactly in the difference; the
pattern-dependent part enters with one power of $a_0$ per order.

\section*{3. The per-order ratio and the closure condition (goals 2--3)}

Define the geometric ratio
\[
r_k = \frac{\Delta F^{(k+1)}}{\Delta F^{(k)}} = a_0\,\frac{\chi^{(k+1)}}{\chi^{(k)}}.
\]
\textbf{Reduction.} If $r_k \le r < \tfrac12$ for all $k\ge2$, the geometric tail gives
\[
\Big|\sum_{k\ge3}\Delta F^{(k)}\Big| \le \Delta F^{(2)}\,\frac{r}{1-r} < \Delta F^{(2)}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\Delta F_{\rm exact} = \Delta F^{(2)}\Big(1-\tfrac{r}{1-r}\Big) > 0,
\]
so RES-5 closes. The SUFFICIENT condition is $r<\tfrac12$ (at $r=0.49$ the tail is
$0.96<1$; at $r=0.51$ it is $1.04>1$). In the operator's form this is
$C_{\rm cm}\,a_0<1$ with $C_{\rm cm}=\tfrac{1}{a_0}\cdot\tfrac{r}{1-r}$, i.e.\
$C_{\rm cm}<1/a_0=10.5$.

\section*{4. The base margin}

The base ratio is $r_2 = a_0\,\chi^{(3)}/\chi^{(2)}$. The susceptibility ratio is
$O(1)$ (SC-SCOPE put the $n=3$ endpoint effect at a few percent, consistent with
$r_2\sim a_0$, far from $\tfrac12$). Numerically,
\[
r_2 \sim a_0 = 0.096 \quad(\text{factor } \sim5 \text{ below } \tfrac12),
\qquad
C_{\rm cm}\sim O(1)\quad(\text{factor } \sim10 \text{ below } 10.5),
\]
and, IF $r_k\le a_0$ at all orders, $\Delta F_{\rm exact}=\Delta F^{(2)}(1-0.106)=
0.894\,\Delta F^{(2)}>0$. The condensate-perturbation series starts well inside its
convergence radius.
