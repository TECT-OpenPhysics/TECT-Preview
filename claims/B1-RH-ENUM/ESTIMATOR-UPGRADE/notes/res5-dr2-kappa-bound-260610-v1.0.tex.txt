\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The endpoint's sole remaining lever (res5-endpoint v1.2) is the DR-2 floor: prove
$\kappa=K_{\rm floor}/T'$ small enough over the dense admissible class. This note
shows the closure is governed by the ABSOLUTE $K_{\rm floor}$ and that every
ENUMERATED competitor closes the endpoint with a large margin, isolating the
residual to the dense worst-case. Scope: the $I=2\times10^{-3}$ endpoint; the
crystallographic competitor class; estimate-grade threshold, exact $K_{\rm floor}$.
No tier action.

\section*{2. The closure threshold}

The floor enters the joint through $\rho_{\rm lat}=K_{\rm budget}/(1+K_{\rm floor})$,
$K_{\rm budget}=266.7$. With the corrected tail $C_{\rm higher}=0.059$
(res5-projection-factor-bound), the endpoint closes for a competitor iff its slack
exceeds the tail:
\[
\mathrm{joint}>\frac{1}{1-0.059}=1.063
\ \Longleftrightarrow\ \rho_{\rm lat}>9.8
\ \Longleftrightarrow\ \boxed{\ K_{\rm floor}<\frac{K_{\rm budget}}{9.8}-1=26.2\ }
\]
The endpoint closure is thus a bound on the ABSOLUTE $K_{\rm floor}=\sum_{t\ne0}
w_t^2/I^2$ of the competitor, not on the ratio $\kappa$.

hypothetical competitor saturating $K_{\rm floor}\sim T'\sim n_{\rm pack}=40.7$

not of the Reading-H competitor list.

\section*{5. The residual is DR-2}

The only way the endpoint can fail is a DENSE admissible competitor with
$K_{\rm floor}>26.2$. The proved refinement $K_{\rm floor}\le T'(1-\|A\|_4^4/I^2)\le
T'(1-1/n)$ allows $K_{\rm floor}$ up to $\sim n_{\rm pack}=40.7$ for a dense $n\sim40$
pattern (insufficient). Whether the dense admissible class actually contains such a
pattern is the worst-case additive-energy question -- the circle-incidence frontier,
$=$ DR-2, $=$ B1's unrestricted-class question. So:
\[
\underbrace{K_{\rm floor}^{\rm enum}\le12}_{\text{closes}}\ <\ \underbrace{26.2}_{\text{threshold}}\ <\ \underbrace{K_{\rm floor}^{\rm dense}\le40.7}_{\text{DR-2}} .
\]

enumerated/lattice class (exact $K_{\rm floor}\le12$, threshold $26.2$ estimate-grade;
