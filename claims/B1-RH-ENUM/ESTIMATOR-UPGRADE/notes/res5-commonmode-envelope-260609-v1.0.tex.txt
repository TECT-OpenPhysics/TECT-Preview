\section*{2. The matched-order-to-exact problem}

\[
\Delta F_{\rm exact} = \Delta F^{(2)} + \sum_{n\ge3}\Delta F^{(n)},
\qquad
\Delta F^{(n)} = F^{(n)}[\mathcal P]-F^{(n)}[\mathcal R_H].
\]
RES-5 closes iff $\big|\sum_{n\ge3}\Delta F^{(n)}\big| < \Delta F^{(2)}$ (the
GAP-2 controlled-error condition $\Delta F^{(2)}-\varepsilon_{\rm ctrl}>0$).

\section*{3. The common-mode mechanism (extending R-U10-3 to the bulk)}

At fixed intensity $I$ the diagonal dressing
\[
\hat r(I) = r_R + 2\lambda' I
\]
is PATTERN-INDEPENDENT: $\mathcal P$ and $\mathcal R_H$ share the same dressed
inverse propagator $D_0(I)$. This is exactly the structure the near-gap
resolution (R-U10-3, neargap-common-mode-resolution) used at second cumulant to
prove $\Delta F^{(2)}\ge0$ unconditionally. Extending it: each
beyond-second-cumulant correction $F^{(n)}$ splits into
\[
F^{(n)} = \underbrace{F^{(n)}_{\rm cm}(D_0)}_{\text{common-mode, pattern-independent}}
+ \underbrace{F^{(n)}_{\rm pd}(D_0;\mathcal P^2)}_{\text{pattern-dependent}},
\]
and the common-mode part CANCELS in $\Delta F^{(n)}=F^{(n)}[\mathcal P]-F^{(n)}[\mathcal R_H]$.
The pattern-dependent part is carried by the condensate's relative weight in the
dressing,
\[
a_0(I) = \frac{2\lambda' I}{\hat r(I)},
\]
so $\Delta F^{(n)} = O(a_0)\,F^{(n)}$: the matched-order difference is $O(a_0)$
suppressed relative to the individual cumulants.
