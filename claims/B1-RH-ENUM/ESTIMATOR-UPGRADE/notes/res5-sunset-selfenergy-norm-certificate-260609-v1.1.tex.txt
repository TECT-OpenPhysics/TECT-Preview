\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This is an audit re-issue of v1.0 following the operator adversarial review of
2026-06-09. The review accepts the self-energy/free-energy normalization
correction (Section 2) and retracts the survival verdict (Sections 4--5): the
higher-skeleton tail bound carried by v1.0 is screened-finite but \emph{same
order} as the leading skeleton, and is therefore not bounded into the thin
SC-SCOPE slack. The honest status of RES-5/GAP-2 is OPEN. No tier action; B1
stays T6 CONDITIONAL on $\{$H-LAYER$\}$.

\section*{2. Correction (ACCEPTED): the certificate quantity is a free-energy ratio}

The a0-skeleton estimate $c\,a_0 \sim 0.49\cdot a_0\cdot(\|\Sigma_2^{\rm pd}\|/
\Delta F_{\rm margin})$ took $\|\Sigma_2^{\rm pd}\|/\Delta F_{\rm margin}\sim0.04$,
but that $0.04$ is the FREE-ENERGY (sunset) ratio, not a self-energy norm.
Because $\Delta\Gamma_2^{\rm pd}=\|\Sigma_2^{\rm pd}\|\,\|\delta G_*^{\rm pd}\|$,
quoting the free-energy ratio AND multiplying by $\|\delta G_*^{\rm pd}\|=O(a_0)$
double-counts $a_0$. The certificate quantity is directly
\[
\mathcal{R}_{\rm cert}\;=\;\frac{|\Delta\Gamma_2^{\rm pd}|}{\Delta F_{\rm margin}},
\]
whose leading (sunset / 2-loop) value is the SC-SCOPE certified joint, NOT
$0.2\%$. This correction stands and is the accepted content of this note.

\section*{3. The normalization map (leading skeleton; identity still OPEN)}

SC-SCOPE certified the endpoint joint
\[
\mathrm{joint}=\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}}{C_2+C_{\rm sunset}+C_{\rm quartic}}
=\times1.040\ (\text{certified})\ \longrightarrow\ \times1.13\ (\text{sunset cap}),
\]
both $>1$: the third cumulant (the leading 2PI skeleton) does not overturn the
one-loop selection. Structurally this is the leading-skeleton statement
$\mathcal{R}_{\rm cert}^{\rm leading}<1$. The exact identity
$\mathcal{R}_{\rm cert}^{\rm leading}=\mathcal{C}_{\rm SC}$, with
$\mathcal{C}_{\rm SC}$ the SC-SCOPE $C_{\rm sunset}+C_{\rm quartic}$
normalization, is asserted from the shared third-cumulant origin but is NOT
proved here. It is the first residual.

\section*{4. Higher skeletons: screened-finite but SAME ORDER (corrected reading)}

v1.0 called the $\ell\ge3$ tail ``screened sub-dominant''. The 2PI screened
response (res5-a0-skeleton-sensitivity) bounds each order by the previous times
$1/(1+g)=0.49$, giving the geometric tail
\[
C_{\rm higher}=\sum_{\ell\ge3}|\Delta\Gamma_\ell^{\rm pd}|
\ \lesssim\ |\Delta\Gamma_{2\text{-loop}}^{\rm pd}|\,\frac{1}{1-0.49}
\ \approx\ 2\,|\Delta\Gamma_{2\text{-loop}}^{\rm pd}| .
\]
This is \emph{screened-finite}, but it is the SAME ORDER as the leading
skeleton, not a small fraction of it. The selection slack at the thin joint is
\[
\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}-C_{\rm leading}}{\mathrm{MARGIN}}
=1-\frac{1}{1.040}\approx 3.85\%,
\qquad
C_{\rm higher}\ \text{must be}\ <0.040\,C_{\rm leading}.
\]
A tail bounded only by $\sim2\,C_{\rm sunset}=O(C_{\rm leading})$ is roughly an
order of magnitude too large to be admitted into a $3.85\%$ slack. The
screened-finiteness therefore does NOT establish that the higher skeletons
respect the thin margin; it only establishes that they do not diverge.
