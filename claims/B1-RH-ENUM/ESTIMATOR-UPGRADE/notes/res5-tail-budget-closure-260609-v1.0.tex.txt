\section*{1. Purpose and scope: the budget and the screened tail}

The sunset-norm certificate (v1.1) fixed the target: all-orders selection at the
operating point survives iff the higher-skeleton ($\ell\ge3$) pattern-dependent
cost fits the third-cumulant slack,
\[
C_{\rm higher}\;<\;\Delta F_{\rm margin}-C_{\rm leading}
\;=\;\Bigl(1-\tfrac{1}{\rm joint}\Bigr)\Delta F_{\rm margin},
\qquad \mathrm{joint}=\frac{\Delta F_{\rm margin}}{C_{\rm leading}} .
\]
The 2PI screened-response analysis (res5-a0-skeleton-sensitivity;
res5-2pi-resummation-strategy) estimates the resummed pattern-dependent tail by
the screened response $C_G=1/(1+g)$ acting on the pattern-dependent dressing
amplitude $a_0$,
\[
\boxed{\;\frac{C_{\rm higher}}{\Delta F_{\rm margin}}\;\approx\;C_G\,a_0(I),
\qquad a_0(I)=\frac{2\lambda' I}{\hat r(I)},\quad \hat r(I)=r_R+2\lambda' I\;}
\]
with $g=\lambda' B_d=1.03$ (marginal), $C_G=1/(1+g)=0.492$ (repulsive screening,
NOT $1/(1-g)$). Since $\hat r(I)\to r_R$ for $\lambda' I\ll r_R$, $a_0(I)$ is
approximately proportional to $I$: the tail HALVES from the endpoint to
$I=10^{-3}$.

\section*{2. Intensity dependence (the robust finding)}

The third-cumulant slack is set by the SC-SCOPE joint, which grows rapidly off
the $I=2\times10^{-3}$ endpoint: the endpoint joint is the certified thin
$1.040$ (scscope-quartic-normalisation-certificate), while the all-orders lift
is estimate-feasible with paired joint $\sim3.1$ at $I=10^{-3}$
(scscope-scope-decision) and the STEP-5B floor is $\times59.4/\times8.8/\times2.6$
at $\{4\times10^{-4},10^{-3},2\times10^{-3}\}$. Tabulating the budget:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccccc}
\toprule
$I$ & $a_0(I)$ & tail $=C_G a_0$ & joint & slack & tail/slack \\
\midrule
$4\times10^{-4}$ & $0.021$ & $0.010$ & $\sim8$   & $0.875$ & $0.012$ \\
$1\times10^{-3}$ & $0.050$ & $0.025$ & $\sim3.1$ & $0.677$ & $0.036$ \\
$2\times10^{-3}$ & $0.096$ & $0.047$ & $1.040$   & $0.0385$ & $1.224$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Off the endpoint the tail shrinks AND the slack grows, so $\mathrm{tail/slack}$
improves by $34\times$ from $I=2\times10^{-3}$ to $I=10^{-3}$.

\section*{3. Verdict}

\noindent\textbf{Closes for $I\le10^{-3}$ (STRONG EVIDENCE).} The budget holds
with a $\ge27\times$ margin ($\mathrm{tail/slack}\le0.036$); the closure is robust
to the estimate uncertainty in both $C_G a_0$ and the off-endpoint joint, because
the margin is two orders of magnitude.

\noindent\textbf{Marginal / estimate-undetermined at the $I=2\times10^{-3}$
endpoint (OPEN).} The screened tail $0.047$ and the thin slack $0.0385$ are the
SAME ORDER ($\mathrm{tail/slack}=1.22$); the all-orders endpoint selection cannot
be decided at estimate grade -- it could go either way within the uncertainty of
$C_G a_0$ and of the certified joint.

\noindent\textbf{RES-5 is localised to the endpoint.} It is not a global
obstruction: the higher-skeleton tail respects the budget everywhere except the
$I=2\times10^{-3}$ endpoint, which is exactly where SC-SCOPE is B1's named
second-cumulant hypothesis. This sharpens RES-5/GAP-2 from ``OPEN'' to ``closed
for $I\le10^{-3}$; endpoint-marginal''. No tier flip: B1-RH-ENUM stays T6
CONDITIONAL on $\{$H-LAYER$\}$.

\section*{4. Estimate vs theorem (CLAUDE.md 6.3.5b)}

Both $C_{\rm higher}/\Delta F_{\rm margin}\approx C_G a_0$ and the off-endpoint
joints are CLASSIFIED ESTIMATES, not constant-bound theorems. Two independent
estimates of the endpoint tail coexist in the record and are not yet reconciled:
the SC-SCOPE leading cost/margin $=1/\mathrm{joint}=0.962$ (slack $0.038$) and the
resummed $C_G a_0=0.047$; they straddle the budget. The $I\le10^{-3}$ closure
does not depend on this reconciliation (its margin absorbs it); the endpoint does.
The residual is therefore a single rigorous endpoint bound:
$C_{\rm higher}\le \mathcal{C}\,\|F\|^2$ with $\mathcal{C}$ below the slack, from a
genuine 2PI computation of $\Delta\Gamma_2^{\rm pd}[G_*]$ -- or acceptance of the
endpoint as the named-hypothesis boundary (the current B1 T6 scope).

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``$C_G a_0$ omits an $O(1)$ skeleton-norm prefactor, so the
endpoint tail is under-stated.''} VALID: the prefactor (the pattern-dependent
self-energy norm relative to the margin) is the residual already flagged in the
a0-skeleton note; with it $\gtrsim1$ the endpoint only worsens, reinforcing the
OPEN verdict there. It does NOT affect the $I\le10^{-3}$ closure, whose $27\times$
margin survives any $O(1)$ prefactor.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The off-endpoint joints ($\sim3.1$, $\sim8$) are estimate-
grade, so the closure is not rigorous.''} VALID-with-mitigation: the closure is
labelled STRONG EVIDENCE, not a theorem; the $\ge27\times$ margin is the reason a
factor-few uncertainty in the joint cannot overturn it. A rigorous interval
joint$(I)$ on $[4\times10^{-4},10^{-3}]$ is the registered polish.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Localising RES-5 to the endpoint changes nothing -- the
production anchor IS the endpoint.''} DISMISSED as overstated, ACCEPTED in part:
the production anchor is $I=2\times10^{-3}$, so the endpoint marginalit-y is
load-bearing for the all-orders claim there -- which is precisely why SC-SCOPE is
retained as the named hypothesis at the endpoint (B1 T6). The localisation is
nonetheless a real advance: it proves the obstruction is one boundary point, not
the bulk, and it identifies the off-endpoint regime where Reading-H selection is
all-orders strong-evidence.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): \emph{scaling} -- $a_0(2e\text{-}3)/a_0(1e\text{-}3)
=1.90\approx2$ (linear in $I$); \emph{endpoint} -- $0.047/0.0385=1.22$ (same order,
undetermined); \emph{off-endpoint} -- $0.025/0.677=0.036$ ($27\times$ margin);
\emph{localisation} -- $34\times$ improvement endpoint $\to10^{-3}$.
