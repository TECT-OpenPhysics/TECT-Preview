\section*{2. The elementary antipodal lemma (proved)}

\noindent\textbf{Lemma.} For any finite antipodal set $Q=-Q$ ($N$ points) and any $t\ne0$,
$\#(Q\cap C_t)\le N/2$, where $C_t=\{x:x\cdot t=|t|^2/2\}$.

\noindent\textbf{Proof.} If $x\in C_t$ then $(-x)\cdot t=-|t|^2/2\ne|t|^2/2$ (as $t\ne0$), so
$-x\notin C_t$. Each antipodal pair $\{x,-x\}$ therefore contributes at most one point to
$C_t$; with $N/2$ pairs, $\#(Q\cap C_t)\le N/2$. $\qquad\blacksquare$

Hence $T'(Q)\le N/2$ for every antipodal competitor, and $K_{\rm floor}\le T'\le N/2$.
