The probe of Steps 1--2 covered the off-diagonal sector (O). Step 3 confirms that the other two
sectors of the Reading-H comparison decomposition --- (D) diagonal isotropy and (S) selection
floor --- extend to the non-lattice competitor class. The conclusion is that both are

on the competitor's lattice structure, so they carry over unchanged. No tier change.

\section*{2. (D) Diagonal isotropy is competitor-agnostic}

The second variation of the diagonal functional $F_0$ at the isotropic dressing $G_*$ is
(T-016 / hdiag-fullcovariance-formulation)
\[
d^2F_0=\tfrac12\int G_*(q)^{-2}(dG)^2\,d^3q+\Phi''_{\rm diag}\,(dM_{\rm tot})^2,\qquad
\Phi''_{\rm diag}=\tfrac32u_{\rm eff}.
\]
Decomposing an \emph{arbitrary} perturbation $dG(q)=\sum_{lm}a_{lm}(|q|)Y_{lm}(\hat q)$ and using
$\int Y_{lm}\,d\Omega=0$ for $l\ge1$, the rotation-invariant interaction $\Phi_{\rm diag}(M_{\rm tot})$
curves ONLY the $l=0$ sector, so the Hessian is block-diagonal:
\[
l=0:\ H_0=\tfrac12G_*^{-2}+\tfrac32u_{\rm eff}>0,\qquad
l\ge1:\ H_l=\tfrac12G_*^{-2}\ge\tfrac12r_R^2=4.64\times10^{-2}>0,
\]
the $l\ge1$ infimum analytic at the gap shell $|q|=q_0$. \textbf{This decomposition never
references the competitor's lattice structure}: a non-lattice competitor is simply another
anisotropic direction $dG$, seen by the SAME positive block-diagonal Hessian. The entropy floor
$\tfrac12r_R^2$ is rotation-invariant ($G_*$ isotropic), so it bounds the curvature toward every
angular direction equally. Hence the strict diagonal minimum $F_{\rm diag}[Q]>F_{\rm diag}[G_*]$
holds for EVERY competitor, lattice or not.

\section*{3. (S) Selection floor is competitor-agnostic}

The SC-SCOPE selection joint (scscope\_endpoint\_sweep)
\[
\mathrm{joint}=\frac{\mathrm{MARGIN}}{C_2+\mathrm{composed}/S_{\rm un}+R_{\max}C_2},\qquad
\rho=\frac{4(1-a_0)\,\mathrm{MARGIN}}{(\lambda I)^2J_{\rm eff}\,n_{\rm pack}},
\]
is built entirely from \emph{layer} quantities --- $\mathrm{MARGIN}$ (Prop-A layer margin),
$J_{\rm eff}$ (minimum-transfer envelope), $S_{\rm un}$ (sunset cap), $R_{\max}$ (realized
quartic) --- and the coherence packing bound $n_{\rm pack}$, which is the same for every
admissible competitor. It carries no explicit dependence on the competitor's additive (lattice)
structure. Numerically $\mathrm{joint}=1.040>1$ at the operating point, and the structural
saturation $\mathrm{joint}\to S_{\rm un}=1.13$ as $\rho\to\infty$ is a layer property. Hence
$\mathrm{joint}>1$ holds identically for non-lattice competitors.
