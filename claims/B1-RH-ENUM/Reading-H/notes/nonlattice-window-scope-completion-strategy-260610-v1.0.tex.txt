\section*{1. The operator decomposition (accepted) and the question}

The enacted T7 is unconditional in the comparison hypothesis H-LAYER but rests on three
standing inputs: (1) A1-KERNEL-CONV (the microscopic-theory kernel-convention
\emph{definition}, T5); (2) the lattice competitor class $\mathcal C_{\rm phys}$ (real
antipodal crystallographic, packing-bounded, coherence-resolved); (3) the operating window
$I=2\times10^{-3}$, $\mu^2\in[\times0.5,\times2]$. The operator correctly observes that an
\emph{absolute} assumption-free T7 is not meaningful --- every theorem about $F_{\rm TECT}$
is relative to the definition of $F_{\rm TECT}$ --- and proposes the realistic target
\[
\textbf{A1-relative unconditional Reading-H T7:}\quad
F[Q]-F[G_*]>0\ \ \forall\,Q\in\mathcal C_{\rm full}\setminus\{G_*\},
\]
$\mathcal C_{\rm full}$ widened to all admissible competitors (lattice and non-lattice),
over a wider window, keeping only A1. The question: how hard, and by what route?

\section*{2. Ruthless check of the ``raise DR-2 to theorem-grade'' framing}

The operator suggests non-lattice closure ``probably needs DR-2 fallback raised to
theorem-grade''. This conflates two different roles of the lattice restriction. The
off-diagonal stability is governed by the single $N$-independent ratio
\[
R_{\rm lead}(Q)=\mathrm{const}\,(1+K_{\rm floor}(Q))\,I,\qquad
\mathrm{const}=\tfrac94 u_{\rm eff}^2 B_{\max}\tfrac{2}{r_R}=23.2,\qquad
R_{\rm lead}<1\iff K_{\rm floor}<K_*=\tfrac{1}{\mathrm{const}\,I}-1\simeq20.5,
\]
where $K_{\rm floor}(Q)=\sum_{t\ne0}(\text{sum-circle occupancy})^2/N^2-1$ is the
additive-energy floor. DR-2 ($E_+\le_\varepsilon N^{2+\varepsilon}$) bounds $K_{\rm floor}$
in the \emph{worst} (arithmetic) case. The probe res5\_031 measures $K_{\rm floor}$ directly:
\begin{center}\footnotesize
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\hline
Configuration (non-lattice unless noted) & $K_{\rm floor}$ & $R_{\rm lead}$\\
\hline
random real-antipodal, $N=12\!-\!60$ & $0.75$--$0.95$ & $\sim0.09$ (margin $\times11$)\\
coplanar regular ring (max symmetry), $N=8\!-\!40$ & $0.62$--$0.93$ & $\sim0.08$\\
jittered-BCC ($10\%$ off-lattice) & $0.92$ & $\sim0.09$\\
\emph{exact} BCC (lattice) & $1.75$ & --\\
admissible lattice class (pinned) & $\le13$ & $\le0.65$\\
threshold $K_*$ & $20.5$ & $1$\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\textbf{Every non-lattice configuration tested has $K_{\rm floor}\sim1\ll13\ll20.5$, and
perturbing a config OFF the exact lattice REDUCES $K_{\rm floor}$.} The additive-energy
floor is maximised by exact arithmetic coincidence of sum-circles; a non-lattice config has
generic (non-degenerate) sum-circles, occupancy $\sim2$, hence $K_{\rm floor}\sim1$.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusion (reframing).} The lattice class is the off-diagonal
\emph{adversarial worst case}, not an easy subcase. The enacted lattice T7 already covers
the hardest competitors of the $R_{\rm lead}$ mechanism; generic non-lattice competitors
are $\times11$ more stable. Re-opening arbitrary-$Q$ DR-2 is the wrong target --- it bounds
the lattice/worst case, which is \emph{already} T7 for the lattice.

\section*{3. The genuine residual: an additive-energy extremal conjecture (T2)}

What ``$\mathcal C_{\rm full}$'' actually requires is the rigidity/extremal statement
\[
\textbf{(EXT)}\qquad
\max_{Q\in\mathcal C_{\rm full},\,|Q|=N} K_{\rm floor}(Q)\ \text{is attained within the
arithmetic (lattice) sub-class, and is}\ \le13.
\]
(EXT) is the honest bridge from ``lattice T7'' to ``$\mathcal C_{\rm full}$ T7'': if the
additive-energy floor is maximised by arithmetic structure (a Freiman/Cauchy--Davenport-type
extremal principle on $S^2$), then no admissible non-lattice competitor exceeds the lattice
bound and the lattice T7 dominates all of $\mathcal C_{\rm full}$. The probe is STRONG
EVIDENCE for (EXT); its status is T2 CONJECTURE with the pre-registered falsifier
\[
\textbf{Falsifier:}\quad\text{a real-antipodal admissible }Q\text{ with }K_{\rm floor}(Q)>13.
\]
This is a tractable additive-combinatorics statement, not the (harder, partly OPEN)
arbitrary-$Q$ $\ell^2$-decoupling programme.

\section*{4. The (I, mu^2) window is bounded by physics, not by a proof gap}

\textbf{Intensity.} R-029 (SC-SCOPE) records a physical critical intensity
$I_c\sim2.5\times10^{-3}$ \emph{beyond} the operating endpoint, where the all-orders joint
flips sign. The honest maximal intensity window is therefore $I\in(0,I_c)$ with
$I_c\sim2.5\times10^{-3}$ --- a phase boundary of the selection, NOT a gap to be closed.
Claiming $I\in(0,\infty)$ would be false. \textbf{Mass.} $\mu^2\in[\times0.5,\times2]$ was
margin-checked (SC-SCOPE worst $\times1.034$); widening it is a tractable scan plus a

\section*{5. The status of A1-KERNEL-CONV (the floor, with a standing obligation)}

A1 is a \emph{definition}, so ``A1-relative unconditional'' is the correct floor: one cannot
remove the kernel convention without removing the theory. One honesty caveat must not be

right floor; for the larger TECT/TOE ambition, ``why this kernel?'' remains a standing

structure rather than fixing it by convention). ``It is just a definition'' is correct for the
