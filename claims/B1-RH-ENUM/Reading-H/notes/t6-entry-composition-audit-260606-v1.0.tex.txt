\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

Unit U1 assembled the candidate T6 theorem by composing the Prop-A
floor with the STEP-5B budget: $\Delta F \ge 0.00432\,(1 - 1/\rho)$.
This audit re-examines the composition's interval structure and finds
ONE objection that must be UPHELD pending verification. Per the
second-order-audit discipline, the finding is recorded with its named
verification item, and the U1 proposal is flagged accordingly.

\section*{2. The finding}

Prop A's proof is a four-interval decomposition in the trial mass
($M$-space). The $+0.00432$ figure is the BAND interval's worst-case
sum (floor $P_B(M_+) = 0.0052460$ minus the sextic-only dip
$9.2595\times10^{-4}$). But on interval I$'$ ($M \ge M_+$, which
CONTAINS the gap point $M_R = 0.109414$), the proof gives strict
positivity with NO uniform margin: all $\Phi$-coefficients are
positive and $\Delta F_0 \ge 0$, with BOTH vanishing at the disordered
point ($t = 0$, $m = m^*$). Near that point the layer excess of a
shell competitor scales as
\[
\Delta F_{\rm layer} \;\approx\; \tfrac{\hat\kappa}{2}\,t^2
\;+\; \Delta F_0(m),
\qquad \hat\kappa\big|_{M = M_R} = \hat r_0(M_R) = m^* = 0.3045 ,
\]
i.e.\ $O(t^2)$, while the certified beyond-layer envelope scales as
$O(I^2)$ ($w_t \propto \lambda' I$) with the class-wide prefactor.
If the chain's certified intensities $I$ identify with the competitor
amplitude scale $t^2$, the comparison at fixed $I$ is
\[
\underbrace{\tfrac{m^*}{2}\,I - O(I^2)}_{\text{near-gap layer floor}}
\quad\text{vs}\quad
\underbrace{\frac{K(n_{\rm pack})(\lambda' I)^2 J_{\rm eff}}
{4(1 - a_0)}}_{\text{certified envelope}} ,
\]
with crossover $I_\times \approx (m^*/2)\,\big/\,c_{\rm env}$,
$c_{\rm env} = {\rm envelope}/I^2 \approx 4.3\times10^2$: numerically
$I_\times \approx 3.5\times10^{-4}$ --- INSIDE the certified window
$[4\times10^{-4}, 2\times10^{-3}]$. At face value, then, the
$0.00432\,(1-1/\rho)$ composition does NOT cover near-gap
small-amplitude competitors at the certified intensities; the U1
arithmetic is valid only where the band floor is the binding layer
protection.

\section*{3. Why this may be benign (two candidate resolutions)}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Route (a) -- self-consistent slaving + Hessian
positivity}: physical competitors are evaluated at their
self-consistent dressing (matched bookkeeping); near the gap point the
off-diagonal operator enters the HESSIAN, where the $P^2$ theorem
($D + W = D_0 + \lambda' P^2 \ge D_0 > 0$) protects the quadratic
order STRUCTURALLY --- the perturbative $O(I^2)$ subtraction is then
the wrong accounting in this regime (the correction renormalizes the
positive quadratic form rather than competing with the floor). The
verification this route requires: a one-page statement that the
second-order envelope formula's domain of use in the chain excludes
(or structurally dominates) the near-gap regime, with the
$P^2$-positivity carrying that regime.
\item \textbf{Route (b) -- crossover arithmetic with per-pattern
prefactor}: below the crossover the $O(I)$ floor dominates; at the
certified intensities the relevant prefactor for SMALL patterns is the
single-circle $K = 14$ (not the packing-maximal $107$), shrinking
$c_{\rm env}$ by $\times7.6$ and pushing $I_\times$ to
$\approx 2.7\times10^{-3} > 2\times10^{-3}$ --- the window closes.
The verification this route requires: the explicit inequality
$\tfrac{m^*}{2} I - C_2^{-1}\,O(I^2) > K(n_{\rm eff}(I))\,(\lambda'
I)^2 J_{\rm eff}/(4(1-a_0))$ across the window, with the $\Delta F_0$
curvature constant $C_2$ at the gap point (one new certified number)
and the honest $n_{\rm eff}$ accounting (large-$n$ patterns carry
larger $K$ but their near-gap layer floor also grows --- the
$\hat\kappa$ of a large star is bounded below by the same
$\hat r_0(M)$, so the trade-off must be written carefully).
\end{itemize}

Either route, if verified, restores the U1 composition with an
amended proof text (the band arithmetic for the band regime; the
near-gap statement for the small-amplitude regime). Neither is
verifiable from the migrated notes alone --- the production
bookkeeping's calibration of ``certified intensity'' against the
trial-$(t, m)$ plane is the missing input.

\section*{5. Numerical verification}

The audit's arithmetic is estimate-grade on certified constants:
$c_{\rm env}({\rm endpoint}) = 1.74\times10^{-3}/(2\times10^{-3})^2
\approx 434$; $I_\times = (0.3045/2)/434 \approx 3.5\times10^{-4}$;
route-(b) single-circle variant $c_{\rm env}/7.6 \approx 57$,
$I_\times \approx 2.7\times10^{-3}$. NO machine asserts executed this
session; the crossover arithmetic is part of the G-U1-SMALLT item.

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): consistency --- at the LAM
equilibrium ($I = 10^{-4}$, below $I_\times$ on either route) the
artefact's measured $\delta_{\rm LAM} = 6.74\times10^{-8}$ sits five
orders below the near-gap floor $(m^*/2)\times10^{-4} =
1.5\times10^{-5}$: the ACTUAL corrections of raced patterns are deep
inside the safe region, consistent with the audit's claim that the
issue is the class-wide ENVELOPE's coverage of a regime, not any
observed counter-behavior; sign-direction --- the audit errs
conservative throughout (it treats the envelope as attainable, which
the per-pattern measurements contradict by orders of magnitude);
limit case --- as $I \to 0$ the floor/envelope ratio diverges, so the
flag concerns a bounded window, not the structure of the selection.

operator-reviewed chain already considered: fourteen verdicts would

U1 author's burden, not the chain's. The flag converts an implicit

uncertainty IS the finding: a composition whose central inequality

sign-off-ready. G-U1-SMALLT item (i) is exactly this calibration.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Flagging U1 as conditional-on-one-more-item
after proposing it looks like instability.''} DISMISSED: it is the

record.
\end{itemize}
