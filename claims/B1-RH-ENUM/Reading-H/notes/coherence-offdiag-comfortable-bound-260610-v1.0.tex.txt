\section*{1. The gap this closes}

The C_full off-diagonal closure (res5\_033/035) rested on the antipodal lemma $T'\le N/2$ with
the packing $N\le n_{\rm pack}\approx41$, giving $K_{\rm floor}\le20$ and a THIN margin
$\times1.026$. That bound ignores a stronger constraint already in the admissible-class
definition: \emph{coherence resolution}. This note proves an elementary uniform bound from
coherence that is tighter by a factor $\sim2$ and makes the closure comfortable.

\section*{2. The coherence circle-packing lemma}

\noindent\textbf{Setup.} The admissible class is coherence-resolved: any two points of $Q$ are
geodesically separated by $\ge\theta_{\min}$, the cap-packing bound being $n_{\rm pack}=
16/\theta_{\min}^2$ (caps of radius $\theta_{\min}/2$). A sum-circle $C_t=\{x:x\cdot t=|t|^2/2\}
\cap S^2$ ($t\ne0$) is a Euclidean circle of radius $\rho=\sqrt{Q_0^2-|t|^2/4}\le Q_0$.

\noindent\textbf{Lemma.} For every admissible $Q$ and every $t\ne0$,
\[
\boxed{\ \#(Q\cap C_t)\ \le\ \Bigl\lfloor\frac{2\pi}{\theta_{\min}}\Bigr\rfloor\ }\qquad\Rightarrow\qquad
T'(Q)=\max_{t\ne0}\#(Q\cap C_t)\le\Bigl\lfloor\frac{2\pi}{\theta_{\min}}\Bigr\rfloor .
\]

\noindent\textbf{Proof.} Points of $Q$ on $C_t$ are pairwise geodesically $\ge\theta_{\min}$,
hence pairwise Euclidean $\ge d:=2Q_0\sin(\theta_{\min}/2)$. If $k$ points lie on a circle of
radius $\rho$ with pairwise Euclidean distance $\ge d$, then the smallest adjacent gap satisfies
$2\rho\sin(\pi/k)\ge d$, i.e.\ $k\le\pi/\arcsin\!\bigl(d/(2\rho)\bigr)$. Since $\rho\le Q_0$,
\[
\frac{d}{2\rho}\ \ge\ \frac{d}{2Q_0}=\sin\frac{\theta_{\min}}{2}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\arcsin\frac{d}{2\rho}\ \ge\ \frac{\theta_{\min}}{2}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
k\ \le\ \frac{2\pi}{\theta_{\min}} . \qquad\blacksquare
\]

\noindent The bound is geometric and uniform: it uses only the separation $\theta_{\min}$ and
$\rho\le Q_0$, with NO lattice / additive / number-theoretic input. It applies identically to
lattice and non-lattice competitors.

\section*{3. The uniform numerical bound and adversarial check}

Across the certified window $\theta_{\min}\in[0.596,0.637]$, so $2\pi/\theta_{\min}\le10.54$ and
\[
T'(Q)\le 10\qquad\text{for every admissible competitor, uniformly on }\mathcal R .
\]
Adversarial search (res5\_036): a maximal admissible single-latitude-ring-pair reaches $T'=8<10$
(near-great rings that would give higher occupancy violate coherence --- the antipodal mirror
collides near the equator); a random coherence-resolved config has $T'=2$. The bound $10$ is never
exceeded.

\section*{4. Comfortable off-diagonal; the selection boundary binds}

With R-025 ($K_{\rm floor}\le T'$):
\[
K_{\rm floor}\le T'\le10\ \Rightarrow\ R_{\rm lead}=23.2\,(1+K_{\rm floor})\,I\le23.2\cdot11\cdot I .
\]
At the operating intensity $I=2\times10^{-3}$, $R_{\rm lead}\le0.510$ (margin $\times1.96$); at the
selection cap $I_c^{\rm sel}=2.41\times10^{-3}$, $R_{\rm lead}\le0.615<1$. The off-diagonal cap is
\[
I_{\rm off}^{\rm coh}=\frac{1}{23.2\cdot11}=3.92\times10^{-3}\ >\ I_c^{\rm sel}=2.41\times10^{-3},
\]
so the SELECTION boundary binds (exactly as for the lattice class), and the C_full operating
region is the FULL Step-1 selection region $\mathcal R$ ($20\%$ headroom at the operating point),
NOT the thin off-diagonal-bound region ($2.6\%$).

\noindent\textbf{Consequence.} The C_full off-diagonal margin is COMFORTABLE ($\times1.96$), and
the thin $\times1.026$ of res5\_033/035 is superseded as an artefact of the loose $T'\le N/2$. The
additive-energy extremal conjecture EXT (target $K_{\rm floor}\sim3$, margin $\times5.4$) is now
UNNECESSARY for comfort; it remains T2, an optional further tightening.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Is $\theta_{\min}$ really the pairwise separation for all of $Q$?''}
Yes --- the admissible class is coherence-resolved with caps of radius $\theta_{\min}/2$ (centres
$\ge\theta_{\min}$ apart); $n_{\rm pack}=16/\theta_{\min}^2$ is the matching cap count.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Could a sum-circle have $\rho>Q_0$ and hold more points?''} No ---
$\rho=\sqrt{Q_0^2-|t|^2/4}\le Q_0$ always; the great-circle limit ($\rho=Q_0$, $t\to0$) is the
worst case and gives the bound $2\pi/\theta_{\min}$.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Does the bound hold across the whole window?''} Yes --- $2\pi/
\theta_{\min}\le10.54$ over the certified $(I,\mu^2)$ range (res5\_036 scan), so $T'\le10$
uniformly; $\theta_{\min}$ grows with $\mu^2,I$, tightening the bound.
\end{itemize}
