\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

The U1 candidate theorem quantifies over the H-ADM-COH-amended class;
B1's evidence quantifies over the enumerated ensembles. For the two to
compose without a gap, every enumerated pattern must BE an
amended-class member (else the races would be evidence about excluded
objects). This note performs that recheck with exact geometry, and
extracts the extremal finding. Scope: single-shell readings of the
enumerated set + the Math437 gallery; the two-shell ensembles inherit
per-shell (their within-shell stars are from the same list).

\section*{3. The extremal finding and the pre-registered stress test}

The decagonal star is the EXTREMAL in-class pattern: margin
$\times1.042$ at the anchor, $\times1.0018$ at the endpoint --- it sits
essentially AT the class boundary at $I = 2\times10^{-3}$
($0.62832$ vs $0.62716$ rad, $0.18\%$). Consequences:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Stress test (pre-registered)}: the decagonal star at the
endpoint intensity is the canonical worst-geometry probe for the
STEP-5B falsifier set --- any future tightening of the chain should be
exercised on it first. (Energetically it is a WEAK competitor --- the
Math437 gallery records ZERO non-trivial 4-wave resonances for it ---
but for the beyond-layer machinery it is the packing-extremal member.)
\item \textbf{Boundary softness}: the class boundary is not a cliff ---
the AddC lemma bounds any boundary-crossing restructuring by
$c_{\rm ind}I^2$ = margin$/\times33$ or better, so the decagonal's
near-boundary position creates no discontinuity threat to the U1
theorem.
\item \textbf{Sea reclassification, formalized}: $k$-fold planar stars
with axis spacing $\pi/k < \theta_{\min}$ ($k \ge 6$ in-plane axes,
i.e.\ 12-fold symmetric and denser; at intensities slightly above the
endpoint even the 10-fold) are NOT class members --- their sub-cutoff
structure belongs to the Gaussian sea. This formalizes why dense
angular stars never surfaced as estimator threats in the races: they
are not independent-reading competitors at the coherence resolution.
\end{itemize}

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``If the decagonal is only $0.18\%$ inside the
class at the endpoint, a tiny upward revision of $\theta_{\min}$ (e.g.\
the criterion-band ambiguity $c_\theta \in [1, \pi]$) EXPELS it --- and
then the B1 races would include an out-of-class pattern, breaking the
composition.''} DISMISSED with the composition's actual logic: the U1
theorem quantifies over the amended class; if a raced pattern exits the
class under a stricter criterion, the race for it becomes evidence
about a sea-reclassified configuration --- harmless surplus, not a gap
(the class theorem never RELIED on the races; U1 made them
corroboration). The composition requires class members to be COVERED
by the theorem (they are), not that every raced object stay a member.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The two-shell inheritance is hand-waved:
cross-shell angular proximity (a reading on shell 1 nearly parallel to
a reading on shell 2) could violate the coherence criterion in a way
the per-shell table misses.''} VALID-with-mitigation: the coherence
criterion of AddB is built from the TRANSVERSE beat scale on a shell;
for cross-shell pairs the beat acquires the RADIAL separation $\Delta q
\ge$ the shell gap, which exceeds $1/\xi$ by construction in the
two-shell ensembles (Lemma J's $\times1.70$ floor operates at exactly
this scale) --- nearly-parallel cross-shell readings are resolved
radially. The formal one-paragraph statement is registered for the
follow-up script's note companion; until then the two-shell row of this
recheck is T3.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Pre-registering the decagonal as the stress
test could bias future verification toward a pattern already known to
be energetically weak (zero 4-wave resonances), creating false
comfort.''} VALID-with-mitigation: the stress-test role is for the
BEYOND-LAYER machinery (packing/energy-bound extremality), where
resonance count is irrelevant; for the LAYER-energetics stress test the
chain's existing worst cases (BCC's 144 resonant excess; the band
interval of Prop A) remain the designated probes. The note records both
roles explicitly so neither substitutes for the other.
\end{itemize}
