\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This is a self-contained referee document for one result: within the certified operating
region, the Reading-H vacuum selection of TECT prefers the isotropic Gaussian-Hartree layer
$G_*$ over every admissible competitor (lattice and non-lattice), given the A1 microscopic
kernel convention. It collects every definition, lemma, and numerical check needed to verify
or attack the result without consulting other files.

\noindent\textbf{Claimed} (tier T7-SCOPE, $\mathcal C_{\rm full}$, comfortable off-diagonal):
\[
F[Q]-F[G_*]>0\qquad\forall\,Q\in\mathcal C_{\rm full}\setminus\{G_*\},\quad (I,\mu^2)\in\mathcal R,
\quad\text{given A1.}
\]
\textbf{Not claimed:} no statement is made for intensities outside $\mathcal R$, for the A1
convention being forced rather than chosen, or for a fully unconditional / all-competitor T7.

\section*{2. The object}

TECT's vacuum-selection functional, restricted to the diagonal Gaussian-Hartree layer, assigns
to a momentum-space condensate pattern $Q$ (a finite point set on the Brazovskii shell
$|k|=q_0$, with amplitudes) a free energy $F[Q]$. The reference $G_*$ is the rotation-invariant
(isotropic) dressing on the shell. The A1 kernel convention fixes the microscopic theory:
$r_{\rm braz}=K(q_0)=\mu^2$, with the Lagrangian constants $u=-0.86$, $v=3.24$, $q_0=0.6802$,
the gap point $r_R=r_R(\mu^2)$, and $M_R$ the dressed shell mass.

\noindent\textbf{Admissible class $\mathcal C_{\rm full}$.} A competitor $Q$ is admissible iff it
is (i) real antipodal, $Q=-Q$; (ii) packing-bounded, $|Q|\le n_{\rm pack}=16/\theta_{\min}^2$;
(iii) coherence-resolved, any two points geodesically separated by $\ge\theta_{\min}$, where
$\theta_{\min}=\sqrt{\widehat r}\,/(2q_0^2\sqrt c)$ is the coherent angular resolution
($\widehat r=r_R+2\lambda I$, $\lambda=3u+30vM_R$, $c$ the gradient stiffness). Crystallographic
(lattice) shells and arbitrary non-lattice configurations are both admitted. At the operating
anchor $(I,\mu^2)=(2\times10^{-3},0.005)$, $\theta_{\min}=0.627$ and $n_{\rm pack}=40.68$.

\noindent\textbf{Operating region $\mathcal R$} (Sec.~7): $\mu^2\in(0,\mu^2_{\max})$,
$I\in(0,I_c^{\rm sel}(\mu^2))$.

\section*{3. The decomposition}

Write $F[Q]-F[G_*]$ as the second variation at $G_*$ plus higher cumulants, split into three
sectors: (D) the diagonal (isotropy) Hessian; (O) the off-diagonal (condensate / additive)
Hessian; (S) the third-cumulant selection floor (SC-SCOPE). The result follows from positivity
of each sector over $\mathcal C_{\rm full}$.

\section*{4. (D) Diagonal isotropy is competitor-agnostic}

The second variation of the diagonal functional $F_0$ at $G_*$ is
\[
\delta^2F_0=\tfrac12\!\int G_*(q)^{-2}(\delta G)^2\,d^3q+\Phi''_{\rm diag}\,(\delta M_{\rm tot})^2,
\qquad \Phi''_{\rm diag}=\tfrac32 u_{\rm eff},
\]
with $u_{\rm eff}=u+10vM_R>0$ and $M_{\rm tot}=\int G$. Expanding an arbitrary perturbation
$\delta G(q)=\sum_{lm}a_{lm}(|q|)Y_{lm}(\hat q)$ and using $\int Y_{lm}\,d\Omega=0$ for $l\ge1$,
the rotation-invariant interaction $\Phi_{\rm diag}(M_{\rm tot})$ curves only the $l=0$ sector, so
the Hessian is block-diagonal in angular momentum:
\[
l=0:\ \tfrac12 G_*^{-2}+\tfrac32 u_{\rm eff}>0;\qquad
l\ge1:\ \tfrac12 G_*^{-2}\ge\tfrac12 r_R^2=4.64\times10^{-2}>0,
\]
the $l\ge1$ infimum attained analytically at the gap shell $|q|=q_0$. The decomposition uses no
lattice / additive structure: a non-lattice competitor is merely another direction $\delta G$,
seen by the same positive block-diagonal Hessian. Hence the diagonal minimum is strict for every
competitor.

\paragraph{Globality caveat.} The conclusion that the diagonal minimum is strict for every
competitor uses T-016 as a global/convex diagonal-minimum statement for the Gaussian-Hartree
diagonal functional. If T-016 is read only as a local Hessian statement, then Sec.~4 proves
strict local diagonal stability; the global comparison invokes the global convexity component
of T-016.

\section*{5. (O) Off-diagonal: the elementary closure}

The off-diagonal stability ratio is, with the additive-energy floor
$K_{\rm floor}(Q)=\sum_{t\ne0}r(t)^2/N^2-1$ ($r(t)=\#\{(a,b)\in Q^2:a+b=t\}$, $N=|Q|$),
\[
R_{\rm lead}(Q)=\mathrm{const}\,(1+K_{\rm floor}(Q))\,I,\qquad
\mathrm{const}=\tfrac94 u_{\rm eff}^2 B_{\max}\tfrac{2}{r_R}=23.2,
\]
$N$-independent, and the off-diagonal Hessian is positive iff $R_{\rm lead}<1$, i.e.
$K_{\rm floor}<K_*=1/(\mathrm{const}\,I)-1\simeq20.5$. Two elementary lemmas bound $K_{\rm floor}$.

\noindent\textbf{Lemma 1 (additive-energy floor $\le$ occupancy).} $K_{\rm floor}(Q)\le T'(Q)$,
where $T'(Q)=\max_{t\ne0}\#(Q\cap C_t)$ is the maximal occupancy of a proper sum-circle
$C_t=\{x:x\cdot t=|t|^2/2\}\cap S^2$. (This is the unconditional sum-circle bound
$E_+(Q)\le(1+T')N^2$; both summands of $a+b=t$ lie on $C_t$.)

\noindent\textbf{Lemma 2 (coherence circle-packing).} For an admissible (coherence-resolved)
$Q$ and any $t\ne0$, $\#(Q\cap C_t)\le\lfloor 2\pi/\theta_{\min}\rfloor$. \emph{Proof.} $C_t$ is a
Euclidean circle of radius $\rho=\sqrt{q_0^2-|t|^2/4}\le q_0$. Its $Q$-points are pairwise
geodesically $\ge\theta_{\min}$, hence pairwise Euclidean $\ge d:=2q_0\sin(\theta_{\min}/2)$.
Let the $k$ points be cyclically ordered on $C_t$ and let $\Delta_{\min}$ be the smallest
adjacent angular gap; the gaps sum to $2\pi$, so $\Delta_{\min}\le2\pi/k$. Coherence makes
every adjacent chord $\ge d$, hence $d\le2\rho\sin(\Delta_{\min}/2)\le2\rho\sin(\pi/k)$,
giving $k\le\pi/\arcsin(d/2\rho)$. Using $\rho\le q_0$, $d/2\rho\ge\sin(\theta_{\min}/2)$,
so $\arcsin(d/2\rho)\ge\theta_{\min}/2$ and $k\le2\pi/\theta_{\min}$. $\qquad\blacksquare$

\noindent Across $\mathcal R$, $\theta_{\min}\ge0.596$ so $2\pi/\theta_{\min}\le10.54$ and
\[
K_{\rm floor}\le T'\le 10\ \Rightarrow\ R_{\rm lead}\le23.2\cdot11\cdot I\le0.615<1
\quad(\text{at }I=2\times10^{-3}:\ R_{\rm lead}\le0.510,\ \text{margin }\times1.96).
\]
The off-diagonal cap $I_{\rm off}^{\rm coh}=1/(23.2\cdot11)=3.92\times10^{-3}$ exceeds the
selection cap $I_c^{\rm sel}=2.41\times10^{-3}$, so the selection boundary binds and the operating
region is the full Step-1 region (Sec.~7). The bound is uniform for lattice and non-lattice; a
direct adversarial search finds the maximal admissible occupancy is $8<10$.

\section*{6. (S) Selection floor is competitor-agnostic}

The SC-SCOPE third-cumulant survival is governed by the joint ratio
$\mathrm{joint}=\mathrm{MARGIN}/(C_2+\mathrm{composed}/S_{\rm un}+R_{\max}C_2)$, built from layer
quantities (the Prop-A margin $\mathrm{MARGIN}$, the minimum-transfer envelope $J_{\rm eff}$, the
sunset cap $S_{\rm un}=1.13$, the realized quartic $R_{\max}=0.385$) and the coherence packing
$n_{\rm pack}$ -- the same for every admissible competitor, with no dependence on the competitor's
additive structure. Numerically $\mathrm{joint}=1.04>1$ at the operating point, with the structural
cap $\mathrm{joint}\to S_{\rm un}=1.13$. Hence the selection floor is positive for non-lattice
competitors identically.

\paragraph{Worst-case-bound caveat.} The competitor-agnostic conclusion for (S) assumes that
$J_{\rm eff}$ and $n_{\rm pack}$ are worst-case layer/coherence bounds over the full
admissible class $\mathcal C_{\rm full}$, not merely over the crystallographic subclass.
Under this definition the selection joint carries no competitor-specific additive structure.

\section*{7. Window certification}

The Prop-A margin $\mathrm{MARGIN}(\mu^2)=\mathrm{PB}(M_+)-\mathrm{DIP}$ requires the upper PD
branch $M_+=(-3u+\sqrt{9u^2-60v\mu^2})/(30v)$, i.e.\ $\mu^2<\mu^2_{\max}=3u^2/(20v)=0.0342$. The
selection critical intensity $I_c^{\rm sel}(\mu^2)$ solves $\mathrm{joint}=1$; it is monotone
increasing in $\mu^2$ (implicit function theorem, with $\mathrm{joint}$ decreasing in $I$ --
analytic, the denominator is linear-increasing in $C_2\sim I^2$ with coefficient
$1+R_{\max}-1/S_{\rm un}=0.500>0$ -- and increasing in $\mu^2$), with minimum
$I_c^{\rm sel}=2.41\times10^{-3}$ on $[\times0.5,\times2]$. The certified region is
\[
\mathcal R=\bigl\{(I,\mu^2):0<\mu^2<\mu^2_{\max}=0.0342,\ 0<I<I_c^{\rm sel}(\mu^2)\bigr\},
\]
with the operating point $(2\times10^{-3},0.005)$ interior (20\% intensity headroom).

(D) strict diagonal minimum (Sec.~4) $+$ (O) $R_{\rm lead}\le0.510<1$ comfortable (Sec.~5) $+$ (S)

competitor. The sole standing input is the A1 kernel convention (a definition of the microscopic

\section*{10. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{Attack the coherence bound.} Lemma 2 rests on $\rho\le q_0$ and pairwise
$\ge\theta_{\min}$; both are definitional. A near-great sum-circle that could hold $>10$ points
forces its antipodal mirror within $<\theta_{\min}$ near the equator, violating admissibility
(verified: maximal admissible occupancy $8$). To falsify, exhibit an admissible $Q$ with a
sum-circle of $>10$ points.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{Attack the A1 input.} The result is relative to the A1 kernel convention;
it does not claim A1 is forced. A referee who rejects A1 rejects the theory's definition, not this
proof.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{Attack the scope.} The claim is confined to $\mathcal R$ and to
$\mathcal C_{\rm full}$ (real antipodal, packing- and coherence-bounded). It is not a global or
all-intensity statement; SC-SCOPE has a structural near-critical cap $S_{\rm un}=1.13$.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): $K_*=20.55$; coherence bound $T'\le10$ (max admissible $8$);
$R_{\rm lead}\le0.510$ ($\times1.96$); $I_c^{\rm sel}=2.41\times10^{-3}$; $\mu^2_{\max}=0.0342$;
entropy floor $4.64\times10^{-2}>0$; joint $1.04>1$. All from res5\_032--036.
