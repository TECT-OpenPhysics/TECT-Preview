\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

B1 asserts $\Delta F[\mathcal R] = F[\mathcal R] - F[\mathcal R_H] > 0$ for every
enumerated reading $\mathcal R$ (single-shell LAM/HEX/FCC/BCC) at $\mu^2=0.005$,
at ESTIMATOR grade -- point estimates from the fast quadrature pipeline with no
certified error. The ESTIMATOR-UPGRADE gate asks for a CONTROLLED error bound.
This note supplies it for the single-shell readings against the dominant
numerical knob (the loop-integral quadrature). Scope: single-shell ensemble;
two-shell + the $\delta I$/grid knobs are registered as the same-method
follow-up.

\section*{2. What "controlled error" means here}

$\Delta F_{\mathcal R}(A)$ is even in the condensate amplitude $A$ with
$\Delta F_{\mathcal R}(0) = 0$ exactly ($A=0$ is the Reading-H reference). The
selection holds iff, for every reading, (a) $A=0$ is a STRICT local minimum --
the curvature
\[
\kappa_{\mathcal R} := \Delta F_{\mathcal R}''(0)
= \frac{2\,\Delta F_{\mathcal R}(h)}{h^2} + O(h^2) \; > \; 0,
\]
and (b) there is no DEEPER minimum at $A>0$ (the amplitude scan's global best is
at $A=0$: no condensate). A reading with $\kappa_{\mathcal R}<0$ or an interior
$\Delta F<0$ minimum would FALSIFY the selection. $\kappa_{\mathcal R}$ is
grid-independent (unlike a "$\min_{A>0}\Delta F$", which scales with the
amplitude-grid spacing), so it is the correct controlled-error margin.

\section*{3. Method and result}

Each verdict is computed at the production fast pipeline ($M_{\rm fast}$, grid at
$N_{\rm pt}=6000$) and at a HIGH-resolution pipeline ($M$ by direct quadrature at
$N_{\rm pt}=20000$, installed by monkeypatching Math424's $M_{\rm fast}$ so its
OWN certified \texttt{dF\_reading}/\texttt{scan\_reading} are reused -- no formula
re-transcription). $h=0.005$ with a half-step $h/2$ quadratic-regime check.

Every reading has $\kappa_{\mathcal R}>0$ with the M-quadrature envelope
$<0.1\%$ of $\kappa$ (five orders below $\kappa$); the binding (smallest) reading
is LAM, $\kappa = 0.851 \pm 1.7\times10^{-5}$. Both pipelines find every reading
collapses to $A=0$ (no condensate: the global $\Delta F$ minimum is at $A=0$).
The $\Delta F(0)=0$ identity holds exactly in both pipelines. Thus the
single-shell selection margins are CONTROLLED-ERROR positive: $A=0$ is a strict
minimum for every reading and the verdict is not a fast-quadrature artefact.

\section*{4. What this advances and what remains}

ADVANCED: the single-shell enumerated $\Delta F>0$ verdict is now controlled-error
(curvature-certified, no condensate, M-quadrature envelope $<0.1\%$). REMAINING
for full ESTIMATOR-UPGRADE closure: (i) the two-shell ensemble (same curvature +
no-condensate method); (ii) the $\delta I$ quadrature and amplitude-grid knobs

$M$ enters $\Delta F$ several times and drives the gap solve). These are
registered follow-ups; the gate stays OPEN pending them and operator sign-off.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate / self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md 6.3, 6 code-discipline)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Only the M-quadrature was varied.''} VALID and scoped:
$M$ is the dominant knob (multiple appearances + drives the gap solve, which also
uses $M_{\rm fast}$ and is therefore refined here too). $\delta I$ and the
amplitude grid are named as follow-ups; the curvature is grid-independent, and
the no-condensate scan uses the same 121-point grid in both pipelines so a
condensate would have to hide from BOTH.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``$\kappa = 2\Delta F(h)/h^2$ may be $A^4$-contaminated.''}
DISMISSED: the $h$ vs $h/2$ check agrees to $<2\%$ for every reading at $h=0.005$,
confirming the finite difference measures $\Delta F''(0)$, not the quartic.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``No formula re-transcription -- sure?''} The hires
pipeline reuses Math424's OWN \texttt{dF\_reading}/\texttt{scan\_reading} via a
one-line monkeypatch of $M_{\rm fast}$; the $\Delta F$ algebra is never copied,
eliminating transcription risk (code-discipline rule 1).
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): magnitude -- envelopes $\sim10^{-5}$ vs
$\kappa\sim1$--$5$ ($<0.1\%$); limit-case -- $\Delta F(0)=0$ exactly in both
pipelines; sign-direction -- $\kappa>0$ means $A=0$ is a strict minimum, the
selection-preserving direction; consistency -- BCC has the largest curvature yet
its $A^4$ contamination forced the smaller $h$, consistent with a steeper
landscape.
