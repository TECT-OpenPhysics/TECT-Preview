\section*{2. The lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Near-gap protection -- sketch]
For every admissible pattern $\mathcal P$ of intensity $I$, within the
chain's stated convention, the competitor's full fluctuation Hessian
obeys
\[
H_{\mathcal P} \;=\; D + W \;=\; D_0 + \lambda' P^2 \;\ge\; D_0
\;=\; H_{\mathcal R_H},
\qquad
D_0 = r_R + C(k^2 - q_0^2)^2 ,
\]
and therefore, by the operator monotonicity of $\ln$ on positive
operators,
\[
F_{\rm fluct}[\mathcal P] - F_{\rm fluct}[\mathcal R_H]
\;=\; \frac{1}{2V}\,\operatorname{tr}\!\bigl[\ln(D_0 + \lambda' P^2)
- \ln D_0\bigr] \;\ge\; 0 .
\]
The fluctuation sector --- diagonal Hartree shift ($+2\lambda' I$) and
off-diagonal Bloch channel ($W$'s off-diagonal part) TOGETHER --- can
never lower a competitor below $\mathcal R_H$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
$D = D_0 + 2\lambda' I\,{\rm Id}$ by the convention; $W = \lambda'(P^2
- 2I\,{\rm Id})$ by the $P^2$ theorem; adding gives the exact identity
$D + W = D_0 + \lambda' P^2$ (this is the chain's own display, v1.7).
$P$ is self-adjoint (multiplication by the real field $F(x)$, v1.8),
so $P^2 \ge 0$; $\lambda' > 0$ is certified at the anchor band. Hence
$H_{\mathcal P} \ge D_0 > 0$. Operator monotonicity of $\ln$
($A \ge B > 0 \Rightarrow \ln A \ge \ln B$, Loewner) gives
$\operatorname{tr}\ln H_{\mathcal P} \ge \operatorname{tr}\ln D_0$,
and the Gaussian fluctuation free energy is $+\tfrac1{2V}
\operatorname{tr}\ln(\text{Hessian})$. $\square$
\end{proof}

\section*{3. Consequence: G-U1-SMALLT closes at the regime level}

The U9 crossover ($O(I)$ floor vs $O(I^2)$ envelope) compared two
BOUNDS; the lemma shows the underlying ENERGIES cannot cross in the
fluctuation sector at all. The competitor's total matched excess
decomposes as
\[
\Delta F[\mathcal P] \;=\;
\underbrace{\Phi(t)}_{\ge 0 \text{ on I}'}
\;+\; \underbrace{\Delta F_0\text{-type layer excursion}}_{\ge 0}
\;+\; \underbrace{F_{\rm fluct}[\mathcal P] -
F_{\rm fluct}[\mathcal R_H]}_{\ge 0 \text{ (this lemma)}} ,
\]
so on interval I$'$ (all $\Phi$ coefficients positive; contains the
gap point) EVERY term is nonnegative and the selection cannot flip ---
no margin needed. The role of the STEP-5B bound is thereby clarified
to exactly the chain's original threat model: on the BAND/sub-band
intervals, where the layer proof spends the $P_B$ floors against the
dips, the off-diagonal correction must not consume the residual
$+0.00432$ --- and there the U1 composed arithmetic stands as written.
The U9 estimated crossover inside the certified window is thus an
artifact of bounding the protected sector instead of using its
positivity.

\section*{5. Numerical verification}

No new machine numbers. The lemma is structural; its inputs
($\lambda' = 8.0551 > 0$; the $P^2$ identity; $r_R$) are certified in
the existing artefact (192/192). The small-$I$ consistency target for
R-U10-3: first order of the trace $= \lambda'\,\langle F^2\rangle\,
M(r_R)$-form $= 2\lambda' I M_R$-scale $\approx 1.76\,I$ --- linear
and positive, dominating the $O(I^2)$ off-diagonal at all certified
intensities (at $I = 2\times10^{-3}$: $3.5\times10^{-3}$ vs the
$1.7\times10^{-3}$ envelope), consistent with the positivity.

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): limit case --- at $I \to 0$,
$P \to 0$ and the inequality degenerates to equality (the competitor
IS $\mathcal R_H$), as it must; sign-direction --- $\lambda' > 0$ is
the load-bearing sign (``pattern Hartree RAISES the diagonal,'' a
certified artefact claim with its own assert); had $\lambda' < 0$ the
lemma would fail and the U9 flag would stand --- the lemma's validity
is pinned to a machine-asserted sign, not an assumption; consistency
--- the first-order trace coefficient $\approx 1.76$ per unit $I$
exceeds the U9 floor coefficient $m^* = 0.3045$ per unit $I$: the
structural protection is STRONGER than the layer floor the U9 audit
was comparing against, explaining why the bound-level crossover was
an artifact.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``The lemma proves the fluctuation sector
never helps competitors --- but then why does the chain bound
$|\delta F_{\rm off}|$ at all? If the total is positive, STEP-5B was
unnecessary.''} DISMISSED: the two statements protect DIFFERENT
regimes. On the band intervals the layer proof itself goes NEGATIVE
in places (the dips) and survives by the $P_B$ floors --- there the
decomposition books the diagonal Hartree gain inside the layer
excursion, and the residual question is whether the off-diagonal
piece (which is genuinely sign-indefinite within the split) can eat
the remaining $+0.00432$; that is STEP-5B and it was needed. The
lemma adds the complementary statement for the regime where the
floors vanish. Both are necessary; neither subsumes the other.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``Operator monotonicity needs $D_0 + \lambda'
P^2$ and $D_0$ to act on the same space with the trace difference
well-defined (the operators are unbounded; $\operatorname{tr}\ln$ is
formal).''} VALID-with-mitigation: per unit volume the difference of
the two $\operatorname{tr}\ln$'s is the standard regularized
log-determinant ratio, finite because $\lambda' P^2$ is bounded
(multiplication by $\lambda'(F^2)$, $\|F\|_\infty^2 \le 4S^2$ finite
for finite patterns) and relatively trace-class against $D_0^{-1}$
decay ($k^{-4}$); the chain's own Lemma B manipulates exactly this
object. The one-paragraph regularization statement folds into
R-U10-2.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``R-U10-1 could be where the problem hides:
if the $r_R$-fixed convention is only first-order, the identity
$D + W = D_0 + \lambda' P^2$ with $D_0 = H_{R_H}$ is approximate and
the positivity could fail at $O(I^2)$ --- the very order of the
threat.''} VALID-with-mitigation --- this is exactly why R-U10-1 is
named rather than waved through: the artefact's own second-order
calibration ($\delta_{\rm second\ order} = 6.7\times10^{-8}$ at the
LAM point, versus the protected linear gain $\sim 1.76 I = 1.8\times
10^{-4}$ there) measures the feedback at $\times2600$ below the
protection at the calibration point, strongly indicating the
convention's slack is harmless; the one-page writeup must make that
class-wide. Until then the lemma is T3 and the U9 flag stands in
REDUCED form (two pages, not an open regime).
\end{itemize}
