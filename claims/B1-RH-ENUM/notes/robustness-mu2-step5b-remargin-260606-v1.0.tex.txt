\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This note closes ROBUSTNESS-MU2 by settling its last residual -- the exact
off-anchor re-margining of the STEP-5B beyond-layer closure. Combined with
the A=0-uniqueness robustness (note robustness-mu2-offanchor), the
Reading-H selection is established robust on the $\mu^2 \in [\times0.5,
\times2]$ neighbourhood. The exact layer margin $m(\mu^2)$ is bounded
(not recomputed); this is a $<$few-\% effect that cannot break the
$\times2.55$ endpoint floor (section 4).

\section*{2. The off-anchor re-margin}

The AddE de-thinned closure margin is
\[
\mathrm{ratio}(\mu^2, I)
= \frac{K_{\rm budget}}{K(n_{\rm pack})}
= \frac{4(1-a_0)\,m\,/\,[(\lambda' I)^2 J_{\rm eff}]}
{8 + 4\sqrt{14}\,\sqrt{n_{\rm pack}}} ,
\]
with $\lambda'(\mu^2) = 3u + 30vM_R$, $\hat r(\mu^2) = r_R + 2\lambda' I$,
$a_0 = 2\lambda' I/\hat r$, $\theta_{\min} = \sqrt{\hat r}/(2q_0^2\sqrt
C)$, $n_{\rm pack} = 16/\theta_{\min}^2$, and the minimum-transfer
envelope $J_{\rm eff} = J(2q_0\sin(\theta_{\min}/2))$ recomputed at the
off-anchor $\hat r$. Everything except $J_{\rm eff}$ is closed-form; the
single envelope integral per $(\mu^2, I)$ is the only cost.

The worst ratio over the whole neighbourhood and all intensities is
$\times2.55$ (the $\times0.5$, $I = 2\times10^{-3}$ corner) -- the
closure holds throughout with room. The margins are monotone increasing
in $\mu^2$, so the anchor sits near the thinnest point.

\section*{3. The layer-margin floor is preserved}

The ratio uses the anchor layer margin $m = 4.32\times10^{-3}$. Its own
$\mu^2$-robustness is the Prop-A $P_B$ floor: $M_R/M_c > 4.11$ throughout
the neighbourhood (robustness-mu2-offanchor, 9/9), so the floor condition
that makes $m > 0$ is preserved, and $m(\mu^2)$ stays positive and
$O(\text{anchor})$ as the constants drift $< 2\%$.

\section*{4. Why the un-recomputed $m(\mu^2)$ cannot break closure}

The endpoint floor is $\times2.55$. The ratio scales linearly in
$m(\mu^2)$; the constants entering $m$ (via $P_B(M_R) - \mathrm{dip}_B$)
drift $< 2\%$ across the $\times4$ band, so $m(\mu^2) = m_{\rm anchor}(1 +
O(2\%))$. Even a conservative $10\%$ drop in $m$ leaves the endpoint at
$\times2.3 > 1$. A break would require $m$ to fall by $> 60\%$, impossible
while $M_R/M_c > 4$ holds. The closure is therefore robust to the exact
$m(\mu^2)$ variation; recomputing $m$ exactly would only sharpen the
already-comfortable margin.
