\section*{2. The candidate T6 theorem}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Candidate Theorem (Reading-H selection, conditional).} At the
corrected canonical operating point $r_{\rm braz} = \mu^2 = 0.005$, for

$\{\text{H-LAYER}^{\flat},\ \text{H-A0},\ \text{H-ADM-COH},\
\text{SC-SCOPE}\}$ enumerated in section~3, where
H-LAYER$^{\flat}$ denotes the layer hypothesis with its beyond-layer
residual DISCHARGED on the amended class by the STEP-5B closure.
\end{quote}

\noindent Assembly chain (every link on disk and operator-reviewed):
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Layer-level positivity (B2, T6 on \{H-LAYER, H-A0\}).}
Proposition A (Math437 v1.2, certified by Math442 dual audit + operator
sign-off): for every admissible pattern and every PD trial,
$F_{\rm var}(t,m) > F_{\rm var}(0,m^*) = F_{\rm var}[\mathcal R_H]$,
with the four-interval quadrature-free floor chain giving the worst-case
band margin $+0.00432$ (intervals: $+0.00432$ / $+0.00417$ / $+0.00467$;
the binding one is the band).
\item \textbf{Beyond-layer domination (B5 chain, STEP-5B
CLOSED-CONDITIONAL, verdict \#14 + AddE polish).} For every pattern of
the H-ADM-COH-amended class, at the three anchor intensities,
\[
\bigl|\delta F_{\rm off}(\mathcal P)\bigr|
\;\le\; \frac{K(n_{\rm pack})\,(\lambda' I)^2\,J_{\rm eff}}{4(1-a_0)}
\;\le\; \frac{m_{\rm band}}{2.6}
\qquad (m_{\rm band} = 0.00432;\ \text{floor at } I = 2{\times}10^{-3}),
\]
with the production anchor at $m_{\rm band}/59.4$ (AddE hardened floors;
band tops $\times290.9$ / $\times12.7$).
\item \textbf{Composition.} For every amended-class $\mathcal P$ at the
certified intensities,
\[
\Delta F[\mathcal P]
\;\ge\; m_{\rm band} - \frac{m_{\rm band}}{2.6}
\;=\; m_{\rm band}\Bigl(1 - \frac{1}{2.6}\Bigr)
\;=\; 0.00432 \times 0.6154
\;\simeq\; +2.66\times10^{-3} \;>\; 0 ,
\]
i.e.\ the composed worst-case margin is $\sim62\%$ of the layer floor at
the thinnest certified endpoint, and $\ge 0.00432\,(1-1/59.4) \simeq
+4.25\times10^{-3}$ at the production anchor.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent The composition is valid because the two links live at the
SAME bookkeeping order (matched second-cumulant; SC-SCOPE below), the

budget $m_{\rm STEP\text{-}5B}$ was DEFINED against the Prop-A band
margin $+0.00432$ throughout the B5 chain (artefact field
\texttt{anchor.margin} $= 0.00432$).

\section*{5. Numerical verification}

No NEW machine numbers are introduced by this note. Every constant used
is already certified in the existing artefact
\url{claims/B5-BEYOND-LAYER-BOUND/runs/260605-gershgorin-reduction/result.json}
(script \url{codes/vacuum/beyond_layer_gershgorin_bound.py} v1.14.0,
192/192 self-test asserts PASS) and in the migrated Prop-A record
(\url{archive/legacy/notes/Math437/...v1.2}, floors $P_B(M_+) =
0.0052460$, $P_B(M_c) = 0.0092199$, band sum $+0.00432$; Math442
certification). The single new arithmetic step is the composition
\[
0.00432\,(1 - 1/2.6) = 0.00432 \times 0.61538 = 2.658\times10^{-3},
\qquad
0.00432\,(1 - 1/59.4) = 4.247\times10^{-3},
\]
which is exact closed-form arithmetic on certified constants.

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): dimensional --- both sides of the
composition are free-energy densities in the same canonical units as the
$+0.00432$ band margin (pure subtraction, no unit mixing); magnitude ---
the composed endpoint margin $2.66\times10^{-3}$ is 62\% of the layer
floor, consistent with $1/2.6 = 0.385$ being subtracted; limit case ---
as the STEP-5B margin ratio $\to \infty$ the composed bound recovers the
full Prop-A floor $+0.00432$, and at ratio $= 1$ it degenerates to $0$
(the composition fails exactly when the gate margin is exhausted, as it
must); sign-direction --- $\delta F_{\rm off}$ enters with absolute
value, so the bound holds whether the beyond-layer correction helps or
hurts the competitor.

\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections; CLAUDE.md 6.3)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``The composition double-counts or mis-aligns
the two margins: Prop-A's $+0.00432$ is a layer-level floor at the
variational comparison, while the STEP-5B budget was tuned against the
same number --- is the subtraction legitimate?''}
DISMISSED with the chain's own construction: the B5 budget
$m_{\rm STEP\text{-}5B}$ was DEFINED as the Prop-A band margin from the
first note of the chain (artefact \texttt{anchor.margin} $= 0.00432$),
precisely so that ``$|\delta F_{\rm off}| < m_{\rm STEP\text{-}5B}$''
means ``the beyond-layer correction cannot exhaust the layer floor.''
The subtraction $m(1 - 1/\rho)$ with $\rho$ the certified margin ratio
is the intended composition, at matched bookkeeping order, same kernel,
same anchors. No constant is used twice in the same inequality.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``H-LAYER is listed as discharged ($\flat$), but
the amendment H-ADM-COH redefined the class --- so the discharge holds
only on the amended class, and quietly promoting that to `H-LAYER
discharged' is an overclaim.''}
VALID-with-mitigation: agreed, and the note's notation is built around
exactly this point --- H-LAYER$^{\flat}$ is defined as ``discharged ON
THE AMENDED CLASS,'' the amendment itself is a separate listed
hypothesis (H-ADM-COH), and the candidate theorem quantifies over the
amended class only. The unrestricted-class statement remains OPEN (DR-2,
research-grade) and is excluded from the theorem by construction. The
residual inventory between ``amended-class conditional'' and
``unconditional'' is the next unit's content.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The thin endpoint: at $I = 2\times10^{-3}$ the
composed margin rests on the $\times2.6$ FLOOR, which itself rests on
the criterion-band conservative end and the $n_{\rm pack} =
16/\theta_{\min}^2$ packing constant. A T6 entry on a $\times2.6$ link
is fragile.''}
VALID-with-mitigation: quoted honestly --- the endpoint is the weakest
certified link and is flagged as such here and in AddE; the band top at
that intensity is $\times12.7$ and the production anchor floor is
$\times59.4$. The T6-conditional tier does not require margin comfort,
only a valid conditional proof with explicit hypotheses; the thinness is
a robustness concern tracked by the registered interval-sweep polish,
not a gap in the conditional chain.
\item[($\delta$)] \emph{``B1's evidence grade is ESTIMATOR + EXECUTED;
promoting B1 to T6 on the strength of B2 + B5 changes what the claim IS
(from enumerated races to class-wide selection) --- a tier change
disguised as a statement change.''}
VALID-with-mitigation: correct that the T6 object is a STRONGER
statement than the original enumerated-race claim; this is why the note
proposes the promotion as an explicit operator decision with the new
statement text supplied (section 7), rather than editing the tier in
place. If the operator prefers, the alternative bookkeeping is a NEW
claim card (e.g.\ B6-RH-CLASSWIDE) holding the conditional theorem at
T6 while B1 stays T5 for the enumerated races; both options are workable
and the choice is the operator's. This note records the proposal in
status.json scope/notes ONLY.
\end{itemize}
