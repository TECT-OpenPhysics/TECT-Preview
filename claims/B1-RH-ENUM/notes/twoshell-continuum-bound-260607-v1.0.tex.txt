\section*{1. Purpose and scope: the operating-point discrepancy}

estimator-upgrade-knobs v1.0 certified the two-shell $\{110\}+\{200\}$ $(0,0)$
Hessian and cited Math432 for the GLOBAL no-condensate (the $m_{31}$ tilted-valley
question). Two problems surfaced on closer inspection. First, Math432 sets its
bare mass $R=0.005$ directly and reproduces $r_R=0.3045$, $M_R=0.1094$ -- this is
NOT the B1 operating point. The B1 single-shell readings use
$r_{\rm bare}=R_{\rm OF}(0.005)=\mu^2+q_0^4=0.219$, $r_R=0.419$, $M_R=0.096$. The
cited global evidence is at a different (lower-mass) point. Second, the soft
$(0,0)$ eigenvalue is NOT $\kappa_{\rm BCC}(\{110\})=5.116$ but
$\kappa(\{200\})=3.86$: the $\{200\}$ shell is the SOFTER direction. This note
redoes the two-shell global no-condensate at the B1 point and corrects both.

\section*{2. The diagonal-continuum evaluator and its validation}

The diagonal-continuum two-shell free energy $\Delta F_{\rm diag}(A_1,A_2,M)$
(Hartree diagonal ansatz, relative to the disordered state) is built from the
exact band-limited moments $\langle\phi_1^a\phi_2^b\rangle$ and the certified
$m424$ primitives $M_{\rm fast}$, $\mathtt{dI}$. The moments are exact:
$\langle\phi_1^2\rangle=12$, $\langle\phi_2^2\rangle=6$,
$\langle\phi_1^4\rangle=540$, $\langle\phi_1^3\phi_2\rangle=144$,
$\langle\phi_1^2\phi_2^2\rangle=96$, $\langle\phi_1\phi_2\rangle=
\langle\phi_1\phi_2^3\rangle=0$ (orthogonality). The evaluator reproduces
Math432's recorded diagonal values (anchored minus bracket) at $r=0.005$ to
$1.6\times10^{-7}$ over four anchors, so the re-implementation is faithful (the
transcription is gated by these asserts).

\section*{3. Two-shell global no-condensate at the B1 point ($r=0.219$)}

Minimising $\Delta F_{\rm diag}$ over the trial mass $M$ (21-point grid,
$M/M_R\in[0.5,1.6]$) on the Math432 scan domain ($A_1\in[0,0.20]$,
$A_2\in[-0.20,0.20]$, $51\times51$):
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{no diagonal condensate}: $\min_{(A_1,A_2)\neq(0,0)}\Delta F_{\rm
diag}=+3.9\times10^{-5}>0$.
\item \textbf{continuum (node-free)}: the 2D curvature-chord lower bound
$\Delta F\ge \Delta F_{ij}-\tfrac18(|D_{xx}|\delta_1^2+|D_{yy}|\delta_2^2+
2|D_{xy}|\delta_1\delta_2)$ away from the origin cell is
$+1.2\times10^{-4}>0$: no condensate hides between grid nodes.
\item \textbf{near origin}: the $(0,0)$ Hessian is positive-definite,
$\kappa_{\{110\}}=5.16$, $\kappa_{\{200\}}=3.86$ (both $>0$), $\kappa_{12}=0$ by
orthogonality.
\end{itemize}
Thus the two-shell diagonal global no-condensate holds at the B1 point, which
supersedes the Math432 $r=0.005$ citation.

\section*{4. Two corrections to estimator-upgrade-knobs v1.0}

\textbf{(1) Operating point.} The two-shell GLOBAL no-condensate is now at
$r=0.219$ (B1), not the cited Math432 $r=0.005$. The B1 point is STIFFER (larger
gap $r_R=0.419$ vs $0.305$; larger $\{110\}$ curvature), so the no-condensate
conclusion holds a fortiori, but it is now computed at the correct point.

\textbf{(2) Soft-direction identification.} estimator-upgrade-knobs v1.0 stated
the soft eigenvalue is $\kappa_{\rm BCC}(\{110\})=5.116$ and that $\{200\}$ is
stiffer (kernel penalty $Cq_0^4>0$). The direct two-shell computation gives
$\kappa_{\{200\}}=3.86<\kappa_{\{110\}}=5.16$: $\{200\}$ is SOFTER. The penalty
argument was naive -- $\{200\}$ has fewer modes ($n_2=3<n_1=6$) and the loop
dressing nets a lower curvature. The $(0,0)$ Hessian is still positive-definite;
only the soft-eigenvalue value ($3.86$, not $5.116$) and the soft-direction label
($\{200\}$, not $\{110\}$) change.
