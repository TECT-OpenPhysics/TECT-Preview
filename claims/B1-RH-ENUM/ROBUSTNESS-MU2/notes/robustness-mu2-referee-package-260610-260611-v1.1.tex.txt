\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This referee document concerns ROBUSTNESS-MU2: whether the STEP-5B layer-closure margin survives OFF the production anchor, across the mass band $\mu^2\in[\times0.5,\times2]$ and all intensities. \textbf{Scope:} the closure-margin ratio $K_{\rm budget}/K(n_{\rm pack})$ recomputed off-anchor. \textbf{Not claimed:} this does not by itself close STEP-5B (the admissible-class exhaustiveness gate is separate); it is robustness evidence.

\section*{2. The result}

The STEP-5B closure margin ratio $=K_{\rm budget}/K(n_{\rm pack})$, recomputed off-anchor, stays $>1$ across $\mu^2\in[\times0.5,\times2]$ and all three intensities; worst case $\times2.55$ at the endpoint, rising to $\sim\times59$ at production intensity. The margin is therefore not an anchor artefact.

\section*{3. Structure of the argument}

The Prop-A layer margin and the minimum-transfer envelope are recomputed from a single source (\texttt{sectorb\_common}) at each $\mu^2$; the budget $K_{\rm budget}$ and the consumption $K(n_{\rm pack})$ are formed and their ratio swept over the band and the intensity grid. Monotonic trends and worst-case values are recorded.

\section*{4. Numerical reproduction}

Three scripts (margin recompute, band sweep, off-anchor re-margin), each with self-test asserts, reproduce the ratio values and the worst-case $\times2.55$. Run them from the bundle. The expected minimum ratio is $\times2.55$ at the band endpoint, and every sampled $(\mu^2,\text{intensity})$ pair satisfies $K_{\rm budget}/K(n_{\rm pack})>1$; at production intensity the ratio is $\sim\times59$.
