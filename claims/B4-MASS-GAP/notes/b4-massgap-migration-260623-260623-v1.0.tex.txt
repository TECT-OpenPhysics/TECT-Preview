\section*{6. Devil's-advocate (three objections)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``A migration that waives re-execution certifies
nothing.''} VALID with mitigation: it certifies precisely two things ---
byte-identity of the textual evidence (\S 3a, SHA-256) and internal consistency
of the card's quoted anchor with the migrated manifest (\S 3b) --- and makes no
stronger claim. The card's reproduction tier is left at PACKAGE-PENDING so the
waiver is visible, not hidden. This is the honest disposition for a
production-PDE numerical anchor under \url{governance/migration-plan.md} \S 6
(``re-validation evidence: re-run artefact \emph{or reasoned waiver}'').
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``The anchor is a metastable, unfavourable
($\Delta F>0$) point --- it does not support a mass-gap claim.''} DISMISSED as
a scope objection: B4 claims uniqueness \emph{within the single-mode constraint
cone} at the operating point, not global ground-state favourability. The
$\Delta F$ sign and the metastable label are carried verbatim into \S 5 and the
card; no favourability is asserted. The favourable point in the same table is
\#3 ($\mu^2=-0.1$, $\Delta F=-6.27\times 10^{-10}$), also migrated.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``Archive copies could drift from the frozen legacy
originals.''} DISMISSED: the legacy repository is frozen read-only (M0); copies
are byte-identical at migration time (\S 3a hashes recorded), ledgered, and
git-tracked from this commit --- any later divergence is visible in history.
\end{itemize}

$r = \mu^2 + 0.2140336$ holds at point \#1 ($5.0\times 10^{-3}+0.2140336
