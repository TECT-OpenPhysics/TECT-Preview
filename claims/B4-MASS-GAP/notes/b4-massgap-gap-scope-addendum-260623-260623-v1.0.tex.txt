\section*{2. The cone spectral gap}

Let $H_{\Psi_*}$ be the Hessian of $F_{\rm TECT}$ at the single-mode-cone
critical point $\Psi_*$, let $\mathcal C$ be the single-mode constraint cone
tangent space, and let $\mathcal Z$ be the subspace of antisymmetric zero modes
(the broken-symmetry directions). The cone spectral gap is
\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\rm cone}:=\inf_{\substack{\delta\Psi\in\mathcal C,\ \delta\Psi\perp\mathcal Z\\
\|\delta\Psi\|_2=1}}\big\langle\delta\Psi,\,H_{\Psi_*}\,\delta\Psi\big\rangle .
\end{equation}
A genuine local mass gap on the cone requires $\Delta_{\rm cone}>0$; hence
\textbf{$\Delta_{\rm cone}\le0$ is a direct falsifier of the gap}, distinct from
the uniqueness falsifier (existence of a second minimiser).

\section*{3. The anchor is a curvature anchor, not the gap}

The migrated continuation reports $m^{*2}=+4.247\times10^{-2}$ at
$\mu^2=+5\times10^{-3}$ on the metastable subset-4-cosine branch. This is a
\emph{positive local curvature anchor} within the single-mode cone. The record
establishes \textbf{no} equality $m^{*2}=\Delta_{\rm cone}$ and \textbf{no}
rigorous bound $m^{*2}\le\Delta_{\rm cone}$ (or $\ge$): the antisymmetric
zero-mode-excluded lowest eigenvalue of $H_{\Psi_*}$ is not computed in the
migrated material. Consequently B4 is, in its current scope, a positive
local-curvature statement, \textbf{not} a continuum or thermodynamic mass-gap
proof. Establishing the latter requires (a) computing the symmetry-projected
$\Delta_{\rm cone}$ on the canonical production Hessian, (b) the identification
or rigorous inequality relating $m^{*2}$ and $\Delta_{\rm cone}$, (c) the
continuum ($N\to\infty$ / $a\to0$) limit, and (d) the production-Hessian
reproduction bundle (PACKAGE-PENDING).
