\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This note establishes the renormalisation foundation A3: the scalar Brazovskii
functional admits a continuum limit with a finite counterterm set, so the
``continuum-anchored'' language used throughout Sector B refers to a
well-defined object. \textbf{Scope (narrow, matching A2):} one real scalar field
in $d=3$ with the quartic kernel $K(q)=\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2$, $\mu^2>0$. The
result is PERTURBATIVE super-renormalisability and the continuum limit of the
perturbative correlation functions; the non-perturbative (constructive) limit of
the full measure is a separate, stronger target ($\mathrm{A3}_{\rm constructive}$),
as are the full Class-II action and the $\mu^2<0$ branch. Numerical constants are
pinned by \url{codes/foundations/a3_renormalisation_checks.py} (6/6 PASS).

\section*{2. Setup}

The free energy (A1-KERNEL-CONV) is
$F_{\rm TECT}[\phi]=\int[\tfrac12\phi K(-i\nabla)\phi+\tfrac\lambda4\phi^4
+\tfrac\gamma6\phi^6]$, with bare propagator
\begin{equation}
G(q)=\frac1{K(q)}=\frac1{\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2}\ \sim\ \frac1{Y\,q^4}\quad(q\to\infty),
\end{equation}
i.e.\ a $q^{-4}$ ultraviolet decay (assert \texttt{propagator\_uv\_decay\_q4}),
in contrast to the $q^{-2}$ of a second-order kernel. Vertices are the
$\phi^4$ ($\lambda$) and $\phi^6$ ($\gamma$) couplings. Regularise by a momentum
cutoff $\Lambda$ (equivalently a lattice spacing $a\sim\Lambda^{-1}$).

\section*{3. Power counting}

A connected diagram with $V$ vertices, $I$ internal lines and $L=I-V+1$ loops
has, in $d=3$ with a $q^{-4}$ propagator, superficial degree of divergence
\begin{equation}
D=dL-4I=3(I-V+1)-4I=3-3V-I .
\label{eq:D}
\end{equation}
For every connected diagram with at least one vertex and one internal line
($V\ge1$, $I\ge1$),
\begin{equation}
D=3-3V-I\ \le\ 3-3-1\ =\ -1\ <\ 0
\end{equation}
(assert \texttt{superficial\_degree\_all\_negative}: the $\phi^4$ tadpole,
$\phi^4/\phi^6$ self-energies and higher diagrams all have $D\in\{-1,-2,\ldots\}$).
The dimension $d=3$ is essential: \eqref{eq:D} reads $D=(d-4)I-dV+d$, which is
$<0$ for all $V\ge1,I\ge1$ only when $d\le3$; $d=4$ is merely marginal.

\section*{4. Ultraviolet finiteness (Weinberg)}

Every \emph{subdiagram} of any diagram is itself connected with $V'\ge1$,
$I'\ge1$, hence has $D'=3-3V'-I'<0$ by \eqref{eq:D}. Weinberg's convergence
theorem then gives \emph{absolute} convergence of every loop integral as
$\Lambda\to\infty$ (assert \texttt{weinberg\_all\_subdiagrams\_convergent}).
Therefore the theory is \textbf{ultraviolet-finite}: the perturbative expansion
requires NO divergent counterterms. The counterterm set is finite --- in fact
empty up to the single finite normal-ordering of the tadpole (\S5).

\section*{5. The tadpole and the continuum limit}

The one-loop equal-point contraction is
\begin{equation}
\langle\phi^2\rangle=\int\frac{d^3q}{(2\pi)^3}\,G(q)
=\frac1{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty\frac{q^2\,dq}{\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2},
\end{equation}
whose integrand decays as $q^2/(Yq^4)=1/(Yq^2)$, so the integral converges:
$\langle\phi^2\rangle=0.767$ at the production parameters, with
$|\!\int^{4\Lambda}-\!\int^{2\Lambda}|\to4.8\times10^{-5}$ (assert
\texttt{tadpole\_integral\_converges}). Hence the cutoff $\Lambda\to\infty$ limit
is finite. At fixed cutoff, the discretised (lattice) tadpole is an isotropic
Riemann sum that converges to the continuum integral as the spacing $a\to0$
(assert \texttt{discretisation\_to\_continuum\_convergence}: error
$3.7\times10^{-10}\to1.5\times10^{-12}$ on refinement, once $a$ resolves the
shell width $\sim\sqrt{\mu^2/Y}/q_0$). The two limits combine: the continuum
($a\to0$) limit of perturbative correlation functions exists and is
cutoff-independent.

\section*{6. Infrared finiteness}

Under $\mu^2>0$, $K(q)\ge\mu^2>0$ for all $q$, so $G(q)\le1/\mu^2$ is bounded and
no infrared divergence occurs (assert \texttt{no\_ir\_divergence\_mu2\_positive}).
The near-degeneracy of $K$ on the shell $|q|=q_0$ (where $K=\mu^2$) is a finite
enhancement, not a divergence.

\paragraph{Theorem (A3, scalar/$d{=}3$/$\mu^2>0$).} \emph{The scalar Brazovskii
functional with the quartic kernel $K(q)=\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2$ in $d=3$,
$\mu^2>0$, is super-renormalisable: every connected diagram with $V\ge1,I\ge1$
has $D=3-3V-I<0$, so by Weinberg's theorem the perturbative expansion is
ultraviolet-finite and infrared-finite, the counterterm set is finite (empty up
to a finite tadpole normal-ordering), and the continuum ($a\to0$,
$\Lambda\to\infty$) limit of the perturbative correlation functions exists and is
cutoff-independent.}

\section*{7. Devil's-advocate (three objections)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Power counting is only superficial; nested
subdivergences could spoil it.''} DISMISSED: every subdiagram has $V'\ge1,I'\ge1$
hence $D'<0$ by \eqref{eq:D}; Weinberg's theorem requires exactly this
(all subdiagrams convergent) and yields absolute convergence. There are no
divergent subgraphs to renormalise.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``This is only perturbative.''} VALID with mitigation: the
claim is explicitly the perturbative continuum limit (the standard meaning of
super-renormalisability). The constructive (non-perturbative measure) limit is
flagged as a separate target $\mathrm{A3}_{\rm constructive}$; it is not claimed.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The shell $|q|=q_0$ where $K=\mu^2$ is small could
cause trouble.''} DISMISSED: $\mu^2>0$ keeps $K\ge\mu^2>0$, so $G$ is bounded;
the shell is a finite enhancement of width $\sim\sqrt{\mu^2/Y}/q_0$, resolved by
any $a$ below that scale (\S5), with no divergence.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): all constants reproduced by
\url{codes/foundations/a3_renormalisation_checks.py} (6/6): $D=3-3V-I\in\{-1,-2,
-5,-6,-7,-10\}$ for the tabulated diagrams; $K(q)/(Yq^4)\to1$; finite tadpole
$\langle\phi^2\rangle=0.767$; $\min_q K=\mu^2=5\times10^{-3}>0$; refinement error
$\to10^{-12}$.
