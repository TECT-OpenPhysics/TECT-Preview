\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This note establishes the ULTRAVIOLET half of the A3 foundation: the scalar
Brazovskii functional in $d=3$ with the quartic kernel is super-renormalisable
(UV-finite). \textbf{Scope (narrow, matching A2):} one real scalar field, $d=3$,
$K(q)=\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2$, $\mu^2>0$; perturbative power counting only. The
\emph{stronger} statement that all perturbative correlation functions converge to
a cutoff-independent continuum limit is NOT claimed here --- it requires a
measure definition and a graphwise regulator-convergence lemma and is the
separate (open) card \texttt{A3-PERTURBATIVE-CONTINUUM-CORRELATORS}. Numerical
checks: \url{codes/foundations/a3_renormalisation_checks.py} (UV asserts).

\section*{2. Setup}

$F_{\rm TECT}[\phi]=\int[\tfrac12\phi K(-i\nabla)\phi+\tfrac\lambda4\phi^4
+\tfrac\gamma6\phi^6]$ with bare propagator
\begin{equation}
G(q)=\frac1{K(q)}=\frac1{\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2}\ \sim\ \frac1{Y\,q^4}\quad(q\to\infty)
\end{equation}
(a $q^{-4}$ UV decay; assert \texttt{propagator\_uv\_decay\_q4}), vertices
$\phi^4(\lambda),\phi^6(\gamma)$, momentum cutoff $\Lambda$.

\section*{3. Power counting}

A connected diagram with $V$ vertices, $I$ internal lines, $L=I-V+1$ loops has,
in $d=3$ with a $q^{-4}$ propagator,
\begin{equation}
D=dL-4I=3-3V-I\ \le\ -1\ <\ 0\qquad(V\ge1,\ I\ge1)
\label{eq:D}
\end{equation}
(assert \texttt{superficial\_degree\_all\_negative}). The dimension is essential:
$D=(d-4)I-dV+d<0$ for all $V\ge1,I\ge1$ only when $d\le3$ ($d=4$ marginal).

\section*{4. Ultraviolet finiteness (Weinberg)}

Every loop-containing connected \emph{subdiagram} is itself a connected graph
with $V'\ge1,I'\ge1$, hence $D'=3-3V'-I'<0$ by \eqref{eq:D}. Weinberg's theorem
then gives absolute convergence of every loop integral as $\Lambda\to\infty$
(assert \texttt{weinberg\_all\_subdiagrams\_convergent}). Precisely:
\begin{center}
\textbf{No UV-divergent counterterm is required.}\\
\textbf{A finite normal ordering of the tadpole is an optional scheme choice.}
\end{center}
That is, the theory is UV-finite; the counterterm \emph{set} contains no
divergent element, and any finite tadpole subtraction is a renormalisation-scheme
convention, not a necessity.

\section*{5. The tadpole: an illustration (not the full continuum theorem)}

The simplest correlator, the equal-point contraction, is the single convergent
integral
\begin{equation}
\langle\phi^2\rangle=\frac1{2\pi^2}\int_0^\infty\frac{q^2\,dq}{\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2}=0.767,
\end{equation}
finite because the integrand decays as $1/(Yq^2)$ (assert
\texttt{tadpole\_integral\_converges}); its isotropic Riemann sum converges as the
spacing $a\to0$ (assert \texttt{discretisation\_to\_continuum\_convergence},
error $\to10^{-12}$). This \emph{illustrates} UV-finiteness for one graph. It does
NOT establish lattice-to-continuum convergence of all Feynman graphs, which
requires the graphwise dominated-convergence lemma of
\texttt{A3-PERTURBATIVE-CONTINUUM-CORRELATORS}.

\section*{6. Infrared finiteness}

Under $\mu^2>0$, $K(q)\ge\mu^2>0$, so $G\le1/\mu^2$ is bounded: no IR divergence
(assert \texttt{no\_ir\_divergence\_mu2\_positive}). The shell $|q|=q_0$ is a
finite enhancement.

\paragraph{Theorem (A3-UV).} \emph{In $d=3$ with $K(q)=\mu^2+Y(q^2-q_0^2)^2$,
$\mu^2>0$, every connected diagram with $V\ge1,I\ge1$ has $D=3-3V-I<0$; by
Weinberg's theorem the perturbative expansion is ultraviolet- and
infrared-finite. No UV-divergent counterterm is required; a finite normal
ordering is an optional scheme choice.}

\section*{7. Devil's-advocate (three objections)}

\begin{itemize}
\item[($\alpha$)] \emph{``Superficial power counting misses nested
subdivergences.''} DISMISSED: every subdiagram has $V'\ge1,I'\ge1$ hence $D'<0$;
Weinberg requires exactly this and yields absolute convergence.
\item[($\beta$)] \emph{``UV-finiteness implies the continuum limit of all
correlators.''} VALID and the reason for the split: it does not, by itself ---
that needs a measure and a graphwise convergence lemma (separate open card). This
note claims only UV-finiteness.
\item[($\gamma$)] \emph{``The finite tadpole is a counterterm.''} DISMISSED: a
finite subtraction is a scheme choice, not a divergent counterterm; the precise
statement is in \S4.
\end{itemize}

Quantitative sanity check (mandatory): $D=3-3V-I\in\{-1,-2,-5,-6,-7,-10\}$ for the
tabulated diagrams; $K(q)/(Yq^4)\to1$; $\min_q K=\mu^2=5\times10^{-3}>0$;
illustrative tadpole $0.767$ with refinement error $\to10^{-12}$ (6/6 checks).
