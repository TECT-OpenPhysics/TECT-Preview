\section*{1. Purpose and scope}

This is the single-place consolidation of the A3 renormalisation foundation ---
two operator-approved T6 theorems --- and the entry note of the A3 reproduction
bundle. It establishes that the scalar Brazovskii functional in $d=3$ is
ultraviolet-finite and that its perturbative correlators have a continuum limit
(spectral regularisation). Full proofs:
\url{claims/A3-UV-SUPERRENORMALISABILITY/notes/a3-uv-superrenormalisability-260623-260623-v1.1.tex.txt}
and \url{claims/A3-PERTURBATIVE-CONTINUUM-CORRELATORS/notes/a3-graphwise-convergence-lemma-260623-260623-v1.4.tex.txt}.
Numerics: \url{codes/foundations/a3_renormalisation_checks.py} (6/6) and
\url{codes/foundations/a3_graphwise_convergence_checks.py} (8/8). Scope:
scalar, $d=3$, $\mu^2>0$, $\gamma>0$, perturbative order-by-order.

\section*{2. The two theorems}

\paragraph{Theorem A (UV super-renormalisability, A3-UV).} \emph{Assume $Y>0$,
$\mu^2>0$. With the $q^{-4}$ propagator $G=1/K$, every connected diagram in $d=3$ with $V\ge1,I\ge1$
has $D=3-3V-I<0$; by Weinberg's theorem the perturbative expansion is UV- and
IR-finite. No UV-divergent counterterm is required; a finite normal ordering is
an optional scheme choice.}

\paragraph{Theorem B (spectral continuum limit, A3-CORRELATORS).} \emph{Assume
$Y>0$, $\mu^2>0$, $\gamma>0$. With the spectral/Galerkin regulator $G_a(q)=\mathbf 1_{\{|q|\le\pi/a\}}/K(q)$ and
$\gamma>0$ (measure well-defined), every connected perturbative amplitude
satisfies, for each FIXED external momentum $p$,
$\lim_{a\to0}\mathcal A_{\mathcal G,a}(p)=\mathcal A_{\mathcal G}(p)$; hence each
order of the perturbative correlators has a cutoff-independent continuum limit
($\Lambda=\pi/a$, so $a\to0\equiv\Lambda\to\infty$).}

\section*{3. Proof structure}

Theorem A: power counting $D=(d-4)I-dV+d<0$ for $d\le3$; all subgraphs satisfy
$D'<0$, so Weinberg gives absolute convergence (UV-finite). Theorem B: the
spectral bound $G_a(q)\le1/K(q)\le C(1+|q|)^{-4}$ ($C=C(\mu^2,Y,q_0)<\infty$,
$a$-independent; production anchor $1.6\times10^3$) holds for ALL unwrapped internal-line momenta; with Theorem A's
Weinberg integrability the dominating function $\prod_iC(1+|q_i|)^{-4}\in L^1$,
so dominated convergence gives the fixed-$p$ limit. $\gamma>0$ makes
$F_{\Lambda,a}$ bounded below ($Z_{\Lambda,a}<\infty$), defining the measure.

\section*{4. Recorded flaw and open refinement}

The finite-difference lattice regulator $\hat q_a=\tfrac2a\sin\tfrac{aq}2$ was
\emph{refuted} for the domination: $\hat q_a$ is periodic, so an unwrapped
internal-line momentum exceeding the BZ folds ($\hat q_a\approx0$ at $q\sim2\pi/a$)
and $G_a^{\rm latt}$ is not bounded by $C(1+|q|)^{-4}$ (aliasing/Umklapp;
\texttt{lattice\_folding\_flaw\_demonstrated}). Theorem B therefore uses the
spectral regulator (Route A). The genuine finite-difference lattice continuum
limit (and regularisation-independence) needs a Reisz-type lattice
power-counting theorem --- \textbf{Route B, OPEN}. Uniformity on compact
$p$-sets is also not claimed.

\section*{5. Devil's-advocate (closed in review)}

($\alpha$) UV power counting is only superficial --- Weinberg (all $D'<0$) gives
absolute convergence. ($\beta$) UV-finiteness $\ne$ continuum limit --- hence the
split; Theorem B proves the latter separately. ($\gamma$) the lattice aliasing
flaw --- recorded (\S4) and avoided by the spectral regulator. ($\delta$)
uniform-on-compact-$p$ --- not claimed (per-$p$ DCT only). Quantitative sanity
check: $D<0$ table; spectral $C=C(\mu^2,Y,q_0)<\infty$ (anchor $1.6\times10^3$, $a$-independent); lattice folding
ratio $\sim10^{7}$--$10^{11}$ (the flaw); Weinberg integrability; $\Lambda=\pi/a$.
