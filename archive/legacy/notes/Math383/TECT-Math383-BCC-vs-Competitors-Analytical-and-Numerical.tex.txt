\subsection*{1. Canonical Brazovskii free energy in 1-mode reduction}

For an $N$-mode equal-amplitude crystalline ansatz
\begin{equation}
\Psi(\mathbf r) = A \sum_{j=1}^{N} \cos(\mathbf q_j \cdot \mathbf r),
\qquad |\mathbf q_j| = q_0 \quad (\text{shell condition}),
\end{equation}
the canonical Brazovskii free energy
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}[\Psi] = \int d^3 r \left[
    \tfrac{r}{2}\Psi^2 + \tfrac{Z}{2}|\nabla\Psi|^2 + \tfrac{Y}{2}|\Delta\Psi|^2
    + \tfrac{\lambda}{2}\Psi^4 + \tfrac{\gamma}{3}\Psi^6
\right]
\end{equation}
reduces (at the shell wavenumber $|\mathbf q| = q_0$, where the
kinetic part contributes only $\mu^2/2 \cdot \langle\Psi^2\rangle$)
to
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\mathcal{F}[\phi_0] = V \cdot \left[
  \tfrac{\mu^2}{2}\phi_0^2 + \tfrac{\lambda K_4(\Lambda)}{2}\phi_0^4
  + \tfrac{\gamma K_6(\Lambda)}{3}\phi_0^6
\right]\;}
\label{eq:F-1mode}
\end{equation}
where $\phi_0^2 := \langle\Psi^2\rangle = (N/2) A^2$ and the
\emph{structure factors} $K_4(\Lambda), K_6(\Lambda)$ depend on
the lattice $\Lambda$ being considered.

Stationary point: $d\mathcal{F}/d\phi_0^2 = 0$ gives
\begin{equation}
\phi_0^2(\Lambda) = \frac{-b(\Lambda) + \sqrt{b(\Lambda)^2 - 4 a(\Lambda) c}}{2 a(\Lambda)},
\quad a = \gamma K_6,\; b = \lambda K_4,\; c = \mu^2/2.
\end{equation}
Stationary value of $\mathcal{F}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}(\Lambda)/V = \tfrac{\mu^2}{2}\phi_0^2 + \tfrac{\lambda K_4}{2}\phi_0^4
+ \tfrac{\gamma K_6}{3}\phi_0^6.
\end{equation}

Math383 uses the standard $\langle\Psi^4\rangle = K_4 \langle\Psi^2\rangle^2$
convention which matches Leibler. The values are robust to
within $\sim 10\%$ across conventions, far smaller than the
$\sim 60\%$--$\sim 200\%$ BCC vs competitor gaps.
