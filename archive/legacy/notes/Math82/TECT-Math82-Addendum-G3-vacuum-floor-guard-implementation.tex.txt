\subsection*{1.\ Patch summary}

\verb|Codes/pde/tect_newton_krylov.py::newton_solve| now contains a

F_norm_total = real_norm(g_proj)
_c_floor = 100.0
_eps_mach64 = 1.1e-16
_vac_floor = _c_floor * math.sqrt(dof_eff) * _eps_mach64
if F_norm_total < _vac_floor:
    history.append(...)        # NewtonStepRecord with rho=1, accepted=True
    converged = True
    if verbose:
        print(f"  >>> Converged at step {step} "

              f"< vac_floor={_vac_floor:.3e})")
    break
\end{verbatim}

\subsection*{2.\ Threshold derivation}

The guard threshold
$$
\mathrm{vac\_floor}\;=\;c_{\mathrm{floor}}\,\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}}\,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}
\quad(\text{double precision: }\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}^{(64)}\!\approx\!1.1\!\times\!10^{-16})
$$
is sized to lie between two regimes:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{From below}: the floating-point roundoff floor of the
projected residual evaluation, which scales as
$\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}}\,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}\,\|\nabla F\|_{\mathrm{op}}$
under the central-limit aggregation of independent FFT roundoffs across
$N_{\mathrm{dof}}$ modes. Empirically (Phase Z Points 4--7),
$\|F\|/\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}} \sim 4$--$8\!\times\!10^{-8}$ at the
purported ``branch-terminated'' iterates.
\item \textbf{From above}: the smallest $\|F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi^{*})\|$
attainable at any genuine non-trivial Brazovskii equilibrium
(Theorem~\ref{thm:vacuum-floor-guard-correctness} below).
\end{itemize}
For $N_{\mathrm{dof}} = 196{,}605$ at $N=32$ and $c_{\mathrm{floor}}=100$:
$$
\mathrm{vac\_floor} \;=\; 100 \cdot 443.4 \cdot 1.1\!\times\!10^{-16}
                       \;\approx\; 4.9\!\times\!10^{-12}.
$$
This sits two orders of magnitude below the Phase Z 4--7 stalled-state
$\|g_{\mathrm{proj}}\|$ values ($\sim 10^{-5}$ in absolute units, or
$\sim 5\!\times\!10^{-8}$ per-DoF). The guard cleanly separates the
``numerical vacuum'' regime from any iterate that has $O(\sqrt{N})$
times larger gradient norm.

\subsection*{3.\ Separation theorem (correctness)}

\begin{theorem}[Vacuum-floor / non-trivial-equilibrium separation]
\label{thm:vacuum-floor-guard-correctness}
Let $\Psi^{*}\neq 0$ be a stationary point of the projected
Brazovskii residual on the $N=32$ BCC-commensurate cubic lattice with
locked parameters $(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma)$ at the operating point. Then
\begin{equation}
  \|F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi^{*})\|_{2}\;\ge\;
  \kappa_{\mathrm{Brazovskii}}\,\|\Psi^{*}\|_{2}^{p}\,\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}},
  \qquad p\in\{1,3,5\},
\label{eq:vacuum-floor-lower-bound}
\end{equation}
where $\kappa_{\mathrm{Brazovskii}}$ depends on the Hessian of
$\mathcal{F}[\Psi]$ along the locked-parameter slice and is bounded
below by $\min(|\mu^{2}|,|\lambda|,|\gamma|)\sim 10^{-2}$.
Consequently, for any $\Psi^{*}$ with condensate amplitude
$\|\Psi^{*}\|_{2}/\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}} > 10^{-3}$:
$$
\|F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi^{*})\|_{2}\;\gg\;
\mathrm{vac\_floor}\;=\;c_{\mathrm{floor}}\,\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}}\,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}.
$$
\end{theorem}

\paragraph*{Proof sketch.} For any $\Psi^{*}\neq 0$ that is a
stationary point, the projected residual satisfies
$F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi^{*}) = 0$ identically (this is the definition).
Therefore the inequality \eqref{eq:vacuum-floor-lower-bound} holds
trivially with $F = 0$ at exact stationarity. What the guard tests is
not whether $\Psi^{*}$ is exactly stationary but whether the residual
$F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi)$ at the current iterate $\Psi$ has fallen
below the FP floor. The relevant lower bound is the
\emph{near-stationary} analogue: by the Brazovskii lock equation
$\mu^{2}\Psi + \lambda(\Psi\cdot\bar{\Psi})\Psi + \gamma(\cdot)\Psi = 0$,
any $\Psi$ within $\delta$ of a true stationary point has
$\|F_{\mathrm{proj}}\| = O(\delta\,|\mathrm{Hess}|)$. For
$\|\mathrm{Hess}\|_{\mathrm{op}}\sim 10$--$100$ in the operating
regime, $\|F\| > \mathrm{vac\_floor}$ whenever $\delta >
\mathrm{vac\_floor}/100 \sim 10^{-14}$. \emph{The guard cannot
mistake a near-stationary non-trivial point for vacuum unless the
amplitude differs from the stationary value by less than $10^{-14}$, which
is below double-precision representable.} $\square$

\subsection*{4.\ Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3 protocol)}

Three potential failure modes were examined:

\paragraph*{(i) Accidental cancellation at non-trivial $\Psi$.}
\emph{Could the projected residual $F_{\mathrm{proj}}(\Psi^{*})$ be
small at a non-trivial $\Psi^{*}$ purely by symmetry?} For the
Brazovskii free energy on a BCC-commensurate torus, all $C_{4v}$ /
$O_h$ symmetry-protected zeros of $F_{\mathrm{proj}}$ are at
$\Psi=0$ or at full BCC condensates. Subset-4 / lamellar
configurations break $O_h$ to a subgroup that does not protect
$F_{\mathrm{proj}} = 0$ except at the lock point. \emph{No accidental
zero.}

\paragraph*{(ii) High-amplitude $\Psi$ but FP-roundoff-zero residual.}
\emph{Could a $\Psi^{*}$ with $\|\Psi^{*}\| \gg \mathrm{vac\_floor}$
produce $\|F_{\mathrm{proj}}\|$ below $\mathrm{vac\_floor}$?} Only at
exact stationarity (in which case the convergence is genuine and the
``vacuum'' label is a misnomer but harmless --- the iterate IS the
solution). The guard's print message uses the phrase
\verb|numerical_vacuum guard|; downstream callers should also check
$\|\Psi^{*}\|$ to disambiguate ``zero solution'' vs.\ ``high-amplitude
exact solution.'' Recommended Phase 2 Lanczos run after guard fires:
if $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ and $\|\Psi^{*}\|/\sqrt{N} > 10^{-3}$, the
guard correctly declared a high-amplitude exact equilibrium; relabel
in MANIFEST as \verb|kind="exact_equilibrium_at_floor"|.

\paragraph*{(iii) FP floor moves with $N$.}
The threshold scales as $\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{dof}}}\,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}$
which correctly tracks the central-limit FP aggregation. For
$N=64$ ($N_{\mathrm{dof}}\approx 1.57\!\times\!10^{6}$):
$\mathrm{vac\_floor}\approx 1.4\!\times\!10^{-11}$. Stays below any
realistic non-trivial $\|F\|$ for the same Brazovskii operator class.
$c_{\mathrm{floor}}=100$ remains conservative.
