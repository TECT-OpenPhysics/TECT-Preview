\subsection{Why G3' is structurally dangerous: the SHG resonance}

The two shells are coupled by an exact 4-wave resonance: with
$\mathbf{k}_1 = (1,1,0)$, $\mathbf{k}_2 = (1,0,1)$, $\mathbf{k}_3 = (0,-1,-1)$
(all in the first shell, units $s = q_0/\sqrt{2}$) and
$\mathbf{k}_4 = (2,0,0)$ in the second shell,
$\mathbf{k}_1 + \mathbf{k}_2 + \mathbf{k}_3 - \mathbf{k}_4 = 0$. Consequently
the cross moment
\begin{equation}
m_{31} \;=\; \big\langle \varphi_1^3\,\varphi_2 \big\rangle \;=\; 144
\qquad (\text{exact; } 4 \text{ unordered shell-1 triples} \times 3! \times 6
\text{ shell-2 partners})
\end{equation}
is nonzero, so the free energy contains the second-harmonic-generation (SHG)
tilt $u\,m_{31} A_1^3 A_2$ at classical level: \emph{the single-shell BCC
point is not stationary in the two-shell space}. The optimal $A_2^*$ is
strictly nonzero for every $A_1 > 0$, and the enriched ensemble is
guaranteed to undercut the single-shell BCC channel at equal $A_1$. The G1''
chain never probed this direction; G3' asks whether the enrichment can
reach below the disordered reference itself. The remaining structural
constants are $m_{22} = \langle \varphi_1^2 \varphi_2^2\rangle = 96$,
$\langle \varphi_2^4 \rangle = 90$, and the kernel value on the second
shell,
\begin{equation}
K_2 \;=\; r_{\rm braz} + C\,(2q_0^2 - q_0^2)^2 \;=\; \mu_2 + C\,q_0^4
\;=\; 0.219034,
\end{equation}
numerically identical to $K(0)$ -- the \{200\} shell sits as far off the
$q_0$ sphere (in kernel units) as the homogeneous mode, which is the
Math426 convention cross-check.

\subsection{Two-shell small-amplitude bound}

The Math428 v1.1 small-$A$ derivation is shell-additive at quadratic order
(shells are orthogonal; the SHG cross term is cubic in amplitudes):
\begin{equation}
\Delta F_{\rm est} = c_2^{(1)} A_1^2 + c_2^{(2)} A_2^2
+ O(A^3_{\rm mixed}, A^4), \qquad
c_2^{(s)} = n_s\big[r_R + C(k_s^2 - q_0^2)^2\big],
\end{equation}
giving $c_2^{(1)} = 6\,r_R = 1.8272$ (Math428 v1.1, unchanged) and
$c_2^{(2)} = 3\,(r_R + C q_0^4) = 1.5557$, verified numerically to 2.7\% at
$A_2 = 0.01$ (claim \texttt{smallA2\_bound\_10pct}). Every off-sphere shell
$s \ge 2$ carries the strictly positive stiffness excess
$n_s\,C(k_s^2-q_0^2)^2$: at quadratic order \emph{no} harmonic enrichment
direction is soft. The danger is confined to the cubic SHG tilt, which the
scan bounds directly: the induced optimum $A_2^* = O(|u| m_{31} A_1^3 /
2c_2^{(2)}) \lesssim 0.025$ on the scanned $A_1$ range, well inside the
$A_2$ grid, and the free-energy gain it produces never approaches the
disordered baseline.
